## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is mental health?**

Mental health is about how you think, feel, and cope with life.

Everyone has mental health — just like everyone has physical health.

Sometimes mental health needs attention and care, the same way a broken bone or fever does.

Getting help is not a sign that something is deeply wrong with you.

It is a sign that you are paying attention to yourself.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Mental health conditions are common in Malaysia. The 2023 National Health and Morbidity Survey estimated that 1 in 5 Malaysians over 16 experience some form of mental health issue — yet treatment rates remain low.

The gap between those who need support and those who seek it is largely shaped by stigma, lack of awareness, and barriers to access — not by a shortage of genuine care.

This page maps what is available, how to access it, and what to expect.

---

## Part 1: Understanding Common Mental Health Conditions

You do not need a diagnosis to seek help. But understanding common conditions helps you describe what you are experiencing and find the right support.

| Condition             | What It Can Feel Like                                              |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Depression            | Persistent low mood, loss of interest, fatigue, hopelessness, changes in sleep or appetite |
| Anxiety disorders     | Excessive worry, physical tension, racing heart, avoidance of situations |
| Panic disorder        | Sudden intense episodes of fear, shortness of breath, chest tightness |
| Post-traumatic stress (PTSD) | Flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance following a traumatic event |
| Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) | Intrusive thoughts, repetitive behaviours used to reduce distress |
| Bipolar disorder      | Alternating periods of very high energy/mood and very low mood    |
| Psychosis             | Difficulty distinguishing reality, hearing or seeing things others do not |
| Burnout               | Exhaustion, detachment, reduced effectiveness — often work-related |

These conditions exist on a spectrum. Mild symptoms do not always require formal treatment — but they are worth paying attention to and worth discussing with someone qualified.

### On Stigma

In Malaysia, mental health struggles are sometimes framed as personal weakness, spiritual failing, or something to manage privately. These framings cause harm — they delay help-seeking and increase suffering.

Mental health conditions have biological, psychological, and social causes. They are not character flaws. And they are treatable.

If someone in your life dismisses what you are experiencing: their opinion about your health does not make you less deserving of care.

## Part 3: Government Mental Health Services

### At Klinik Kesihatan

Mental health support is available at the primary care level:

* General practitioners (medical officers) can screen for depression and anxiety
* Referrals to psychiatric outpatient services at government hospitals
* Counselling services at selected Klinik Kesihatan (via community mental health nurses or counsellors)
* Medication management for stable conditions

**Starting here is often the most accessible route** — cost is RM 1 per visit, and you do not need a prior diagnosis.

Tell the medical officer what you have been experiencing. You do not need to use clinical terms. Describing how you feel in your own words is enough.

### At Government Hospitals

Government hospitals offer:

| Service                           | What It Involves                                              |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Psychiatric outpatient clinic     | Assessment, diagnosis, medication management, therapy         |
| Inpatient psychiatric ward        | For acute episodes requiring intensive care and stabilisation |
| Community psychiatric nurse       | Follow-up visits for patients managed in the community        |
| Medical social work               | Support for social factors affecting mental health            |

Access is via referral from Klinik Kesihatan for outpatient psychiatric clinics. Emergency psychiatric cases can present directly to hospital A&E.

### Dedicated Psychiatric Hospitals

For severe or acute conditions requiring specialist inpatient care:

| Hospital                        | Location         |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------- |
| Hospital Bahagia Ulu Kinta      | Perak            |
| Hospital Permai                 | Johor Bahru      |
| Hospital Mesra Bukit Padang     | Kota Kinabalu    |
| Hospital Sentosa                | Kuala Lumpur     |

Admission is via referral from a government hospital psychiatric team — not directly by self-presentation.

## Part 4: Private Therapy and Counselling

Private mental health care offers shorter wait times and a wider choice of practitioners — at higher cost.

### Types of Practitioners

| Practitioner           | What They Do                                              | Can Prescribe? |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| Psychiatrist           | Medical doctor specialising in mental health; diagnoses and prescribes medication | Yes |
| Clinical psychologist  | Assesses and provides evidence-based therapy (CBT, DBT, etc.) | No  |
| Counsellor             | Provides talk-based support; not diagnostic               | No             |
| Psychotherapist        | Provides structured therapeutic approaches                | No             |

For medication management, a **psychiatrist** is required. For therapy without medication, a **clinical psychologist or counsellor** may be the right fit.

### Cost Range (Private)

| Service                      | Typical Cost                         |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Psychiatrist consultation    | RM 150–RM 400 per session            |
| Clinical psychologist session | RM 150–RM 350 per session           |
| Counselling session          | RM 80–RM 200 per session             |
| Online therapy (platforms)   | RM 100–RM 250 per session            |

### Finding a Registered Practitioner

| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------ |

**Check that your counsellor is registered with Lembaga Kaunselor Malaysia (LKM).** In Malaysia, the title "counsellor" is legally regulated — only LKM-registered practitioners may provide counselling services professionally.

## Part 5: Workplace Mental Health and EAP

### Employee Assistance Programmes (EAP)

Many employers — particularly larger organisations and MNCs — offer EAP as part of their employee benefits.

EAP typically provides:

* Free and confidential counselling sessions (usually 3–8 sessions per year)
* Referral to specialist support if needed
* Financial counselling and legal advisory in some programmes
* Crisis support access

**Ask your HR whether your company offers EAP.** It is a benefit many employees are unaware of, and sessions are confidential — your employer does not receive information about what you discuss.

### SOCSO's Return-to-Work Programme

If a mental health condition has affected your ability to work, SOCSO's **Return-to-Work (RTW) Programme** provides:

* Vocational rehabilitation support
* Retraining assistance
* Coordination between medical teams and employers

This is available to SOCSO contributors. Cross-reference: **Section 2.5 — PERKESO/SOCSO**

### Your Rights at Work

Employees experiencing mental health conditions have employment protections:

* Mental illness is a recognised medical condition — employers cannot dismiss an employee solely on this basis
* Medical leave applies for mental health-related illness
* Harassment or discrimination related to mental health may be actionable under employment law

Cross-reference: **Section 2.8 — Know Your Labour Rights**

## Practical Steps If You Are Struggling

It can be hard to know where to start. A simple sequence:

```
1 Name it — "I have not been okay" is enough to begin
2 Talk to someone you trust, or call a crisis line
3 Visit Klinik Kesihatan — describe what you have been experiencing
4 Follow through on referrals or appointments
5 Give treatment time — mental health recovery is not always linear
```

Seeking help once and stopping is far better than never starting. Most people who seek support feel differently — not immediately, but meaningfully — with the right care.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Depression mistaken for laziness**

A young man spent months unable to get out of bed, losing interest in work and friends. Family assumed he was being lazy. He finally visited Klinik Kesihatan, described his symptoms, and was referred to a psychiatric outpatient clinic. A combination of medication and counselling helped him return to work within four months.

**Scenario 2: EAP discovered late**

A manager at a large company had been paying RM 180 per session for private counselling for six months before a colleague mentioned their company's EAP. She switched to EAP-covered sessions at no cost. The sessions were equally qualified — the only difference was she had not known to ask.

**Scenario 3: Crisis line at 2am**

A university student was overwhelmed and alone at 2am with nowhere to turn. She called Befrienders KL. The conversation did not solve her problems — but it helped her make it to morning, and she sought professional help the following week.

## FAQ

**Q: Is everything I tell a counsellor or psychiatrist kept confidential?**
Yes, with narrow exceptions: if there is serious risk of harm to yourself or others, practitioners have a duty to act. In all other cases, what you share is confidential.

**Q: Can I get mental health medication from Klinik Kesihatan?**
Yes — medical officers can prescribe basic psychiatric medication and manage stable conditions. More complex cases will be referred to a psychiatrist.

**Q: Is therapy a sign that I cannot handle things myself?**
No. Therapy is a structured, evidence-based process for working through difficulties — the same way a physiotherapist helps with physical recovery. Using available tools is competence, not weakness.

**Q: What if I cannot afford private therapy?**
Start with Klinik Kesihatan (RM 1 per visit). Government psychiatric outpatient services are heavily subsidised. If employed, check for EAP. Relate Malaysia and some NGOs offer sliding-scale fees.

**Q: Can mental health conditions affect my employment?**
Mental illness is a recognised medical condition in Malaysian employment law. Sick leave applies. Dismissal solely on the basis of mental illness is not lawful. See Section 2.8 for your full employment rights.
