## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is Klinik Kesihatan?**

A Klinik Kesihatan is a government health clinic run by KKM.

It is your first stop for most health issues.

What it does:

* Treats common illnesses
* Manages chronic conditions like diabetes and high blood pressure
* Gives vaccinations
* Provides maternal and child health care
* Issues referrals to specialist hospitals

Cost: **RM 1 per visit** for Malaysian citizens.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia's public health system is structured in tiers. Each tier serves a specific level of care and feeds into the next through referrals.

```
Klinik Desa / Klinik Kesihatan (Primary Care)
        ↓ referral
District / General Hospital (Secondary Care)
        ↓ referral
Specialist / Teaching Hospital (Tertiary Care)
```

Understanding this structure saves time, reduces unnecessary costs, and ensures you access the right level of care.

## Part 1: Types of Government Facilities

### Klinik Desa (Rural Health Clinic)

| Item       | Detail                                                      |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Location   | Rural and remote communities                                |
| Staffing   | Community nurses, assistant medical officers                |
| Services   | Basic primary care, maternal health, vaccinations           |
| Limitations | No doctor on-site in some locations; refer to Klinik Kesihatan for complex cases |

### Klinik Kesihatan (Health Clinic)

The backbone of Malaysian primary healthcare.

| Item       | Detail                                                        |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Location   | Urban and suburban areas, one in most mukim (sub-districts)  |
| Staffing   | Medical officers (doctors), nurses, pharmacists              |
| Services   | Full primary care (see Part 2)                                |
| Fee        | RM 1 per visit (Malaysian citizens)                          |
| Hours      | Typically 8am–5pm weekdays; some operate on Saturday mornings |

### Hospital Daerah / Hospital Umum (District / General Hospital)

| Item       | Detail                                                        |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Role       | Secondary care — handles cases referred from Klinik Kesihatan |
| Services   | Inpatient wards, surgery, specialist outpatient clinics, A&E  |
| Access     | Walk-in for emergencies; referral for specialist outpatient   |
| Examples   | Hospital Ampang, Hospital Batu Pahat, Hospital Sultanah Bahiyah (Alor Setar) |

### Hospital Pakar / Hospital Pengajian Tinggi (Specialist / Teaching Hospital)

| Item       | Detail                                                          |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Role       | Tertiary care — complex cases, sub-specialties, research        |

## Part 2: Services at Klinik Kesihatan

Klinik Kesihatan provides a wider range of services than most Malaysians realise:

| Service Category              | What Is Available                                           |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| General outpatient            | Acute illness, minor injury, medication                     |
| Chronic disease management    | Diabetes, hypertension, asthma, COPD follow-up              |
| Maternal health               | Antenatal care, postnatal follow-up, family planning        |
| Child health                  | Growth monitoring, developmental screening, immunisation    |
| Immunisation                  | National Immunisation Programme vaccines (free)             |
| Oral health                   | Dental services at selected Klinik Kesihatan (Klinik Pergigian) |
| Nutritional counselling       | Dietitian services at larger Klinik Kesihatan               |

## Part 3: How to Use Klinik Kesihatan

### First Visit — Registration

1. Bring your **MyKad** (mandatory)
2. Proceed to the registration counter
3. State your reason for visit
4. Pay **RM 1** (or present your exemption status)
5. Wait for your number to be called

**MySejahtera queue:** Some Klinik Kesihatan now allow queue registration via the MySejahtera app before arriving. Check your nearest clinic's availability.

### Chronic Disease Follow-up

If you have a chronic condition managed at Klinik Kesihatan:

* You will be given a follow-up appointment date
* Bring your **appointment card and medication booklet** on every return visit
* Blood tests and medication are provided at subsidised cost
* You may be eligible for home delivery of medication — ask your medical officer

### Getting a Referral

A referral letter from Klinik Kesihatan:

* Grants access to subsidised specialist outpatient clinics at government hospitals
* Is required for most non-emergency specialist consultations
* Specifies the specialty required and urgency level
* Should be brought to the specialist clinic on the appointment date

**Important:** Referral letters have an expiry — typically 3 months. Request a new one if yours has lapsed.

### Sick Leave Certificates (MC)

Medical certificates issued by Klinik Kesihatan are legally recognised for employment purposes.

For sick leave entitlements: **Section 2.8 — Know Your Labour Rights**

## Part 4: Navigating Government Hospitals as an Inpatient

### Admission Process

| Pathway              | How It Works                                               |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Emergency (A&E)      | Walk in, triaged by severity — critical cases seen first   |
| Elective admission   | Scheduled via specialist outpatient clinic after referral  |
| Transfer from private | Request transfer; hospital coordinates bed availability   |

### Ward Classes and Fees

| Ward Class   | Daily Rate (approx.)  | Conditions                                  |
| ------------ | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Third Class  | RM 3–RM 5             | Open bay, multiple beds, most common        |
| Second Class | RM 20–RM 50           | Smaller shared room (2–4 beds)              |
| First Class  | RM 100–RM 200         | Single or twin room, more privacy           |

Malaysian citizens are automatically admitted to Third Class unless they request an upgrade.

Third Class is clinically equivalent — the same doctors treat all ward classes.

### Rights as an Inpatient

* Right to know your diagnosis, treatment plan, and prognosis
* Right to consent to or refuse treatment
* Right to request a second opinion
* Right to be treated with dignity regardless of ward class
* Right to discharge yourself (AOR — at own risk) though this is discouraged without clinical clearance

### Hospital Billing

Government hospital bills are issued after discharge.

If unable to pay:

* Apply for a **bill waiver** via the hospital's Medical Social Work Unit (Unit Kerja Sosial Perubatan)
* Social workers can assess financial hardship and arrange full or partial waivers
* This service is available at all government hospitals and is underutilised

## Part 5: Fees and Exemptions

### Standard Fees

| Visit Type           | Malaysian Citizen    | Non-citizen (approx.)  |
| -------------------- | -------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Klinik Kesihatan     | RM 1                 | RM 15–RM 30            |
| Specialist outpatient | RM 5 (with referral) | Higher rate            |
| A&E (non-admission)  | RM 5                 | Higher rate            |
| Inpatient (Third)    | RM 3–RM 5 / day      | Higher rate            |

### Fee Exemption Categories

The following groups are fully exempt from government healthcare fees:

| Group                                        | Exemption Basis                          |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Children under 1 year old                    | Automatic                                |
| Government pensioners and spouses            | Pensioner card                           |
| Recipients of welfare assistance (JKM)       | Welfare letter                           |
| Persons with disabilities (OKU) — registered | OKU card                                 |
| Police and armed forces (ATM, PDRM)          | Service card                             |
| SOCSO employment injury cases                | SOCSO panel authorisation                |
| War veterans                                 | Veterans card                            |

**Bring supporting documentation** for any exemption claim at the registration counter.

## Practical Habits for Using Government Healthcare Well

Patients who navigate the public system effectively tend to:

* Register at a Klinik Kesihatan near home or work before they get sick — not during
* Keep a small personal health file: MyKad copy, medication list, appointment cards, referral letters
* Arrive early — counters open at 8am and queues fill fast by 9am
* Use MySejahtera queue feature where available to reduce waiting time
* Ask the medical officer directly about referral eligibility rather than waiting passively
* Know the Medical Social Work Unit exists for billing hardship — many patients do not

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Referral system bypassed**

A patient with a suspected heart condition took a bus directly to Hospital Kuala Lumpur's cardiology clinic. She was redirected to register at Klinik Kesihatan first for a referral. An additional half-day was lost. A brief call to KKM's helpline beforehand would have clarified the process.

**Scenario 2: Bill waiver not requested**

A family incurred RM 4,200 in government hospital bills after a road accident. Unaware of the Medical Social Work Unit, they borrowed money to pay. A social worker assessment could have arranged a partial or full waiver based on household income.

**Scenario 3: Chronic disease managed well at Klinik Kesihatan**

A diabetic patient switched from a private clinic (RM 80/visit) to his nearest Klinik Kesihatan. He now pays RM 1 per visit, receives the same blood tests and medications, and has a consistent medical officer managing his condition long-term.

### Path B: I need to see a specialist at a government hospital

**Step 1:** Visit your nearest Klinik Kesihatan.

**Step 2:** Tell the medical officer your symptoms and ask for a referral if appropriate.

**Step 3:** Receive the referral letter specifying the hospital and specialty.

**Step 4:** Call or visit the hospital to make a specialist outpatient appointment.

**Step 5:** Attend with your referral letter, MyKad, and any relevant medical records.

---

### Path C: I cannot afford my government hospital bill

Go to the **Unit Kerja Sosial Perubatan (Medical Social Work Unit)** at the hospital.

Explain your financial situation.

They will assess your household income and arrange a waiver or payment plan.

This is a formal service — use it without hesitation.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I walk into any government hospital without a referral?**
For emergencies (A&E), yes — no referral needed. For specialist outpatient clinics, a referral from Klinik Kesihatan is required to access the subsidised RM 5 rate.

**Q: What if my nearest Klinik Kesihatan is closed?**
Emergency cases go to the nearest hospital A&E. For non-urgent matters, some Klinik Kesihatan operate Saturday morning hours. Check MySejahtera or klik.moh.gov.my for your clinic's specific hours.

**Q: Can I request a specific doctor at Klinik Kesihatan?**
Generally no — you are seen by whichever medical officer is on duty. For chronic disease patients, some clinics arrange continuity of care. Ask your clinic directly.

**Q: Is Third Class ward care inferior to First Class?**
No. Clinical care — doctors, treatment protocols, medication — is identical across ward classes. Differences are in room privacy, facilities, and comfort, not medical quality.

**Q: Can foreigners access the RM 1 fee?**
No. The RM 1 subsidised rate applies to Malaysian citizens only. Permanent residents and foreigners pay a higher rate set by KKM.
