### 🧒 Simple

**What is MySejahtera?**

MySejahtera is a health app made by the Malaysian government.

You can use it to:

* See your vaccination records
* Register for a queue at Klinik Kesihatan
* Access free clinic visits if you qualify (Skim Peduli Sihat)
* Book health appointments

It is free to download and use.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia's shift toward digital health services accelerated significantly during the COVID-19 pandemic. MySejahtera — originally a contact tracing and vaccine management tool — has since been expanded into a broader digital health platform under KKM.

Alongside MySejahtera, KKM maintains several other digital health services, including the MyHEALTH Portal and government-supported telehealth infrastructure.

This page covers the main tools, what each does, and how to use them.

---

## A Note on MySejahtera's Origins

MySejahtera was launched in 2020 as a mandatory contact tracing application during the COVID-19 pandemic. During that period, concerns were raised in public discourse about data privacy, the scope of data collected, and how it was managed. A 2022 parliamentary inquiry raised questions about data governance.

Since the end of the pandemic emergency, MySejahtera has transitioned to a general health services platform. Check-in functionality has been removed. The app's current use is voluntary for most features.

For users with data privacy concerns: KKM's privacy policy for MySejahtera is available at **mysejahtera.malaysia.gov.my**. The Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) applies to government-held health data.

Cross-reference: **Section 8.4.2 — Data Privacy and PDPA**

## Part 2: Queue Registration and Appointment Booking

### Queue Registration at Klinik Kesihatan

One of MySejahtera's most practical features for daily users:

1. Open MySejahtera → select **Queue Registration**
2. Choose your nearest participating Klinik Kesihatan
3. Select service type (general, maternal, chronic disease, etc.)
4. Receive a queue number and estimated wait time
5. Arrive at the clinic when your number is approaching

**Not all Klinik Kesihatan have enabled this feature.** Walk-in registration at the counter remains available at all clinics regardless.

### Appointment Booking

Selected KKM facilities offer appointment booking via MySejahtera for:

* Antenatal follow-up visits
* Vaccination appointments
* Chronic disease follow-up (at participating clinics)
* Specialist outpatient (at selected government hospitals)

For facilities not on MySejahtera, call the clinic or hospital directly.

## Part 3: Vaccination Records and Health History

### Accessing Your Vaccination Record

MySejahtera maintains a digital log of vaccinations administered through KKM facilities:

1. Open MySejahtera → **Health Records** or **Vaccination Certificate**
2. Your NIP and COVID-19 vaccination history will be displayed
3. Records can be downloaded as PDF or shared digitally

**Important limitations:**

* Only vaccinations at KKM-connected facilities are logged
* Private clinic vaccinations may not appear unless the clinic submits to KKM's system
* Vaccinations received abroad will not appear
* If records are missing, contact your Klinik Kesihatan to update them manually

## Part 4: Skim Peduli Sihat (SPS) Access

SPS allows eligible B40 Malaysians to receive free primary care at private panel clinics via MySejahtera.

### How to Use SPS via MySejahtera

1. Open MySejahtera → **Skim Peduli Sihat**
2. The app displays your SPS eligibility status
3. If eligible, locate the nearest approved SPS panel clinic via the in-app map
4. At the clinic, present your **MyKad** — the clinic verifies eligibility in real time
5. No payment required for covered services

### What SPS Covers

* General outpatient consultation
* Basic investigations (urine test, blood glucose)
* Medication dispensed at the panel clinic
* Referral letter to government hospital if needed

SPS does not cover specialist consultations, dental, or procedures beyond primary care scope.

Eligibility is based on Bantuan Sara Hidup (BSH/STR) or e-Kasih registration. If you believe you qualify but SPS is not showing, contact your nearest Klinik Kesihatan or Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat (JKM).

## Part 5: MyHEALTH Portal

The MyHEALTH Portal is KKM's official public health information website — separate from the MySejahtera app.

### What It Is

* A web-based health education resource, not a service platform
* Contains verified health information across hundreds of conditions
* Available in Bahasa Malaysia and English
* No login required — fully public

### What It Contains

| Section                   | Content                                                       |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Diseases A–Z              | Symptoms, causes, treatment, prevention                       |
| Medications               | Drug information and safety guides                            |
| Nutrition and diet        | Dietary guidelines, healthy eating resources                  |
| Mental health             | Conditions, coping strategies, crisis resources               |
| Women's health            | Maternal health, reproductive health, screening guides        |
| Child health              | Growth milestones, immunisation, parenting guidance           |
| Elderly health            | Ageing well, chronic disease management, falls prevention     |
| First aid                 | Step-by-step first aid guides                                 |
| Health calculators        | BMI, calorie needs, risk assessment tools                     |

### MySejahtera vs MyHEALTH Portal

| Feature              | MySejahtera (App)                      | MyHEALTH Portal (Web)                  |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Type                 | Service platform                       | Health information resource             |
| Login required       | Yes (most features)                    | No                                      |
| Main use             | Queues, records, SPS, appointments     | Health education, condition information |
| Vaccination records  | Yes                                    | No                                      |

Use MySejahtera when you need to **do** something. Use MyHEALTH Portal when you need to **know** something.

## Part 6: Telehealth Services

### Government Telehealth

KKM has been expanding telehealth for:

* Chronic disease follow-up (diabetes, hypertension) at selected Klinik Kesihatan
* Medication refills for stable chronic disease patients
* Mental health triage and counselling referrals

Government telehealth remains in selective rollout as of 2025. Check MySejahtera or call your Klinik Kesihatan to confirm availability.

### Private Telehealth Platforms

| Platform       | Type                          | Notes                                       |
| -------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| DoctorOnCall   | GP and specialist teleconsult | Largest Malaysian telehealth platform       |
| BookDoc        | Appointment + teleconsult     | Links to public and private doctors         |
| Speedoc        | Home visit + teleconsult      | Also offers home medical services           |
| MyDoc          | Corporate health + teleconsult | Common as employer health benefit          |
| Naluri         | Mental health + coaching      | Chronic disease and mental wellness focus   |

Private telehealth typically costs RM 30–RM 80 per session. Some employer benefits or insurance policies cover telehealth — check your HR or insurer.

All licensed telehealth platforms are regulated under KKM's telemedicine guidelines. Verify any platform is licensed before sharing personal health information.

## Practical Habits for Using Digital Health Services

Malaysians who use health digital tools well tend to:

* Download MySejahtera and verify their vaccination records are complete and accurate
* Check SPS eligibility in the app if they are in the B40 group — many eligible users are unaware
* Use MyHEALTH Portal as a first reference for health questions rather than unverified social media
* Register for Klinik Kesihatan queue via MySejahtera to avoid long morning walk-in waits
* Keep their MySejahtera phone number updated — OTP verification is tied to the registered number

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: SPS eligibility unknown**

A B40 household had been paying RM 35–RM 50 per private GP visit. A family member checked MySejahtera and discovered SPS eligibility — switching to a panel clinic at no cost for all routine consultations.

**Scenario 2: Vaccination record needed for travel**

A traveller needed to prove COVID-19 vaccination status for a visa application. Her paper card was lost. MySejahtera provided a downloadable PDF certificate within minutes — accepted by the embassy.

**Scenario 3: Symptom research before clinic visit**

A parent noticed an unusual rash on his child. Before visiting the clinic he checked MyHEALTH Portal's skin conditions section, which described the likely condition and what to tell the doctor. The consultation was more productive.

## FAQ

**Q: Is MySejahtera mandatory to use?**
No — since the end of the COVID-19 emergency, MySejahtera use is voluntary. Klinik Kesihatan and most KKM services remain accessible without the app.

**Q: My vaccination record in MySejahtera is incomplete — what do I do?**
Visit your Klinik Kesihatan and request missing vaccination records be updated in the system. Bring your physical pink health booklet or any paper records as supporting documentation.

**Q: Can I delete my MySejahtera data?**
Account deletion requests can be submitted via the app settings or to KKM directly. Full data deletion is subject to KKM's data retention policy. For specific concerns, refer to Section 8.4.2 on PDPA rights.

**Q: Is private telehealth covered by medical insurance?**
Increasingly yes — many employer medical benefits and insurance plans now include telehealth coverage. Check with your HR or insurer for specifics.

**Q: Is the MyHEALTH Portal reliable?**
Yes — it is produced and maintained by KKM and reviewed by medical professionals. More reliable than general health websites or social media. It is not a substitute for professional consultation.
