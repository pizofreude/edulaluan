## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is KKM?**

KKM stands for Kementerian Kesihatan Malaysia — the Ministry of Health Malaysia.

It is the government body responsible for:

* Running public hospitals and clinics
* Making sure healthcare is safe and affordable
* Tracking and controlling diseases
* Telling Malaysians how to stay healthy

If you use a government hospital or Klinik Kesihatan, you are using a KKM facility.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

The Ministry of Health Malaysia (KKM) was established to protect and improve the health of all Malaysians.

Its core responsibilities:

* Planning and delivering public healthcare services
* Regulating private healthcare providers
* Managing national disease surveillance and outbreak response
* Setting food safety and pharmaceutical standards
* Running health promotion and disease prevention campaigns
* Administering the national immunisation programme

KKM operates under the **Malaysia Healthcare Blueprint** — the long-term strategic plan for healthcare delivery and reform.

---

## Part 1: What KKM Manages

### Public Healthcare Network

KKM operates Malaysia's national healthcare infrastructure:

| Facility Type | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Hospital Kerajaan (Government Hospitals) | Specialist and inpatient care, emergency services |
| Klinik Kesihatan (Health Clinics) | Primary care, maternal health, vaccinations, chronic disease management |
| Klinik Desa (Rural Health Clinics) | Healthcare access for rural and remote communities |
| Hospital Psychiatry | Dedicated mental health inpatient facilities |
| Institute for Medical Research (IMR) | Disease research and laboratory reference services |
| National Blood Centre (PDN) | Blood supply management and transfusion services |

### Private Sector Regulation

KKM also regulates private healthcare under the **Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 1998**:

* All private hospitals, clinics, and diagnostic centres must be licensed by KKM
* Private practitioners must hold valid Annual Practising Certificates (APC)
* Complaints about private healthcare providers can be submitted to KKM

### Food Safety and Pharmaceuticals

| Body | Under KKM | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Food Safety and Quality Division (BKKM) | Yes | Food standards, inspection, and enforcement |
| National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency (NPRA) | Yes | Drug registration, adverse drug reaction monitoring |
| Traditional and Complementary Medicine Division | Yes | Regulates traditional medicine practitioners and products |

## Part 2: Key Programmes and Initiatives

### National Health Programmes

| Programme | What It Does |
| --- | --- |
| National Immunisation Programme (NIP) | Free childhood and selected adult vaccinations |
| Maternal and Child Health | Antenatal care, postnatal follow-up, child development monitoring |
| Peka B40 | Free health screening for B40 Malaysians aged 40 and above |
| Skim Peduli Sihat (SPS) | Free primary care at panel clinics for B40 recipients |
| Non-Communicable Disease (NCD) Programme | Manages diabetes, hypertension, heart disease at primary care level |
| Mental Health Programme | Community mental health services, suicide prevention |

### Peka B40 — What It Covers

Peka B40 is specifically targeted at lower-income Malaysians:

* Free health screening for 8 conditions including cancer, cardiovascular, and kidney disease
* Available for those aged 40 and above receiving e-Kasih or Bantuan Sara Hidup (BSH/STR)
* Screenings done at Klinik Kesihatan and selected panel clinics

Check eligibility and enrol: **pekab40.com.my**

### Skim Peduli Sihat (SPS)

SPS allows B40 recipients to seek primary care at **private panel clinics** at no cost:

* Administered via MySejahtera and the patient's MyKad
* Covers general consultation, basic investigations, and medication
* Panel clinic list available via MySejahtera app

## Part 3: How to Access KKM Services

### As a Patient

| Need | Where to Go |
| --- | --- |
| General illness, minor injury | Klinik Kesihatan nearest to you |
| Specialist care | Referral from Klinik Kesihatan → Hospital Kerajaan |
| Emergency | Jabatan Kecemasan at any government hospital |
| Mental health support | Klinik Kesihatan (initial), Hospital Psychiatry (referred) |
| Vaccination | Klinik Kesihatan |
| Maternal care (antenatal) | Klinik Kesihatan or Hospital Kerajaan |

### Fees at Government Facilities

| Patient Category | Outpatient Fee | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Malaysian citizen | RM 1 per visit | All ages |
| Non-citizen | Higher rate | Varies by facility |
| Emergency (life-threatening) | Treated first | Billed after |

Hospital admission fees vary by ward class (First Class, Second Class, Third Class).

Third Class wards are heavily subsidised for Malaysian citizens.

### Finding a Facility

KKM's facility locator: **klik.moh.gov.my**

MySejahtera app also lists nearby Klinik Kesihatan and panel clinics.

## Part 4: Regulation of Private Healthcare

Patients at private facilities have formal rights under KKM regulation:

* Right to receive an itemised bill
* Right to be informed of treatment options and costs before proceeding
* Right to a second opinion
* Right to access their medical records

**To file a complaint about a private healthcare provider:**

Submit to KKM's complaint portal: **aduan.moh.gov.my**

Or call: **1-800-88-1000**

Complaints about registered medical practitioners (conduct and ethics) can also go to the **Malaysian Medical Council (MMC)**: mmc.gov.my

---

## Practical Habits for Navigating KKM Services

Patients who navigate public healthcare effectively tend to:

* Register at a nearby Klinik Kesihatan early — it becomes your primary care anchor point
* Bring MyKad to every visit — it is required for the RM 1 fee and system registration
* Use referral letters — bypassing the referral system usually means paying higher private fees
* Keep a personal health record (vaccination history, chronic conditions, medications)
* Check Peka B40 eligibility if over 40 and in the B40 group

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Skipping the referral system**

A patient with persistent back pain went directly to a government hospital's specialist clinic without a referral from Klinik Kesihatan. He was redirected back to primary care, delaying his appointment by weeks. Understanding the referral pathway would have saved time.

**Scenario 2: Peka B40 not claimed**

A 52-year-old factory worker qualified for Peka B40 free health screening but was unaware of the programme. An undetected early-stage condition could have been identified much earlier at no cost.

**Scenario 3: Private clinic complaint resolved**

A patient received an unexplained charge on a private clinic bill. She submitted a complaint via aduan.moh.gov.my. KKM investigated and the clinic corrected the billing error.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need an appointment at Klinik Kesihatan?**
Walk-ins are accepted. Some clinics also offer online queue registration via MySejahtera.

**Q: Can foreigners use KKM facilities?**
Yes, but at a higher fee rate. Emergency care is provided regardless of nationality.

**Q: Is KKM the same as the Malaysian Medical Council?**
No. KKM is the government ministry. MMC is the independent statutory body that regulates medical practitioners' conduct and ethics.

**Q: What if I disagree with a public hospital's treatment decision?**
You have the right to seek a second opinion. Speak to the treating doctor or the hospital's patient relations unit.

**Q: Where can I verify if a supplement or traditional medicine is registered?**
Check the NPRA's public database at npra.gov.my — all registered health products have a registration number (MAL number).
