## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is LHDN?**

LHDN is the government agency that collects income tax.

If you work and earn money, you may need to:

* Register as a taxpayer
* Report your income
* Pay tax if required

**What is e-Filing?**

e-Filing is the website where you submit your tax form online.

Usually:

* Employees use **Form BE**
* Business owners use **Form B**

Most employees already pay tax monthly through salary deductions (PCB).

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

The **Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (LHDN)** administers Malaysia’s direct tax system including:

* Personal income tax
* Corporate tax
* Real property gains tax
* Stamp duty

For most individuals, interaction with LHDN focuses on **annual income tax filing** through the e-Filing system.

Even if your employer deducts PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan), you are still responsible for submitting your annual tax declaration.

---

## What Is PCB?

PCB (Monthly Tax Deduction) is:

Tax deducted automatically from your salary.

Purpose:

* Spread tax payments across the year
* Reduce large lump sum payments
* Improve compliance

Important:

PCB is **not the final tax calculation**. Your final tax is determined after you:

* Declare income
* Claim tax reliefs
* Submit e-Filing

You may:

* Pay additional tax
* Receive a tax refund
* Have zero balance

---

## Who Needs To File Tax?

You generally need to file if:

| Situation          | Filing likely required |
| ------------------ | ---------------------- |
| Full-time employee | Yes                    |
| Part-time income   | Yes                    |
| Freelance income   | Yes                    |
| Business owner     | Yes                    |
| No income          | Usually no             |

Even if PCB already deducted, filing is still required if your income meets the taxable threshold.

---

## When To File

Typical filing windows:

| Category            | Deadline                   |
| ------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Employees (Form BE) | 30 April                   |
| e-Filing employees  | 15 May (typical extension) |
| Business owners     | 30 June                    |

Always verify deadlines yearly as they may change.

---

## What You Need Before Filing

Prepare:

| Document            | Why                          |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| EA Form             | Income summary from employer |
| IC number           | Identity verification        |
| Bank account        | Refund processing            |
| Receipts            | Tax relief claims            |
| Previous tax number | Reference                    |

Your employer must provide your EA Form before filing season.

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

* Forgetting to file because PCB deducted
* Not claiming eligible reliefs
* Entering wrong EA data
* Missing deadlines
* Ignoring LHDN notices

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Missed refund**

An employee paid PCB but did not file tax. They missed a RM1,200 refund.

**Scenario 2: Penalty risk**

A freelancer did not declare side income and received a compliance notice later.

**Scenario 3: Relief opportunity**

A graduate did not know laptop purchases could qualify under lifestyle relief.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to file if PCB already deducted?**
Yes. PCB is only a prepayment.

**Q: What happens if I do not file?**
Possible penalties or compliance action.

**Q: How long does refund take?**
Usually within 30 working days if accurate.

**Q: Can I file myself without an agent?**
Yes. Most employees can file independently.

**Q: Is e-Filing difficult?**
Usually takes 15–30 minutes for employees.
