## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**Do businesses pay tax differently from individuals?**

Yes.

When you run a business, you may need to:

* Pay tax on business profits
* Collect and submit SST (a sales or service tax) on behalf of the government
* Estimate and prepay your tax before the year ends
* Withhold a portion of payments made to foreign suppliers or contractors

Each obligation has its own deadlines and forms.

Missing them costs money.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Business tax in Malaysia is administered by:

* **LHDN (Inland Revenue Board)** — income tax, withholding tax, CP204
* **Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD)** — SST

Your obligations depend on:

* Business structure (sole trader, partnership, Sdn Bhd, etc.)
* Revenue and activity type
* Whether you pay foreign parties

This page covers the four core areas every business owner should understand.

## Part 1: Business Income Tax

### Who Pays It

| Structure                     | Tax Form | Tax Rate                                      |
| ----------------------------- | -------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Sole proprietorship           | Form B   | Personal income tax rates (0–30%)             |
| Partnership                   | Form P + Form B (each partner) | Personal income tax rates   |
| Private company (Sdn Bhd)     | Form C   | 17% on first RM 600,000; 24% above that (for SMEs) |
| Limited liability partnership (PLT) | Form PT | Personal income tax rates (partners)    |

**Key distinction:**

Sole traders and partners are taxed as individuals — their business profit is added to personal income.

Companies are separate legal entities taxed independently.

### What Counts as Business Income

All revenue from business activities, including:

* Product sales
* Service fees
* Commissions
* Rental from business properties
* Digital product or platform income

Deductible expenses reduce taxable income. Common deductions:

| Expense                    | Deductible?                              |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Staff salaries and EPF     | Yes                                      |
| Office rent                | Yes                                      |
| Business equipment         | Yes (capital allowance applies)          |
| Marketing and advertising  | Yes                                      |
| Personal expenses          | No                                       |
| Fines and penalties        | No                                       |
| Private vehicle (partial)  | Partial, with conditions                 |

### Filing Deadlines

| Structure           | Deadline                          |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Sole proprietor     | 30 June (Form B)                  |
| Company (Sdn Bhd)   | 7 months after financial year end |
| Partnership         | 30 June (Form P)                  |

## Part 2: SST (Sales and Service Tax)

### What Is SST

SST replaced GST in Malaysia in September 2018.

It has two components:

| Component    | Who Collects It                                         | Rate              |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Sales Tax    | Manufacturers and importers of taxable goods            | 5% or 10%         |
| Service Tax  | Service providers exceeding the registration threshold  | 8% (from 2024)    |

SST is not a value-added tax. It applies at one point in the supply chain, not at every stage.

### When You Must Register for Service Tax

Mandatory registration threshold:

**RM 500,000 in taxable services within 12 months**

If your revenue from taxable services crosses this threshold, you must:

1. Register with RMCD via the MySST portal
2. Charge 8% service tax on invoices to customers
3. Submit returns and remit collected tax every 2 months

**Important:** Revenue from exported services and certain exempt services (e.g. financial services, education, healthcare) do not count toward the threshold.

### Common Taxable Services

* Professional services (consulting, legal, accounting, IT)
* Food and beverage operators (above threshold)
* Hotels and accommodation
* Telecommunications
* Advertising

### SST for Manufacturers

Sales tax applies to manufacturers whose taxable goods exceed:

**RM 500,000 in annual sales**

Goods differ in rate:

* 5%: Selected goods (e.g. certain food products, construction materials)
* 10%: Most taxable manufactured goods

### Filing SST Returns

| Period         | Deadline                  |
| -------------- | ------------------------- |
| Every 2 months | Last day of the following month |

Submit via: **MySST portal (mysst.customs.gov.my)**

Failure to register when required or to remit collected SST is a serious offence under the Service Tax Act 2018.

## Part 3: Estimated Tax (CP204)

### What Is CP204

CP204 is the **tax estimate form for companies**.

Malaysia's tax system requires companies to:

* Estimate annual tax payable before the year begins
* Pay in instalments over 12 months
* Reconcile actual tax at year-end

This prevents companies from owing a large lump sum at filing.

### How It Works

```
1. Submit CP204 → 30 days before financial year begins
2. LHDN reviews estimate
3. Company pays in monthly instalments (usually 12)
4. File Form C after year-end
5. Pay balance or receive refund based on actual profit
```

### CP204 for Sole Traders

Sole traders do not use CP204.

They use **CP500** — a bi-monthly instalment scheme for self-employed individuals with significant business income.

### Consequences of Under-Estimating

If actual tax exceeds estimate by more than 30%:

A **10% penalty** is imposed on the difference.

This incentivises realistic estimations.

## Part 4: Withholding Tax

### What Is It

Withholding tax (WHT) is a mechanism where the **payer deducts tax at source** before remitting payment to a foreign recipient.

If you pay a foreign freelancer, consultant, or company for services rendered in Malaysia, you may be required to withhold a portion of that payment and remit it to LHDN.

### Common Withholding Tax Rates (Malaysia)

| Payment Type                        | Rate    |
| ----------------------------------- | ------- |
| Royalties                           | 10%     |
| Interest                            | 15%     |
| Special classes of income (technical fees, management fees, rental of movables) | 10% |
| Contract payments to non-residents  | 10% (contractor) + 3% (employee portion) |

Rates may be reduced under Malaysia's **Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs)** with specific countries.

### When WHT Applies

WHT applies when:

* The recipient is a **non-resident** (foreign individual or company)
* The income source is in Malaysia
* The payment type falls within WHT categories

WHT **does not apply** to most payments between Malaysian residents.

### Filing and Payment

| Obligation         | Deadline                          | Form   |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------- | ------ |
| Remit WHT to LHDN  | Within 1 month of payment date    | CP37 / CP37A / CP37D (depending on type) |

Failure to withhold and remit is the **payer's liability**, not the foreign recipient's.

## Common Compliance Mistakes

| Mistake                                         | Risk                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Not registering for SST when threshold is met   | Back taxes, penalties, possible prosecution   |
| Treating personal expenses as business expenses | Disallowed deductions, audit risk             |
| Under-estimating CP204 significantly            | 10% penalty on excess                         |
| Forgetting WHT on foreign contractor payments   | Payer bears the tax liability                 |
| Mixing personal and business accounts           | Difficult to defend deductions under audit    |
| Late SST return submission                      | RM 5,000–RM 50,000 fine per offence           |

---

## Practical Compliance Habits

Low-risk businesses typically:

* Maintain separate business and personal bank accounts
* Keep all invoices, receipts, and contracts for at least 7 years
* Reconcile accounts monthly, not just at year-end
* Work with a licensed tax agent for Form C and SST submissions
* Track revenue against SST threshold in real time

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: SST registration missed**

A boutique IT consultancy grew past RM 500,000 in annual revenue but did not register for service tax. Upon RMCD audit, they owed two years of backdated service tax plus penalties — a six-figure liability.

**Scenario 2: CP204 underestimated**

A Sdn Bhd estimated conservatively to preserve cashflow. Their actual profit came in 40% above estimate. They paid the 10% penalty on the difference — avoidable with better forecasting.

**Scenario 3: WHT overlooked**

A startup paid a Singapore-based UX design firm without withholding. LHDN assessed the Malaysian company for unpaid WHT plus late payment penalties. The foreign firm never saw the issue — the Malaysian payer bore the cost.

## FAQ

**Q: Does registering my business with SSM mean I am automatically registered for tax?**
No. SSM registration (business registration) and LHDN tax registration are separate. You must register with LHDN independently.

**Q: I am a freelancer. Do I use Form B or Form C?**
Form B. Freelancers and sole traders are taxed as individuals, not companies.

**Q: What if I am both an employee and run a side business?**
Declare both. Employment income goes on Form BE sections; business income on Form B. You file Form B, which covers both.

**Q: Do I need a tax agent?**
Not mandatory, but strongly recommended for Sdn Bhd, SST-registered businesses, or any business with foreign payments.

**Q: What records must I keep?**
All business records for 7 years: invoices, receipts, contracts, bank statements, payroll records.
