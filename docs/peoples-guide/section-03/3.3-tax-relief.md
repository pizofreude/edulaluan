## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is tax relief?**

Tax relief means:

Money you spent that reduces how much tax you need to pay.

Example:

Income = RM40,000

Reliefs = RM10,000

Tax calculated on:

RM30,000 instead.

This may:

* Reduce tax
* Give refund
* Reduce payment

**Examples of relief:**

* EPF savings
* Medical insurance
* Laptop purchase
* Books
* Education fees

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Tax relief reduces your **chargeable income**, not your tax directly.

Formula concept:

```
Income – Reliefs = Taxable Income
```

Reliefs exist to encourage:

* Education
* Healthcare
* Family support
* Savings
* Lifestyle development

Reliefs only apply if:

* You qualify
* You paid the expense
* You keep documentation

---

## Major Individual Tax Relief Categories

Below are commonly claimed reliefs (amounts may change yearly).

| Relief Category          | Typical Limit     | Purpose                  |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- | ------------------------ |
| Individual relief        | RM9,000           | Automatic for taxpayers  |
| EPF + Life insurance     | Up to RM7,000     | Retirement protection    |
| Medical insurance        | Included above    | Healthcare protection    |
| Lifestyle relief         | Up to RM2,500     | Books, devices, internet |
| Education fees           | Up to RM7,000     | Skill development        |
| Medical expenses parents | Up to RM8,000     | Family support           |
| Child relief             | RM2,000 per child | Family support           |
| SSPN education savings   | Up to RM8,000     | Education planning       |

Always verify current limits from LHDN as they may change.

---

## Lifestyle Relief (Commonly Used)

This is one of the most accessible relief categories.

Examples:

Eligible:

* Laptop
* Smartphone
* Books
* Sports equipment
* Internet subscription

Not eligible:

* Luxury goods
* Non-educational items
* Business purchases

Important:

Keep receipts showing:

* Your name
* Date
* Amount
* Vendor

---

## Education Relief

Encourages skill development.

Examples:

Eligible:

* Professional certifications
* Postgraduate courses
* Approved skills programmes

Not always eligible:

* Short hobby classes
* Informal courses
* Non-recognised providers

---

## Medical Relief

Supports healthcare costs.

Possible claims:

* Serious disease treatment
* Fertility treatment
* Mental health treatment
* Parents' medical expenses
* Medical equipment

Always keep:

Medical invoices and doctor documentation.

---

## Family Relief

Supports dependents.

Includes:

| Category              | Purpose                  |
| --------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Spouse relief         | Single income households |
| Child relief          | Raising children         |
| Disabled child relief | Additional support       |
| Parents relief        | Elder care               |

---

## Documentation Requirements

Always keep:

| Document            | Why                  |
| ------------------- | -------------------- |
| Receipts            | Proof of purchase    |
| Invoices            | Transaction record   |
| Bank statements     | Payment verification |
| Medical reports     | Healthcare claims    |
| Course certificates | Education claims     |

LHDN may audit up to several years later.

Recommended:

Keep documents **7 years**.

---

## How Relief Affects Refunds

Example scenario:

| Item           | Amount   |
| -------------- | -------- |
| Income         | RM50,000 |
| Reliefs        | RM15,000 |
| Taxable income | RM35,000 |

Lower taxable income:

Lower tax.

If PCB already deducted:

Possible refund.

---

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

* Claiming without receipts
* Claiming spouse purchases incorrectly
* Claiming business expenses as personal relief
* Forgetting reliefs
* Claiming in wrong category

---

## Audit Risk Warning

Do not:

* Fabricate receipts
* Claim fake expenses
* Claim duplicate reliefs
* Guess numbers

Penalties may include:

* Additional tax
* Fines
* Legal action

Accuracy matters more than maximising claims.

## FAQ

**Q: Do reliefs guarantee a refund?**
No. They reduce taxable income.

**Q: Can I claim without receipts?**
No.

**Q: Can husband and wife claim same receipt?**
Usually no (unless allowed category).

**Q: Can I claim cash purchases?**
Yes, if documented.

**Q: How long keep receipts?**
Recommended 7 years.
