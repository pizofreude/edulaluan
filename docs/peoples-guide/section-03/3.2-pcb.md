## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is PCB?**

PCB is tax money taken from your salary every month.

Example:

If your salary is RM3,500:

Your payslip may show:

* Salary: RM3,500
* PCB: RM120
* Take home: RM3,380

Your employer sends that RM120 to LHDN.

**Important:**

This does NOT mean your tax is finished.

At the end of the year:

You must still file tax.

You may:

* Get refund
* Pay extra
* Pay nothing

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan), also called **Monthly Tax Deduction (MTD)**, is a tax prepayment mechanism under the Income Tax Act.

Employers must:

* Calculate PCB
* Deduct from salary
* Submit to LHDN monthly

PCB applies to:

* Salary
* Allowances
* Bonuses
* Commissions
* Certain benefits

It ensures taxes are collected gradually rather than annually.

---

## How PCB Is Calculated

PCB depends on:

| Factor                         | Effect                      |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| Salary amount                  | Higher salary increases PCB |
| Marital status                 | Affects tax bracket         |
| Number of children             | Reduces tax                 |
| EPF contributions              | Reduces taxable income      |
| Tax relief claims via employer | May reduce PCB              |

Employers typically use:

LHDN PCB calculator or payroll software.

---

## Where You See PCB

PCB appears on your payslip.

Typical payslip section:

| Item         | Amount  |
| ------------ | ------- |
| Basic salary | RM4,000 |
| EPF          | RM440   |
| PERKESO      | RM20    |
| EIS          | RM8     |
| PCB          | RM150   |

If you do not see PCB:

Possible reasons:

* Income below taxable level
* Employer misclassification
* Payroll error

Always clarify if unsure.

---

## PCB vs Final Tax

Many workers misunderstand this difference.

| PCB                  | Final Tax            |
| -------------------- | -------------------- |
| Monthly estimate     | Annual calculation   |
| Done by employer     | Done by taxpayer     |
| Based on assumptions | Based on actual data |
| May be inaccurate    | Final amount         |

After filing:

Outcome may be:

| Result         | Meaning      |
| -------------- | ------------ |
| Refund         | PCB too high |
| Additional tax | PCB too low  |
| Zero balance   | PCB accurate |

---

## Can You Adjust PCB?

Yes.

Situations where adjustment may help:

* Multiple incomes
* Spouse relief changes
* New child
* Large relief claims
* Bonus income

Possible actions:

* Submit TP1 form (relief adjustment)
* Inform employer
* Plan during tax filing

---

## Common PCB Situations

### Scenario 1: No PCB deducted

Possible causes:

* Salary below threshold
* HR error
* Incorrect tax status

Action:

Check with HR.

---

### Scenario 2: PCB suddenly increases

Possible causes:

* Bonus payment
* Salary increase
* Relief changes

This is normal if income increases.

---

### Scenario 3: PCB too high

Possible causes:

* Relief not declared
* Incorrect marital status
* Payroll assumption

You may recover excess via refund during filing.

---

## What To Do If Employer Does Not Deduct PCB

Employer responsibility:

Employers must deduct PCB if required.

If not:

You may still be responsible for tax.

Possible actions:

* Ask HR
* Verify EA Form
* Contact LHDN if serious issue

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Unexpected tax bill**

An employee had two jobs. PCB deducted separately was insufficient. Extra tax was required after filing.

**Scenario 2: Refund opportunity**

A worker overpaid PCB and received RM900 refund after claiming reliefs.

**Scenario 3: Payroll error**

An employer misclassified marital status, causing excessive PCB deductions.

## FAQ

**Q: Is PCB mandatory?**
Yes, if income reaches taxable threshold.

**Q: Can I opt out of PCB?**
No, if eligible.

**Q: Is PCB the same as EPF?**
No. PCB is tax. EPF is retirement savings.

**Q: Why does PCB change monthly?**
Because salary components may change.

**Q: Do freelancers have PCB?**
No. They pay tax during filing instead.
