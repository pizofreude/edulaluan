## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is zakat?**

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.

It is an obligation — not optional — for Muslims who meet certain conditions.

Two main types:

* **Zakat Fitrah** — paid once a year during Ramadan, a fixed small amount, everyone pays
* **Zakat Harta** — paid on wealth (income, savings, business, gold, etc.) when it crosses a threshold

When you pay zakat, the amount is also deducted from your income tax — so you pay less tax.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Zakat is a structured system of wealth redistribution in Islam.

In Malaysia, it is:

* **Legally recognised** — administered under state Islamic law (enakmen zakat negeri)
* **State-governed** — each state has its own zakat authority (Majlis Agama Islam Negeri)
* **Tax-integrated** — zakat paid to a state authority qualifies as a tax rebate under Malaysian income tax law

Zakat is distributed to eight categories of recipients (asnaf):

| Asnaf           | Description                                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Fakir           | Extremely poor, no means of income          |
| Miskin          | Poor but with some minimal income           |
| Amil            | Zakat collectors and administrators         |
| Muallaf         | New Muslims or those whose hearts are to be reconciled |
| Riqab           | Freedom from bondage (historical; now reinterpreted) |
| Gharimin        | Those burdened by debt                      |
| Fi Sabilillah   | Those striving in the cause of Allah        |
| Ibnus Sabil     | Stranded travellers                         |

## Part 1: Zakat Fitrah vs Zakat Harta

### Zakat Fitrah

| Item            | Detail                                                    |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Who pays        | Every Muslim who is alive at the end of Ramadan           |
| When            | During Ramadan, before Solat Eid                          |
| Amount          | Fixed by state authority annually (typically RM 7–RM 22) |
| Basis           | Based on local staple food price (rice equivalent)        |
| Paid on behalf of | Head of household may pay for dependants               |

Zakat Fitrah is the most straightforward obligation — a fixed annual amount set by each state.

### Zakat Harta

Zakat Harta is wealth-based. It applies to accumulated wealth across several categories when two conditions are met:

* **Nisab** — wealth has reached the minimum threshold
* **Haul** — wealth has been held for a full lunar year (approximately 354 days)

---

## Part 2: Nisab and Haul

### Nisab

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth that triggers a zakat obligation.

It is pegged to the value of:

* **Gold nisab:** 85 grams of gold (approx. RM 25,000–RM 30,000+ depending on current gold price)
* **Silver nisab:** 595 grams of silver (lower threshold; some scholars use this for income zakat)

In practice, Malaysian zakat authorities typically use the **gold nisab** for most zakat harta calculations.

**Nisab values change** as gold prices fluctuate. Always check with your state zakat authority for the current figure.

### Haul

Haul means the wealth must have been in your possession for one full Islamic lunar year.

Exceptions where haul does not apply:

* **Zakat Pertanian (agriculture)** — paid at harvest, no haul required
* **Zakat Rikaz (buried treasure or natural findings)** — paid immediately

For most working Malaysians, haul is satisfied automatically by year-end savings or accumulated income.

---

## Part 3: Types of Zakat Harta

### Zakat Pendapatan (Income Zakat)

The most widely applicable type for salaried and self-employed Malaysians.

| Item        | Detail                                                    |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Who pays    | Muslims with annual net income above nisab                |
| Rate        | 2.5% of eligible income                                   |
| Calculation | Based on net income after basic living deductions (varies by state) |
| Haul        | Typically calculated on annual income or monthly via salary deduction |

Many employers facilitate automatic monthly zakat deduction from salary (potongan zakat gaji) through their HR or payroll system.

---

### Zakat Simpanan (Savings Zakat)

| Item        | Detail                                                        |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Who pays    | Muslims with savings held above nisab for a full lunar year   |
| Rate        | 2.5% of total savings balance                                 |
| What counts | Bank savings, ASB/ASN dividends, fixed deposits, EPF savings (with conditions) |
| EPF note    | EPF is subject to zakat; some states offer EPF zakat payment via EPF directly |

---

### Zakat Perniagaan (Business Zakat)

| Item        | Detail                                                        |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Who pays    | Muslim business owners whose business assets exceed nisab     |
| Rate        | 2.5% of zakatable business assets                            |
| Calculation | Based on current assets minus current liabilities (working capital method) |
| Haul        | Business must have operated for a full lunar year             |

---

### Zakat Emas dan Perak (Gold and Silver)

| Item        | Detail                                             |
| ----------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Who pays    | Muslims holding gold above 85g or silver above 595g |
| Rate        | 2.5%                                               |
| What counts | Physical gold, gold savings accounts, jewellery (with conditions) |

---

### Other Types (Less Common)

| Type                  | Basis                                   |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Zakat Pertanian       | Agricultural produce at harvest         |
| Zakat Saham (shares)  | Investment portfolio value              |
| Zakat KWSP / unit trust | Accumulated investment value          |
| Zakat Rikaz           | Found buried wealth or natural findings |

## Zakat as a Tax Rebate

Zakat paid to a **recognised state zakat authority** qualifies as a **tax rebate** under Malaysian income tax law.

This is different from a tax relief:

| Mechanism    | How It Works                                      | Example                                      |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Tax Relief   | Reduces your chargeable income before tax is calculated | RM 1,000 relief reduces taxable income by RM 1,000 |
| Tax Rebate   | Reduces your actual tax payable, ringgit for ringgit | RM 1,000 zakat paid = RM 1,000 less tax owed |

**Zakat is a rebate, not a relief** — it is more valuable than standard reliefs.

### Conditions for the Rebate

* Must be paid to a **state-recognised zakat authority**
* Payment to mosques, individuals, or unrecognised bodies does not qualify
* Keep your **zakat receipt** as proof for e-Filing
* Enter the amount under **Rebat Zakat** in your LHDN e-Filing form

Cross-reference: **Section 3.3 — Individual Tax Reliefs** for the full relief and rebate list.

## Practical Habits for Zakat Compliance

Muslims who manage zakat well typically:

* Use their state zakat authority's online calculator annually
* Set aside monthly savings specifically for zakat pendapatan
* Keep zakat receipts alongside tax documents for e-Filing
* Check nisab rates at the start of each year (gold price changes affect the threshold)
* Confirm EPF zakat payment status via i-Akaun if applicable

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Zakat and tax rebate misunderstood**

A salaried Muslim professional paid zakat to a local surau committee. The payment did not qualify as a recognised zakat authority payment and could not be claimed as a tax rebate. Paying via the state zakat portal instead would have reduced tax payable by the full zakat amount.

**Scenario 2: Overlooked savings zakat**

A salaried employee focused only on income zakat via salary deduction. Her ASB savings had grown above nisab and remained there for over a year — triggering a zakat simpanan obligation she had not been calculating. Most people are unaware savings have a separate zakat obligation.

**Scenario 3: Business zakat undercalculated**

A Muslim SME owner calculated zakat on net profit. The correct method for zakat perniagaan uses working capital (current assets minus current liabilities), not profit — resulting in a different, sometimes higher, obligation.

## FAQ

**Q: Is zakat compulsory if I am Muslim but not practising?**
Zakat is a religious obligation under Islamic law. Whether to fulfil it is a personal and religious matter. This page presents the obligation and how to fulfil it; it does not adjudicate on personal belief.

**Q: Can I pay zakat to a family member in need?**
Paying directly to a family member does not constitute zakat unless channelled through a recognised zakat authority. It may qualify as infaq or sadaqah instead.

**Q: Is there a penalty for not paying zakat in Malaysia?**
State Islamic law (enakmen) varies. Some states have provisions for enforcement, though in practice individual enforcement is rare. The obligation remains a religious one.

**Q: If I already paid income tax, do I still owe zakat?**
Yes. Zakat and income tax are separate obligations. However, zakat paid reduces your income tax payable as a rebate — so fulfilling both reduces the double burden.

**Q: Can a non-Muslim pay zakat?**
No. Zakat is a religious obligation specific to Muslims.
