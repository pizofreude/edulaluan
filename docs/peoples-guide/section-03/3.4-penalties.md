### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia’s tax system operates on **self-assessment**.

This means:

You are responsible for:

* Declaring income correctly
* Claiming correct reliefs
* Submitting on time
* Keeping records

LHDN may:

* Review submissions
* Request documents
* Conduct audits
* Impose penalties

Most issues arise from:

* Ignorance
* Poor record keeping
* Late filing
* Incorrect claims

Not fraud.

---

## Common Tax Offences

Typical compliance issues include:

| Offence                 | Description                  |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Late filing             | Missing submission deadline  |
| Under-declaring income  | Not declaring all income     |
| Incorrect relief claims | Claiming ineligible expenses |
| Failure to keep records | No supporting documents      |
| Non-payment             | Ignoring tax payable         |

More serious offences include:

* Deliberate tax evasion
* False documentation
* Fraudulent reporting

---

## Late Filing Penalties

If you file late:

Possible consequences:

| Situation        | Possible Outcome         |
| ---------------- | ------------------------ |
| Late filing      | Penalty percentage added |
| Continued delay  | Increased penalty        |
| Ignoring notices | Enforcement action       |

Penalty rates depend on:

* Delay length
* Tax amount
* Compliance history

---

## Under-Declaration Risk

This happens when:

Income declared is lower than actual.

Examples:

* Freelance income not declared
* Side business ignored
* Rental income omitted

Possible outcomes:

* Additional tax
* Penalty assessment
* Audit review

Important:

LHDN receives data from multiple sources including:

* Employers
* Financial institutions
* Government agencies

---

## Incorrect Relief Claims

Common mistakes:

* Claiming without receipts
* Claiming non-eligible purchases
* Claiming same relief twice
* Claiming spouse expenses incorrectly

Usually resolved by:

Adjustment and additional tax.

Serious cases may involve penalties.

---

## Record Keeping Requirements

Taxpayers must keep:

Supporting documents.

Recommended:

7 years retention.

Examples:

| Document           | Purpose             |
| ------------------ | ------------------- |
| EA Form            | Income verification |
| Receipts           | Relief claims       |
| Bank records       | Payment proof       |
| Medical invoices   | Healthcare claims   |
| Education invoices | Course claims       |

If audited:

You must provide proof.

---

## Honest Mistakes vs Fraud

Important distinction:

| Situation            | Treatment           |
| -------------------- | ------------------- |
| Typing mistake       | Usually correctable |
| Misunderstood relief | Adjustment          |
| Late filing          | Penalty             |
| Fake claims          | Serious offence     |
| Hidden income        | Serious offence     |

LHDN typically differentiates:

Error vs intent.

Voluntary correction usually reduces risk.

## Practical Compliance Habits

Low risk taxpayers usually:

* File yearly without delay
* Keep receipts organised
* Declare all income
* Claim only eligible reliefs
* Respond quickly to LHDN

Compliance is mostly about consistency.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Late filing**

A worker delayed filing by several months and paid additional penalty.

**Scenario 2: Missing freelance income**

A designer did not declare side income and had to pay additional tax later.

**Scenario 3: Honest correction**

A taxpayer corrected a mistake early and avoided escalation.

## FAQ

**Q: Will I be jailed for small mistakes?**
No. Usually only serious fraud cases.

**Q: Can I fix mistakes after submission?**
Yes, within allowed amendment period.

**Q: What if I cannot pay tax immediately?**
Contact LHDN to discuss options.

**Q: How common are audits?**
Most taxpayers are never audited if compliant.

**Q: Should I panic if contacted?**
No. Respond calmly and provide information.
