## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is price control?**

Some everyday goods in Malaysia have a maximum price set by the government.

This means sellers cannot charge more than that price — even if they want to.

If a shop charges you more than the controlled price for petrol, cooking oil, or certain foods — that is illegal.

You can report it to KPDNHEP and they will investigate.

**Anti-profiteering** is a related rule — it stops businesses from raising prices unreasonably, even for goods that are not on the controlled list.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia uses two complementary mechanisms to protect consumers from unfair pricing:

**1. Price Control** — under the **Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011 (PCAPA)** and earlier orders, the government fixes maximum retail prices (MRP) for selected essential goods. Selling above the MRP is a criminal offence.

**2. Anti-Profiteering** — also under PCAPA, businesses are prohibited from making unreasonable profits by raising prices beyond what cost increases justify. This applies to goods and services not covered by price control.

Both are enforced by **KPDNHEP** through routine market surveillance and complaint-driven investigations.

## Part 1: Price Control — Controlled Goods

### What Is a Controlled Good?

A controlled good is one where the government has gazetted a maximum price under the **Price Control Act 1946** or subsequent orders. The price may be:

| Control Type | What It Means |
| --- | --- |
| Maximum retail price (MRP) | Seller cannot charge above this price at any level |
| Controlled supply price | Wholesale or distributor price is capped |
| Regional price variation | Different maximum prices apply in Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, and Sarawak |

### Commonly Controlled Goods — Reference Table

| Category | Item | Control Type | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Fuel** | RON 95 petrol | MRP (fixed) | Subsidised; uniform price nationwide |
| **Fuel** | Diesel (standard) | MRP (fixed) | Subsidised for eligible users |
| **Cooking oil** | Cooking oil (bottled, 1kg and 2kg) | MRP | Includes branded and generic |
| **Flour** | Wheat flour (subsidised) | MRP | Standard grade |
| **Rice** | Super tempatan white rice | MRP | Selected grades; price varies by region |
| **Bread** | Standard white bread (selected) | MRP | Basic loaf formats |
| **Sugar** | White sugar (standard grade) | MRP | Per kg maximum |
| **Eggs** | Chicken eggs (Grade A, B, C) | MRP | By grade; varies Peninsular vs East Malaysia |
| **Fresh chicken** | Standard whole chicken | MRP | Gazetted price per kg |
| **LPG** | Domestic cooking gas (subsidised cylinder) | MRP | 12kg and 14kg cylinders |
| **Cement** | Portland cement (selected types) | Controlled supply price | Building material |

**Important caveats:**

* Controlled prices are updated periodically by gazette — prices in this table are indicative and may change
* Price controls often apply to specific grades, weights, or formats — premium or branded variants may not be controlled
* Prices in Sabah and Sarawak may differ from Peninsular Malaysia
* Always check the current KPDNHEP price list for definitive figures: **kpdnhep.gov.my/senarai-harga**

## Part 2: Anti-Profiteering

### What It Is

Anti-profiteering provisions under PCAPA 2011 apply to **all goods and services** — including those not on the controlled list.

A business commits an anti-profiteering offence if it:

* Raises prices beyond what is justified by increases in input costs
* Makes unreasonable profits at consumers' expense
* Uses supply disruptions or high demand as cover for excessive price hikes

### How It Is Assessed

KPDNHEP uses a **cost-plus analysis** — examining whether a price increase reflects actual cost increases in:

* Raw materials
* Transportation and logistics
* Labour
* Overheads

If price increases significantly exceed cost increases — particularly if profit margins expand — the business may be found guilty of profiteering.

### Penalties

| Offence | Individual | Company |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Price control violation | Up to RM 100,000 fine or 3 years imprisonment | Up to RM 500,000 fine |
| Anti-profiteering offence | Up to RM 500,000 fine or 3 years imprisonment | Up to RM 1,000,000 fine |
| Repeat offence | Higher penalties; prosecution escalated | Same |

These are significant penalties — businesses take KPDNHEP enforcement seriously.

## Part 3: Festive Seasons and Supply Disruption Gouging

### Why Festive Periods Are High Risk

Price gouging is most prevalent during:

* **Hari Raya Aidilfitri** (Ramadan and pre-Raya period)
* **Chinese New Year**
* **Deepavali**
* **School holidays**
* **Supply disruptions** (floods, supply chain issues, pandemics)

During these periods, demand for certain goods (chicken, vegetables, cooking oil, festive food) spikes — and some sellers exploit this.

### KPDNHEP's Festive Response

KPDNHEP typically:

* Deploys additional enforcement officers to wet markets, hypermarkets, and pasar tani during festive periods
* Issues public price lists before major festivals
* Operates special complaint hotlines
* Conducts high-visibility raids in the weeks before major festivals

### What You Can Do During Festive Periods

* Check KPDNHEP's published festive price list before shopping
* Compare prices between sellers — not all will overcharge
* Report violations immediately — festive enforcement is active and responsive
* Be specific when reporting: seller name, location, item, price charged vs controlled price, date and time

The **PriceCatcher** app (developed by the Department of Statistics Malaysia in collaboration with KPDNHEP) allows consumers to:

* Check current prices of over 300 items across retailers
* Compare prices across locations

This is one of the most useful consumer tools in Malaysia for day-to-day price checking — and significantly underused.

## Part 5: How to Report Violations

### What to Collect Before Reporting

| Evidence | Why It Helps |
| --- | --- |
| Photo of price tag | Shows price displayed |
| Receipt | Shows price charged |
| Seller name and address | Required for investigation |
| Date and time | Establishes the offence |
| Item name, weight, grade | Confirms it is a controlled item |

Take both the price tag photo and receipt at the point of sale — once you leave, recreating this evidence is difficult.

### How to Report

| Channel | How | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| eAduan online | e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my → Aduan Harga | Detailed complaints with photo evidence |
| KPDNHEP Hotline | 1-800-886-800 | Quick reports, festive period violations |
| PriceCatcher App | In-app report function | Reporting retail price anomalies |
| Walk-in | Nearest KPDNHEP office | Complex cases |

### What Happens After You Report

```
Day 1–3:   Complaint acknowledged; reference number issued
Week 1–2:  Enforcement officers visit the premises
Week 2–4:  Investigation completed
            Compound issued, warning given, or case closed
```

For festive season violations, response is typically faster — enforcement is already deployed in the field.

## Practical Habits for Price-Conscious Consumers

Consumers who catch and report price violations tend to:

* Check controlled prices before shopping — particularly for chicken, eggs, cooking oil, and petrol
* Use the PriceCatcher app to compare prices across retailers for non-controlled items
* Always take a photo of price tags for major food purchases at wet markets
* Keep receipts for controlled goods purchases during festive periods
* Report promptly — not days later when evidence is less fresh

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Overcharged for chicken at pasar tani**

A consumer bought 2kg of standard whole chicken at RM 4 per kg above the controlled price during Hari Raya week. She photographed the price tag and the receipt at the stall. She filed an eAduan complaint with the photos, stall name, and location. KPDNHEP officers visited the market three days later — the stall operator was compounded.

**Scenario 2: Hotel profiteering during major event**

A hotel near a major stadium tripled room rates during a concert weekend. Several guests complained to KPDNHEP, arguing the increase was unjustified by cost increases. KPDNHEP investigated under anti-profiteering provisions and found the margin expansion unjustifiable. The hotel was required to justify its pricing and faced a formal warning.

**Scenario 3: PriceCatcher reveals retailer anomaly**

A consumer checking prices on the PriceCatcher app noticed that one hypermarket branch was consistently pricing cooking oil above the maximum for the 2kg bottled category. She reported it via the app's report function. KPDNHEP's data monitoring team flagged the retailer for an inspection.

### Path A: I was overcharged for a controlled item

**Step 1:** Check the current controlled price at **kpdnhep.gov.my/senarai-harga** or call 1-800-886-800.

**Step 2:** If confirmed overcharged, photograph the price tag and your receipt.

Note down the business name, item, previous price (if known), and current price.

File at **e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my** → Aduan Antipencatutan.

Describe the price increase and why you believe it is unjustified.

KPDNHEP will assess whether the increase is defensible under cost analysis.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a restaurant charge more than the controlled price for chicken in a dish?**
Price controls on raw ingredients (whole chicken) apply to retail sale of the ingredient itself — not to prepared food dishes. A restaurant can set its own menu prices. However, anti-profiteering provisions still apply if menu prices increase unreasonably without cost justification.

**Q: What if a premium or organic version of a controlled item is priced higher?**
Price controls typically apply to standard grades and formats — premium, organic, or speciality variants may not be controlled. Check the specific gazette order for the item to confirm which grades or formats are controlled.

**Q: Does price control apply to online purchases?**
Yes — price control applies to all sales of controlled goods regardless of channel. An online seller charging above the MRP for controlled goods is equally in violation. Report via eAduan with screenshots of the listing and any receipts.

**Q: What if I'm not sure whether the price I paid is above the controlled price?**
Call the KPDNHEP hotline (1-800-886-800) — they can confirm the current controlled price for the specific item, grade, and state. Keep your receipt until confirmed.

**Q: How are controlled prices set and updated?**
The government sets and updates controlled prices through **gazettes** — official government notices published in the Warta Kerajaan. KPDNHEP implements and communicates these changes. Prices may be revised in response to global commodity prices, subsidy policy changes, or supply conditions.
