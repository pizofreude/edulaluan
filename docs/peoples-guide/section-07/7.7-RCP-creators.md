## About This Page

This page lists Malaysian content creators, official agency channels, and media resources that help the public understand consumer rights, complaint processes, tenancy, price control, and product warranties. This is a living page. If you know a creator or channel that belongs here, use the **"Suggest a resource"** button at the bottom.

**Note:** EduLaluan does not endorse any individual creator's legal views. Consumer rights and legal information from creators should always be verified against KPDNHEP, TTPM, or a qualified legal professional. Be cautious of creators who offer paid "complaint services" — most consumer complaint channels in Malaysia are free.

## Content Gap Notice

As of March 2026, Malaysian consumer rights content is one of the most critically underserved areas across all creator categories — despite being among the most practically impactful for everyday Malaysians:

* **Tenancy rights in plain language** — independent, legally grounded tenancy content for renters is almost entirely absent; what exists comes from property portals with commercial interests or unverified community forums; the absence of a Residential Tenancy Act compounds this gap
* **TTPM walkthrough content** — very few creators have filmed an actual TTPM filing or hearing process; most people have never heard of TTPM despite it being free, accessible, and effective
* **Lemon Law education** — following the Budget 2026 announcement, there is a significant opportunity for creators to explain what the incoming law means, what it covers, and how consumers can prepare; this space is currently almost empty
* **Warranty refusal escalation** — how to respond when a manufacturer or retailer refuses a valid warranty claim is a high-demand topic almost entirely unaddressed by Malaysian creators
* **BM-language legal rights content** — consumer rights content in BM is dominated by KPDNHEP's official channel; independent voices explaining rights in accessible BM are rare, leaving Malay-dominant audiences dependent on official government content only
* **Cross-border and e-commerce consumer rights** — as more Malaysians buy from international platforms (Temu, AliExpress, TikTok Shop), understanding what CPA protections apply to cross-border purchases is increasingly important and completely underserved

**If you create or know of a channel covering any of these gaps, please suggest it below.** EduLaluan will review and add verified resources.
