## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What does KPDNHEP do?**

KPDNHEP (Kementerian Perdagangan Dalam Negeri dan Hal Ehwal Pengguna) is the government ministry that protects consumers and regulates trade in Malaysia.

If a business:

* Overcharges you beyond the controlled price
* Sells you something fake or mislabelled
* Refuses to honour a warranty
* Uses dishonest business practices

KPDNHEP is one place you can report it.

You file a complaint through their **eAduan** system — online or by calling their hotline.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

KPDNHEP oversees a wide range of laws protecting consumers and regulating domestic trade. It is not a single-issue body — its mandate spans price control, product safety, intellectual property enforcement, business licensing compliance, and consumer protection.

For most consumers, KPDNHEP is the first point of contact for:

* Overpricing and price gouging complaints
* Fake, counterfeit, or mislabelled goods
* Unfair trade practices
* Warranty and refund disputes (alongside TTPM)
* Weighing and measuring equipment fraud

For complaints about **financial services** → BNM TELELINK
For complaints about **telcos** → MCMC
For complaints about **food safety** → MOH / local authority
For complaints about **healthcare providers** → KKM

## Part 1: What KPDNHEP Regulates

### Key Laws Under KPDNHEP's Enforcement

| Law | What It Covers |
| --- | --- |
| Consumer Protection Act 1999 | Unfair practices, product safety, false representations |
| Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011 | Controlled goods pricing, profiteering offences |
| Trade Descriptions Act 2011 | False trade descriptions, misleading labelling, fake halal claims |
| Weights and Measures Act 1972 | Accurate measurement in trade (petrol, market produce, etc.) |
| Intellectual Property laws | Counterfeit goods, trademark infringement |
| Direct Sales and Anti-Pyramid Scheme Act 1993 | Pyramid schemes, direct selling regulations |
| Hire Purchase Act 1967 | Hire purchase agreements and consumer rights |
| Moneylenders Act 1951 | Licensed moneylender oversight |

### Types of Complaints KPDNHEP Handles

| Complaint Type | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Price control violation | Charging above controlled price for essential goods |
| Profiteering | Excessive price increases unjustified by cost |
| False trade description | Mislabelled products, fake brand claims |
| Counterfeit goods | Fake branded goods sold as genuine |
| Fake halal certification | Displaying halal logo without JAKIM certification |
| Warranty refusal | Seller refusing to honour valid warranty |
| Pyramid scheme | Recruitment-based income schemes disguised as businesses |
| Weighing fraud | Inaccurate scales at wet markets, petrol stations |
| Unlicensed direct selling | Direct sales companies operating without KPDNHEP licence |

## Part 2: How to File a Complaint via eAduan

### Three Ways to File

| Channel | How | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| eAduan portal | e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my | Detailed complaints with supporting documents |
| Hotline | 1-800-886-800 | Quick enquiries and urgent pricing complaints |
| Walk-in | Nearest KPDNHEP office | Complex cases requiring officer assistance |

### Step-by-Step: Filing via eAduan

```
1. Go to e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my
2. Select complaint category (Harga / Barangan / Pengguna / etc.)
3. Fill in complaint details:
   - Business name and address
   - Date and description of incident
   - Amount involved (if applicable)
4. Upload supporting evidence:
   - Receipt or invoice
   - Photo of price tag or product
   - Screenshot (for online purchases)
5. Submit — you will receive a reference number
6. Track status using your reference number
```

### What Makes a Strong Complaint

| Element | Why It Helps |
| --- | --- |
| Business name and address | Officers need to know who and where to investigate |
| Date of incident | Establishes timeline |
| Receipt or invoice | Proves the transaction occurred and at what price |
| Photos | Visual evidence of overpricing, mislabelling, or fake goods |
| Your contact details | KPDNHEP officers may need to follow up |

Complaints without supporting evidence are harder to act on. Take photos and keep receipts before filing.

## Part 3: What Happens After You File

### Typical Process

```
Day 1–3:    Complaint acknowledged; reference number issued
Day 3–14:   Assigned to KPDNHEP officer for assessment
Week 2–4:   Investigation — officer may contact you for more information
            Business may be visited or audited
Week 4–8:   Outcome communicated — compound, warning, or case closed
Ongoing:    Some cases (counterfeit goods, pyramid schemes) take longer
```

### Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | What It Means |
| --- | --- |
| Compound issued to business | Business fined for the violation |
| Warning issued | First offence — business cautioned |
| Prosecution | Serious or repeat offences escalated to court |
| Case closed (insufficient evidence) | Complaint noted but not actionable without more proof |
| Referral to another agency | Complaint redirected to the appropriate body |

**Important:** KPDNHEP's role is enforcement — it does not directly compensate you as a consumer. For personal refunds or compensation, use **TTPM (Tribunal Tuntutan Pengguna Malaysia)** — see Section 7.3.

## Part 4: What KPDNHEP Cannot Help With

Knowing the boundaries prevents wasted time.

| Issue | Correct Channel |
| --- | --- |
| Bank or insurance complaint | BNM TELELINK (1-300-88-5465) |
| Telco or internet provider complaint | MCMC (aduan.skmm.gov.my) |
| Food safety (restaurant hygiene) | Local authority / MOH district office |
| Healthcare provider complaint | KKM (aduan.moh.gov.my) |
| Employment dispute | Labour Department (JTK) |
| Online marketplace fraud (criminal) | PDRM / CCID |
| Civil debt recovery | Magistrates' Court or TTPM |
| SSM business registration issues | SSM directly |

A quick rule: **KPDNHEP handles goods, trade, and consumer protection. If the issue is about a regulated service (banking, telco, healthcare) it goes to that sector's regulator.**

## Practical Habits for Consumer Protection

Consumers who protect themselves effectively tend to:

* Keep receipts for all significant purchases — digital or physical
* Photograph price tags before purchase, especially for controlled goods
* Check JAKIM's halal directory before assuming a product is halal-certified
* Know that warranty refusals are a KPDNHEP matter — escalate do not just accept it
* Use the eAduan portal for written complaints — it creates a paper trail

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Petrol station overcharge**

A driver noticed her petrol receipt showed a higher per-litre rate than the posted controlled price. She photographed the pump display and the receipt and filed an eAduan complaint. KPDNHEP officers visited the station within two weeks. The station was issued a compound fine.

**Scenario 2: Fake branded goods**

A shopper bought what he believed was a branded sports shoe at a night market. When the sole peeled off after two weeks, he examined the shoe more closely — the branding was misspelled. He reported to KPDNHEP with photos of the product and the stall location. Officers conducted a raid and seized counterfeit stock.

**Scenario 3: Warranty refusal resolved via KPDNHEP**

A consumer's blender broke within the warranty period. The shop refused to repair or replace it. She filed a KPDNHEP complaint citing the Consumer Protection Act 1999. The officer contacted the shop. The shop replaced the blender within the week rather than face a formal investigation.

## Full Wizard Content (Modal / Sidebar)

### Path A: I was overcharged for a controlled item

Take a photo of the price tag and your receipt.

File at: **e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my** → Aduan Harga

Include: business name, address, item, controlled price, price charged, date.

Or call: **1-800-886-800**

---

### Path B: I was sold a fake or counterfeit product

Photograph the product, packaging, and labelling clearly.

Note where and when you bought it.

File at: **e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my** → Aduan Barangan

Include photos as attachments.

---

### Path C: A business refused to honour my warranty

Check your warranty card or receipt for validity period.

Document the refusal in writing if possible (WhatsApp screenshot, email).

File at: **e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my** → Aduan Pengguna

If seeking compensation or refund directly, also consider TTPM (Section 7.3).

## FAQ

**Q: Is filing a KPDNHEP complaint free?**
Yes — filing via eAduan or the hotline is free. Walk-in visits to KPDNHEP offices are also free.

**Q: Will KPDNHEP get my money back for me?**
Not directly. KPDNHEP's role is enforcement — fining or prosecuting businesses that violate the law. For direct refunds or compensation, file a claim with TTPM (Section 7.3) which can order businesses to compensate consumers.

**Q: How long does KPDNHEP take to act on a complaint?**
Acknowledgement is typically within 3 working days. Investigation timelines vary — price complaints may be resolved within 2–4 weeks; counterfeit goods investigations may take longer depending on complexity.

**Q: Can I file a complaint anonymously?**
KPDNHEP accepts tip-offs without full personal details for enforcement purposes. However, formal complaints that may require follow-up or where you want to be kept informed should include your contact details.

**Q: What if KPDNHEP closes my case without action?**
You can follow up using your reference number and request a review. If the issue involves a criminal element (fraud, scam), escalate to PDRM's Commercial Crime Investigation Department (CCID).
