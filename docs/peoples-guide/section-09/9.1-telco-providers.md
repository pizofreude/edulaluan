## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is a telco?**

A telco (telecommunications company) is the company that provides your phone's mobile network — the signal that lets you call, text, and use mobile data.

In Malaysia, the main mobile networks are:

* **Maxis** — large postpaid network; known for coverage
* **CelcomDigi** — merged company (Celcom + Digi); largest subscriber base
* **U Mobile** — competitive pricing; growing network
* **Yes (YTL Communications)** — 4G-first network; strong in selected areas

Your phone works on the network of whichever company's SIM card you use.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia's mobile telecommunications market is served by four licensed mobile network operators (MNOs) plus several mobile virtual network operators (MVNOs) that use the MNOs' infrastructure. All are licensed and regulated by MCMC.

Understanding the landscape helps you choose the right plan, understand your rights, and know where to turn when things go wrong.

### 5G in Malaysia

Malaysia's 5G rollout is managed through a single wholesale network operator model:

* **Digital Nasional Berhad (DNB)** was the original wholesale 5G provider
* Following industry negotiations, a **dual wholesale network** model was approved — with Maxis and CelcomDigi building separate 5G networks alongside DNB
* As of 2025, 5G coverage is concentrated in major urban centres — Klang Valley, Penang, Johor Bahru, and selected corridors

5G availability varies significantly by provider and location. Check each provider's 5G coverage map before selecting a 5G plan.

## Part 2: Postpaid vs Prepaid — How to Choose

### Key Differences

| Feature | Postpaid | Prepaid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Contract | Usually 12–24 months | No contract |
| Payment | Monthly bill after use | Top up before use |
| Data | Typically more generous; may include unlimited | Pay-as-you-go or fixed reload packages |
| Call and SMS | Usually included in plan | Charged per use or bundled add-on |
| Credit check | Required (IC check) | Not required |
| Number stability | High — harder to lose number | Expires if not topped up |
| Device subsidy | Often bundled with device | No device bundling |
| Flexibility | Lower — contract penalties for early termination | High — stop anytime |

### When Postpaid Makes Sense

* You use mobile data heavily and consistently
* You want a device subsidy bundled with the plan
* You want predictable monthly billing
* You have a stable income and can commit to a 12–24 month contract
* You travel frequently and want roaming included

### When Prepaid Makes Sense

* You use mobile data lightly or inconsistently
* You want no long-term commitment
* You are a student or on a variable income
* You want to manage spending precisely
* You are a tourist or short-term visitor to Malaysia

### Prepaid Top-Up Options

Prepaid SIMs can be topped up via:

* Telco's own app
* Online banking (FPX)
* Convenience stores (7-Eleven, KK Mart, myNEWS)
* ATMs
* JomPAY

**Prepaid SIM expiry:** Most prepaid SIMs expire if not used or topped up within a set period (typically 30–180 days depending on the reload amount). Check your provider's validity terms — an expired SIM loses its number.

## Part 3: SIM Registration and Number Porting

### SIM Registration (Mandatory)

All SIM cards in Malaysia — prepaid and postpaid — must be registered to a valid identity document under MCMC regulations.

| Required Document | Who Presents It |
| --- | --- |
| Malaysian MyKad | Citizens and permanent residents |
| Passport | Foreigners |
| Military / Police ID | Uniformed personnel |

Registration links the SIM to your identity. This means:

* A SIM cannot be used anonymously in Malaysia
* Lost or stolen SIMs registered to you can be deactivated by you
* Fraudulent SIM registrations (using someone else's IC) are a criminal offence

**eKYC registration** (registering online via selfie + IC scan) is available with most providers — no physical counter visit needed for new SIM activation.

### Mobile Number Portability (MNP)

You can keep your existing phone number when switching from one provider to another. This is called Mobile Number Portability.

**How to port your number:**

```
Step 1: Obtain a new SIM from your desired new provider
Step 2: Request porting at the new provider's outlet or app
         → Bring your MyKad and current SIM
Step 3: New provider submits the porting request to your old provider
Step 4: Porting completes — typically within 3 working days
Step 5: Your old service is deactivated; number is active on new network
```

**Important before porting:**

* Settle all outstanding bills with your current provider
* If on a postpaid contract, check early termination fees — porting during a contract triggers these
* Porting does not transfer your prepaid balance — use up or forfeit remaining credit

**MCMC's MNP portal:** You can check porting status and file complaints about delayed porting at **mcmc.gov.my/mnp**

## Part 4: Roaming and International Calls

### Outgoing International Calls from Malaysia

Dial: **00 + country code + number**

Common country codes:

| Country | Code |
| --- | --- |
| Singapore | 65 |
| Indonesia | 62 |
| United Kingdom | 44 |
| United States / Canada | 1 |
| Australia | 61 |
| India | 91 |
| China | 86 |

International call rates vary significantly by provider and plan. Check your provider's international call rates before making calls — they can be expensive without an add-on.

### Roaming When Travelling Abroad

All major Malaysian providers offer international roaming — but default roaming rates can be very high.

**Before travelling internationally:**

| Option | How | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Roaming add-on / day pass | Activate via provider app before departure | Most travellers — predictable cost |
| Local SIM at destination | Buy a local prepaid SIM on arrival | Extended stays; heavy data users |
| eSIM | Purchase a destination eSIM digitally | Newer phones with eSIM support; convenient |
| Wi-Fi calling | Use WhatsApp / Telegram / Signal over Wi-Fi | Avoiding roaming entirely when Wi-Fi available |

**Roaming tips:**

* Activate your roaming add-on before leaving Malaysia — airport activation can be unreliable
* Enable "data roaming" in your phone settings only after activating a roaming add-on — default roaming data can be extremely expensive per MB
* Check which countries are included in your add-on — Southeast Asian day passes may not include Japan, Australia, or Europe
* Wi-Fi calling via WhatsApp is free over Wi-Fi and avoids roaming charges entirely for most communication needs

## Part 5: Complaints and Escalation (Brief)

### Step 1: Contact Your Provider First

All providers have dedicated complaints processes:

| Provider | Complaints Channel |
| --- | --- |
| Maxis | 1800-82-1123 / maxis.com.my/contact |
| CelcomDigi | 019-601-1111 / celcomdigi.com/contact |
| U Mobile | 018-388-1318 / u.com.my/contact |
| Yes | 1-800-10-9375 / yes.my/contact |

For billing disputes, service failures, and plan issues — contact your provider's complaints unit first (not general customer service). A reference number should be provided.

### Step 2: Escalate to MCMC If Unresolved

If your provider does not resolve the complaint within a reasonable timeframe (typically 7–14 days):

* File at: **aduan.skmm.gov.my**
* Call: **1-800-18-0300**

## Practical Habits for Telco Consumers

Malaysians who get the best value from their telco tend to:

* Check coverage at home and work before signing a postpaid contract — a good plan on a weak network is a bad deal
* Review their plan annually — telco plans improve regularly and loyalty does not always mean the best current deal
* Activate roaming add-ons before travelling — not at the airport
* Keep prepaid SIMs active with periodic top-ups to avoid expiry
* File formal complaints in writing — not just verbally — so there is a reference trail for MCMC escalation if needed
* Never share their SIM registration details with strangers — SIM swapping fraud starts with IC information

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Coverage checked before committing**

A family moving to a new house in Nilai checked coverage maps for all four providers before porting. They found that their existing provider had poor indoor coverage at the new address while a competitor had strong signal. They ported before moving and avoided months of dropped calls.

**Scenario 2: Prepaid SIM expired during travel**

A student left her prepaid SIM unused for four months while studying abroad. On return, the number had expired. Her bank, workplace, and several apps were registered to that number — recovery was time-consuming. Topping up via the app remotely (RM 5 minimum) would have preserved the number.

**Scenario 3: Early termination fee not anticipated**

A postpaid customer switched providers mid-contract to access a better plan. He was charged an early termination fee of RM 400 — not prominently disclosed in his original agreement. Checking the contract terms, including the early termination fee formula, before signing would have allowed him to plan the timing of the switch.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use a foreign SIM in Malaysia?**
Yes — most foreign SIMs work on Malaysian networks for calls, SMS, and data at international roaming rates set by your home provider. For extended stays, a Malaysian prepaid SIM is more economical.

**Q: What happens to my postpaid number if I don't pay my bill?**
Unpaid postpaid accounts are typically suspended first (incoming calls only), then disconnected. After disconnection, the number may be reallocated after a period. Settle bills promptly to avoid losing your number.

**Q: Is eSIM available in Malaysia?**
Yes — Maxis, CelcomDigi, U Mobile, and Yes all offer eSIM for compatible devices. eSIM allows you to activate a plan digitally without a physical SIM card and is particularly useful for international travel.

**Q: Do MVNOs offer reliable service?**
MVNOs use MNO infrastructure, so network quality is the same as the underlying MNO. The difference is plan pricing, customer service quality, and features — which vary by MVNO. Yoodo and Unifi Mobile are among the better-regarded MVNOs in Malaysia.

**Q: Is 5G worth paying for in Malaysia?**
Depends on your location and device. If you are in a 5G coverage area with a 5G-capable phone, speeds are meaningfully faster for data-intensive tasks. Outside coverage areas, your device falls back to 4G. Check your provider's 5G map for your specific area before paying a 5G premium.
