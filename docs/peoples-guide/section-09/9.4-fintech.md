## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is an e-wallet?**

An e-wallet is a digital app that stores money — like a virtual purse on your phone.

You can use it to:

* Pay at shops by scanning a QR code
* Transfer money to friends
* Pay for food delivery, ride-hailing, and online shopping
* Collect cashback and rewards

Popular Malaysian e-wallets: **Touch 'n Go (TNG), GrabPay, Boost, MAE (Maybank), ShopeePay**

All legitimate e-wallets in Malaysia are licensed by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM).

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia's digital payments ecosystem is one of the most developed in Southeast Asia. The combination of BNM-licensed e-wallets, the national DuitNow payment infrastructure, FPX online banking, and a growing merchant payment gateway ecosystem has moved Malaysia substantially toward a cashless society.

This page covers both the **consumer side** (e-wallets for everyday use) and the **merchant/business side** (payment gateways for accepting online payments) — two parts of the same ecosystem that most guides treat separately.

### All Are BNM-Licensed

All e-money issuers operating in Malaysia must be licensed by BNM under the Financial Services Act 2013. You can verify any e-wallet's licensing status at **bnm.gov.my/financial-consumer-alert**.

Unlicensed payment apps are not permitted to legally operate in Malaysia — if you encounter an app claiming to be a Malaysian e-wallet without BNM licensing, treat it as a scam risk.

## Part 2: DuitNow — Malaysia's National Payment Infrastructure

### What DuitNow Is

DuitNow is not an e-wallet — it is the **national payment infrastructure** operated by **PayNet** (Payments Network Malaysia) under BNM. It comprises two key services:

| Service | What It Does |
| --- | --- |
| DuitNow Transfer | Instant interbank money transfer using phone number, IC number, or account number as proxy |
| DuitNow QR | Universal QR code standard — any DuitNow-enabled app or banking app can scan it |

### Why DuitNow Matters

DuitNow interoperability means:

* You can transfer money from your TNG eWallet to someone's Maybank account using their phone number
* A merchant with one DuitNow QR accepts payment from any Malaysian banking app or e-wallet
* You do not need to use the same e-wallet as the person you are paying

**DuitNow is replacing FPX** as the primary online bank transfer rail. As of 2025, DuitNow Online Banking/Wallets (DOBW) is increasingly the backend for online payments that previously used FPX.

### FPX (Financial Process Exchange)

FPX has been the standard for online banking payments in Malaysia since 2000 — enabling direct bank account debit for online purchases. While being progressively succeeded by DuitNow, FPX remains widely used across merchant payment systems.

## Part 3: Business Payment Gateways

Payment gateways are the infrastructure that allows businesses to accept online payments. They connect merchants to banks, e-wallets, and card networks.

### How a Payment Gateway Works

```
Customer selects payment → Gateway encrypts and routes transaction → Bank / e-wallet / card network verifies → Funds transferred to merchant → Settlement to merchant's bank account (T+1 to T+3 typically)
```

## Part 4: Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) in Malaysia

BNPL services allow consumers to split purchases into instalments — typically interest-free for short periods.

### Licensed BNPL Providers in Malaysia

| Provider | Operator | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Grab PayLater | Grab Financial | Integrated in Grab app; repay monthly |
| SPayLater | Shopee / Sea Group | Strong for Shopee purchases |
| Atome | Advance Intelligence Group | Widely accepted at online and physical merchants |
| Hoolah (now Pace) | Pace Enterprise | Installment payments at partner merchants |
| myIOU | Malaysian-owned | Local BNPL provider |

**BNM regulatory note:** BNM and the Consumer Credit Bill 2025 are moving toward tighter regulation of BNPL providers. The Consumer Credit Commission, once established, will oversee non-bank consumer credit including BNPL. Monitor BNM updates for current regulatory status.

### BNPL Caution

BNPL can cause debt accumulation if mismanaged — particularly for younger users without a credit history. The absence of interest does not mean absence of cost: late fees apply if instalments are missed, and missed payments are increasingly being reported to credit bureaus (CCRIS/CTOS).

## Part 5: E-Wallet and Fintech Security

### Common E-Wallet Fraud Types

| Fraud Type | How It Happens |
| --- | --- |
| Phishing | Fake e-wallet login pages steal credentials |
| Social engineering OTP scam | Scammer convinces you to share OTP "for verification" |
| Fake merchant QR | Scammer replaces a merchant's QR with their own — payment goes to scammer |
| SIM swap + account takeover | Scammer ports your number to receive your OTP |
| Fake e-wallet app | Lookalike app harvests login credentials |

### Security Best Practices

| Practice | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Enable biometric lock on e-wallet apps | Prevents access if phone is lost or stolen |
| Never share OTP with anyone — including callers claiming to be from TNG/GrabPay/Boost | Legitimate e-wallets never ask for OTP via phone |
| Verify merchant QR before scanning | Check that the QR code sticker is not covering another QR |
| Download only from official App Store / Google Play | Fake apps steal credentials |
| Monitor transaction notifications | Enable push notifications — real-time alerts catch fraud quickly |
| Keep a low balance in e-wallets | Load what you need; keep excess in bank |

## Practical Habits for Digital Payment Users

Malaysians who use e-wallets safely and effectively tend to:

* Keep TNG eWallet topped up for daily use (transport, QR payments) while keeping large balances in bank accounts
* Use DuitNow Transfer for person-to-person payments — faster and free vs bank transfer fees
* Enable all transaction notifications on every e-wallet and banking app
* Verify merchant QR stickers — particularly at markets and smaller outlets where sticker fraud has been reported
* Know that BNPL is a financial product — treat it with the same discipline as a credit card

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Fake merchant QR at a pasar malam**

A consumer scanned a QR code at a stall and transferred RM 50 for food. She later noticed the merchant had not received her payment — a scammer had placed a fake QR sticker over the merchant's original QR. She reported to her e-wallet provider and PDRM. The merchant's original QR was underneath the sticker. Lifting a QR sticker before scanning would have exposed the fraud immediately.

**Scenario 2: ToyyibPay for a community fundraiser**

A community welfare committee needed to collect donations online. They registered for ToyyibPay — no SSM registration required, Shariah-compliant, no setup fee. They created payment links and shared via WhatsApp. Donations were collected digitally within 24 hours of setup. The committee was not a registered business and would not have qualified for most payment gateways.

**Scenario 3: BNPL missed payment, credit score affected**

A 23-year-old used SPayLater for several purchases across three months without tracking his total instalments. When employment income dipped, he missed two payments. Late fees accumulated and the provider reported the arrears to CTOS. His credit score was affected — making his first car loan application more difficult. Understanding that BNPL affects credit history before using it would have changed his approach.

## FAQ

**Q: Is TNG eWallet the same as the Touch 'n Go card used on highways?**
They are related but separate. The Touch 'n Go RFID card is a physical prepaid card for tolls and some transit. The TNG eWallet is a separate digital app with its own balance. You can reload your RFID tag through the TNG eWallet app, but the two balances are separate.

**Q: Is my money in an e-wallet protected like a bank deposit?**
No — e-wallet balances are not protected by PIDM (Perbadanan Insurans Deposit Malaysia), which protects bank deposits up to RM 250,000. E-wallets are licensed e-money instruments under BNM regulation, but they do not carry the same depositor protection as bank accounts. Keep large balances in licensed banks.

**Q: What is the maximum balance allowed in a Malaysian e-wallet?**
BNM regulations cap e-wallet balances for standard (non-verified) accounts at RM 1,500 and for verified (eKYC completed) accounts at RM 9,999. These limits are set to manage risk for unlicensed deposit-taking.

**Q: Can I use Malaysian e-wallets abroad?**
Some e-wallets have international acceptance — TNG eWallet works in Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, and selected ASEAN markets for QR payments. BigPay has a Visa card accepted globally. GrabPay works in Grab-operating countries. Check your specific wallet's international coverage.

**Q: What is the difference between FPX and DuitNow?**
Both are bank transfer payment methods but use different infrastructure. FPX redirects you to your bank's login page to authorise a payment. DuitNow uses account proxies (phone number, IC number, account number) for transfers and QR for merchant payments. DuitNow is the newer standard replacing FPX progressively for online payments.
