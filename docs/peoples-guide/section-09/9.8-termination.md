```

```

## 9.8.1 Telco Termination and Complaints

### When This Applies

Use this pathway for:

* Mobile network service quality (dropped calls, poor signal)
* Broadband service failures (persistent outages, slow speeds)
* Billing disputes (incorrect charges, unexplained fees)
* Contract disputes (early termination fees, plan changes)
* Number porting delays or failures
* Unsolicited charges (SMS subscriptions, data add-ons you did not request)

### Step 1: File with Your Provider

All telcos must have a dedicated complaints unit — not just general customer service. Complaints filed with the dedicated unit carry more accountability.

| Provider | Complaints Contact |
| --- | --- |
| Maxis | 1800-82-1123 / maxis.com.my/contact-us |
| CelcomDigi | 019-601-1111 / celcomdigi.com/contact |
| U Mobile | 018-388-1318 / u.com.my/contact |
| Yes (YTL) | 1-800-10-9375 / yes.my/contact |
| Unifi (TM) | 100 / unifi.com.my |
| TIME | 1800-88-8637 / time.com.my |
| Maxis Home | 1800-82-1123 |
| Astro | 1-300-82-3838 |
| CelcomDigi Home | 019-601-1111 |
| Allo | 1800-22-2556 |

**What to do at this step:**

* File in writing (email or in-app complaint form) — not just a phone call
* Note the date, time, and case reference number
* State clearly what you want: refund, service fix, fee reversal
* Give the provider a reasonable resolution period — 7–14 working days

### Step 2: Escalate to MCMC

If your provider does not resolve within the stated period:

| Channel | Contact | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MCMC eAduan | aduan.skmm.gov.my | Primary escalation portal — submit with provider reference number |
| MCMC Hotline | 1-800-18-0300 | For urgent matters or guidance |
| Email | aduan@mcmc.gov.my | For written escalation |

**When escalating to MCMC:**

* Include your provider's case reference number
* Attach all correspondence with the provider
* State the dates of all contacts with the provider
* Describe what resolution you are seeking

MCMC contacts the provider on your behalf. Most providers resolve complaints promptly once MCMC is involved.

### Terminating a Telco Contract

If you want to cancel your service:

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Check your contract for the notice period (typically 30 days) and early termination fee (ETF) |
| 2 | Submit termination in writing — letter or email to the provider |
| 3 | CC MCMC (aduan@mcmc.gov.my) on your termination notice for disputed ETFs |
| 4 | Return equipment (routers, set-top boxes) to avoid additional charges |
| 5 | Confirm service termination date in writing — get confirmation from provider |

**Important:** Do not assume cancellation is complete until you receive written confirmation. Providers have been known to continue billing after verbal cancellation.

## 9.8.2 Financial Services Termination and Complaints

### When This Applies

Use this pathway for:

* Bank charges you did not authorise
* Insurance claim denials
* E-wallet disputes (unauthorised transactions, failed refunds)
* Investment product mis-selling
* Loan terms disputes
* Insurance agent misconduct
* Unfair financial service practices

### Step 1: File with Your Institution's Complaints Unit

Under BNM regulation, all financial institutions must maintain a dedicated complaints unit — separate from regular customer service.

| Institution Type | How to Find Their Complaints Unit |
| --- | --- |
| Banks | Check the bank's website under "Contact Us" or "Customer Service" — look for "Complaints" or "Feedback" specifically |
| Insurance companies | Policy documents include the insurer's complaints contact; also on their website |
| E-wallets | In-app support → Disputes; or provider's website |
| Investment firms | SC-licensed firms must publish complaints procedures |

**What to include:**

* Account or policy number
* Description of the issue
* Date(s) of the disputed transaction or event
* What resolution you are seeking
* Supporting documents (statements, receipts, policy documents)

### Step 2: Escalate to BNM TELELINK or OFS

If the institution's complaints unit does not resolve within a reasonable period (typically 14 working days):

| Channel | When to Use | Contact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| BNM TELELINK | General financial service complaints; systemic issues; guidance | bnmtelelink@bnm.gov.my / 1-300-88-5465 |
| Ombudsman for Financial Services (OFS) | Disputes with banks, insurance companies, takaful operators — for compensation | ofs.org.my |

**OFS — Key Details:**

* Independent dispute resolution for consumers
* Free service — no cost to the complainant
* Can award compensation up to **RM 250,000** for banking disputes; **RM 10,000** for insurance/takaful
* Decisions are binding on the financial institution (not on the consumer — you can still go to court)
* Eligibility: Individual consumers and small businesses (turnover below RM 5 million)

**Insurance agent misconduct:**

| Issue | Escalate To |
| --- | --- |
| General insurance agent misconduct | PIAM (General Insurance Association of Malaysia) — piam.org.my |
| Life insurance agent misconduct | LIAM (Life Insurance Association of Malaysia) — liam.org.my |
| Takaful operator agent | MTA (Malaysian Takaful Association) — malaysiantakaful.com.my |

### Closing a Financial Account

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Submit account closure request in writing to the institution |
| 2 | Settle all outstanding balances, loans, or pending transactions |
| 3 | Return physical cards and cheque books |
| 4 | Update auto-debit instructions linked to the account |
| 5 | Confirm closure in writing — keep the closure confirmation |

## 9.8.3 E-Commerce and Consumer Service Escalation

### When This Applies

Use this pathway for:

* Defective goods or services from online or physical sellers
* Sellers not delivering after payment
* Products that do not match the description
* Warranty refusals
* Overpricing of controlled goods
* Counterfeit goods
* Unfair contract terms

### Step 1: Platform Dispute (for Online Purchases)

For purchases on Shopee, Lazada, or TikTok Shop:

| Action | How |
| --- | --- |
| Raise dispute | In-app before auto-confirm deadline — My Purchases → Raise Dispute |
| Return request | Follow platform's return window (typically 7–15 days) |
| Appeal | If platform rules against you, request a review with additional evidence |

**For Mudah and Carousell direct transfers:**

No platform escrow exists — file directly with PDRM CCID if fraud is suspected.

### Step 2: KPDNHEP eAduan

For consumer protection violations by businesses — whether online or physical:

| Channel | Contact | What to Include |
| --- | --- | --- |
| eAduan | e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my | Business name, address, incident description, evidence (photos, receipts) |
| Hotline | 1-800-886-800 | For urgent matters or guidance |
| Email | e-aduan@kpdn.gov.my | Written escalation with attachments |

KPDNHEP enforces the Consumer Protection Act 1999, Trade Descriptions Act 2011, and Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011.

### Step 3: TTPM — Consumer Claims Tribunal

For compensation claims up to RM 50,000:

| Feature | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Filing fee | RM 5 (under RM 10,000) / RM 10 (RM 10,001–RM 50,000) |
| No lawyer | Both parties self-represent |
| Timeline | Hearing typically within 30–60 days |
| Outcome | Legally binding award |
| Filing | ttpm.gov.my or walk-in to TTPM office |

**What TTPM can award:**

* Refund of purchase price
* Replacement
* Compensation for consequential loss
* Repair at seller's cost

**What TTPM cannot handle:**

* Claims above RM 50,000 → civil court
* Financial services disputes → OFS or civil court
* Criminal fraud → PDRM

### For Fraud (Criminal)

If a seller has taken payment and disappeared, or the transaction is clearly fraudulent:

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Call your bank immediately to attempt fund recall |
| 2 | Call NSRC: **997** |
| 3 | File at PDRM CCID: **ccid.rmp.gov.my** |
| 4 | Report the seller's account at **semak.my** |

Cross-reference: **Section 8.1 — Scam Prevention and Semak Mule**

## The Complaint Filing Principles

Effective complaints share these qualities regardless of sector:

```
1. In writing — WhatsApp, email, or online form creates a record
2. Reference number — get one; use it in every follow-up
3. Specific — exact dates, amounts, account numbers
4. Evidence — receipts, screenshots, photos, statements
5. Clear ask — state exactly what resolution you want
6. Deadline — give the provider a specific response deadline
7. Escalation path — know your next step before you need it
```

---

## Practical Habits for Consumer Complaints

Consumers who successfully resolve complaints tend to:

* File complaints in writing from the first contact — not verbally
* Escalate promptly if not resolved — don't wait months before going to MCMC or BNM
* Keep all reference numbers in a single document or note
* CC the relevant regulator when sending a formal termination notice — it signals seriousness
* Know which forum is right for which complaint — TTPM for compensation, MCMC for telco enforcement, OFS for financial services
* Never accept "we'll look into it" without a reference number and timeline

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Telco billing dispute escalated correctly**

A subscriber was charged RM 38 per month for a data add-on she did not activate. Three calls to customer service produced promises but no reversal. She filed a written complaint to the provider's complaints unit with a 14-day deadline. When unresolved, she filed an MCMC eAduan including her case reference number. MCMC contacted the provider directly. The charge was reversed and credited within five working days.

**Scenario 2: Insurance claim denial reversed via OFS**

A car insurance policyholder's claim was denied on grounds she considered spurious. The insurer's complaints unit upheld the denial. She filed with OFS — providing her policy, the denial letter, and photos of the damage. OFS reviewed independently and ruled in her favour. The insurer was required to pay the claim. The entire process took 45 days. She paid nothing — OFS is free.

**Scenario 3: E-commerce fraud recovered via bank recall**

A buyer on Carousell paid RM 1,800 via bank transfer for a laptop. The seller became unresponsive immediately after payment. Within 20 minutes, she called her bank, reported the fraudulent transfer, and requested a recall. She simultaneously called NSRC (997) and filed at CCID. The bank placed a hold on the receiving account — the funds were still there. RM 1,500 was recovered within five days. The 20-minute window was critical.

**Step 1:** File with the institution's complaints unit in writing.

**Step 2:** Give 14 working days.

**Step 3:** If unresolved → contact **BNM TELELINK** (1-300-88-5465 / bnmtelelink@bnm.gov.my).

**Step 4:** For compensation → file with **OFS** (ofs.org.my) — free, no lawyer.

**Step 1:** Call your bank immediately — request transaction recall.

**Step 2:** Call NSRC: **997**

**Step 3:** File at **ccid.rmp.gov.my**

**Step 4:** Report the account at **semak.my**

**Step 5:** For compensation after recovery → file at **TTPM** (ttpm.gov.my)
