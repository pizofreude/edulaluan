### 🧒 Simple

**What is e-commerce?**

E-commerce means buying and selling things online.

In Malaysia, the biggest platforms are:

* **Shopee** — largest by users; everything from electronics to groceries
* **Lazada** — strong for branded products and LazMall
* **TikTok Shop** — social commerce; buying directly from TikTok videos
* **Mudah** — classifieds; mostly second-hand and large items
* **Carousell** — peer-to-peer; fashion, collectibles, second-hand

When you buy on Shopee or Lazada:
* Your payment is held by the platform
* It is only released to the seller when you confirm you received your order
* If something goes wrong, you can raise a dispute

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia's e-commerce market is one of Southeast Asia's most active. Shopee and Lazada have dominated since the mid-2010s, but the landscape has shifted significantly with TikTok Shop's rapid growth and the emergence of cross-border platforms like Temu.

This page covers the major platforms from both buyer and seller perspectives — what each is best for, how protections work, and how to navigate disputes.

## Part 2: Buyer Protections and Dispute Resolution

### How Platform Escrow Works

For Shopee and Lazada (and TikTok Shop):

```
Buyer pays → Platform holds payment in escrow
             → Seller ships order
             → Buyer receives order → Confirms receipt
             → Platform releases payment to seller

If buyer does not confirm within auto-confirm period (typically 3–7 days):
             → Payment auto-releases to seller unless dispute is raised
```

**Key action:** Raise a dispute BEFORE the auto-confirm deadline if there is a problem. Once payment auto-releases, recovery is significantly harder.

### Dispute Process by Platform

| Platform | Dispute Window | How to Raise | Escalation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shopee | Before auto-confirm; return window varies (3–15 days) | In-app: My Purchases → Raise Dispute | Shopee mediates; final decision via Shopee resolution |
| Lazada | Within return policy window (typically 7–15 days) | In-app: My Orders → Return/Refund | Lazada mediates; escalate to Lazada Guarantee team |
| TikTok Shop | Within order protection window | In-app: Orders → Request Return/Refund | TikTok Shop mediates |
| Mudah | No platform dispute — direct transaction | Contact seller directly | No platform escalation — go to PDRM if fraud |
| Carousell | CarouSell managed transactions only | In-app dispute | Carousell mediates for enrolled transactions only |

### Escalating Beyond the Platform

If a platform dispute is unresolved:

| Escalation | When to Use |
| --- | --- |
| KPDNHEP eAduan (e-aduan.kpdn.gov.my) | Consumer protection violation by seller |
| TTPM (ttpm.gov.my) | Compensation claim up to RM 50,000 |
| PDRM CCID (ccid.rmp.gov.my) | Criminal fraud — seller disappears with payment |

## Part 4: Cross-Border Shopping — Temu, AliExpress, and Import Considerations

### The Cross-Border Platforms

| Platform | Origin | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Temu | PDD Holdings (China) | Aggressive pricing; direct-from-factory model; very low prices; concerns about quality and data practices |
| AliExpress | Alibaba (China) | Established; wide product range; Malaysian-focused shipping options |
| Amazon | USA | Available to Malaysia via global shipping; not the same as a local Amazon MY |
| ASOS | UK | Fashion-focused; ships to Malaysia |
| Taobao | Alibaba (China) | Chinese-language platform; requires a forwarding agent for most Malaysian buyers |

### Key Differences from Local Platforms

| Factor | Local Platforms (Shopee, Lazada) | Cross-Border Platforms (Temu, AliExpress) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer protection | Strong — platform holds escrow; local dispute resolution | Weaker — disputes handled by foreign platform; CPA may not apply |
| Delivery time | 1–7 days typically | 2–6 weeks typically |
| Return process | Relatively accessible | More complex; return shipping often not covered |
| Customs duty | Usually included in price (platform handles) | May attract import duties above RM 500 threshold |
| SIRIM certification | Sellers on local platforms should comply | Cross-border items may not meet Malaysian safety standards |
| Warranty | Local after-sales support may not apply | Typically no local warranty |

### Import Duty Considerations

Malaysia levies customs duty on imported goods above a certain threshold:

* **De minimis threshold:** Goods below RM 500 in value are typically exempt from import duty (check current Royal Malaysian Customs regulations as this threshold has been under review)
* Above RM 500: Standard customs duty and SST rates apply depending on product category
* **Note:** The de minimis threshold has been a subject of policy debate — the government has signalled potential reduction to support local e-commerce. Check current rates at **customs.gov.my** before ordering high-value items from overseas.

### Temu-Specific Considerations

Temu has grown rapidly in Malaysia since its 2023 entry. Key considerations for Malaysian consumers:

* Prices are extremely low — often due to direct factory-to-consumer model and subsidised shipping
* Quality is highly variable — reviews and photos from other buyers are important
* Returns: Temu's return policy is stated as relatively generous, but international return logistics can be complex
* Data privacy: Temu's data practices have been scrutinised in multiple markets — be mindful of the data you share
* Product safety: Items may not carry SIRIM certification — particularly relevant for electrical items

## Part 5: Getting Started Selling on Malaysian Platforms

### For New Sellers — Checklist

```
✅ 1. Register SSM (for professional selling) — Section 6.1
✅ 2. Create seller account on chosen platform(s)
✅ 3. Set up a payment account (ShopeePay, Lazada Wallet, or bank account)
✅ 4. Prepare product listings — clear photos, accurate descriptions, correct categories
✅ 5. Set competitive pricing — check similar listings
✅ 6. Configure shipping — use platform logistics or self-arrange
✅ 7. Respond to enquiries promptly — response rate affects ranking
✅ 8. Join platform seller education programmes (Shopee University, Lazada University)
```

### Common Seller Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
| --- | --- |
| Inaccurate product descriptions | High return rates; negative reviews; account warning |
| Late shipping | Low seller rating; delisted products |
| Ignoring chat enquiries | Lower conversion; platform penalisation |
| Overpricing relative to competitors | Lost sales to similar listings |
| Not joining platform campaigns (11.11, 12.12) | Missing peak traffic periods |
| Operating without SSM when required | Risk of account suspension for official stores |

## Practical Habits for E-Commerce Consumers

Buyers who have good e-commerce experiences tend to:

* Shop from Mall / LazMall / verified stores for high-value or brand-sensitive purchases
* Check seller ratings and review count before buying from non-mall sellers
* Take screenshots of product listings before purchasing — proof if description does not match delivery
* Raise disputes BEFORE the auto-confirm deadline — not after
* Use Semak Mule (semakmule.rmp.gov.my) to check seller bank accounts before direct transfers on Mudah or Carousell
* Be cautious of Mudah and Carousell transactions involving overseas payment or unusual delivery requests

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Dispute raised before auto-confirm saved the purchase**

A buyer received a laptop bag on Shopee that was clearly a different colour from the listing. She raised a dispute in-app before the 3-day auto-confirm window closed — attaching photos of the listing and the received item. Shopee ruled in her favour and refunded in full. Had she waited until after auto-confirm, the process would have been significantly harder.

**Scenario 2: Mudah scam, no platform protection**

A buyer paid RM 2,200 via bank transfer for a second-hand laptop on Mudah. The seller disappeared after receiving payment. Unlike Shopee or Lazada, Mudah has no escrow — the buyer had transferred directly. He reported to PDRM CCID. The bank account was checked on Semak Mule after the fact — it had existing scam reports. Checking Semak Mule before transferring would have flagged this.

**Scenario 3: TikTok Shop product quality issue**

A consumer bought skincare products featured in a TikTok Live session. The products arrived but smelled unusual and the packaging seemed inconsistent with the brand's official website. She filed a return request within the order protection window and received a refund. She also reported the seller to TikTok Shop's trust and safety team. Buying from verified TikTok Shop Mall stores reduces this risk.

## FAQ

**Q: Is TikTok Shop safe to buy from?**
TikTok Shop has buyer protection for transactions made within the app. Buying from TikTok Shop Mall (verified brand stores) is safer than individual sellers. The platform is newer and its protection mechanisms are less battle-tested than Shopee or Lazada — exercise more caution for high-value purchases.

**Q: Can I sell on Shopee without SSM registration?**
Individual sellers can open a basic Shopee account without SSM. However, SSM registration is required for Shopee Mall status, accessing higher monthly withdrawal limits, and operating as a legitimate business. For more than occasional selling, SSM registration is strongly recommended — and legally required if you are running a business.

**Q: What is the difference between Shopee Mall and regular Shopee sellers?**
Shopee Mall sellers are verified businesses (SSM registered) who have agreed to Shopee's brand guidelines and stricter quality standards. They typically offer better return policies (15-day returns vs 7-day for non-Mall) and faster dispute resolution. The "Shopee Mall" badge is a meaningful quality signal.

**Q: Does the Consumer Protection Act apply to cross-border purchases from Temu or AliExpress?**
The CPA applies to transactions with businesses operating in Malaysia. Foreign platforms operating cross-border are in a grey area — they are increasingly expected to comply with local consumer laws for goods sold to Malaysian consumers, but enforcement is difficult. Platform-level buyer protection is your primary recourse for cross-border purchases.

**Q: Are prices on Temu genuinely that cheap — or is there a catch?**
Temu's prices are genuinely low — enabled by a direct-from-factory model, subsidised shipping, and lower seller margins. The trade-offs are: variable quality, longer delivery times, potential data privacy concerns, and weaker local buyer protection. For non-electrical, non-safety-critical items where price matters most, it may be reasonable. For electrical devices or items requiring Malaysian safety certification, buy locally.
