## For Kids 👦👧

### Screens Are Useful — But Your Brain and Body Need Balance

Phones, tablets, computers, and TVs are amazing tools.

You can:

* Learn new things 📚
* Talk to friends 💬
* Watch videos 🎬
* Play games 🎮
* Do schoolwork 🏫

But just like eating too much candy is not healthy, **too much screen time is not healthy either.**

Your brain and body need different kinds of activities to grow strong.

Think of your day like a balanced meal:

* 🍎 Learning
* 🏃 Playing
* 😴 Sleeping
* 👨‍👩‍👧 Family time
* 📱 Some screen time

Too much of any one thing is not good — even technology.

---

### What Happens If You Use Screens Too Much?

Too much screen time can cause:

| Problem             | Why It Happens                                         |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| 😴 Trouble sleeping | Blue light from screens tells your brain to stay awake |
| 👀 Eye strain       | Looking at screens too long tires your eyes            |
| 🪑 Less movement    | Sitting too long is not healthy for growing bodies     |
| 😡 Mood changes     | Too much gaming or social media can affect emotions    |
| 🧠 Less focus       | Fast entertainment can make schoolwork feel harder     |

Technology itself is not bad.

**The goal is to use it wisely.**

---

### How Much Screen Time Is Healthy?

There is no single rule for every child, but a good simple guide is:

```
📚 Schoolwork → allowed when needed
🎮 Fun screen time → usually 1–2 hours per day
😴 No screens → 1 hour before bedtime
🏃 Outdoor play → every day if possible
```

Your parents may set different rules — and that is okay.

Rules help protect your health.

---

### 5 Healthy Tech Habits You Can Start Today

### 1️⃣ Take Eye Breaks (20-20-20 Rule)

Every 20 minutes:

Look at something about 20 feet away
For at least 20 seconds.

This helps your eyes relax.

You can remember it like this:

```
👀 20 minutes
🌳 Look far away
⏱️ 20 seconds
```

---

### 2️⃣ Do Not Use Screens Before Sleep

Try to stop screens **at least 1 hour before bedtime.**

Instead you can:

* Read a book 📖
* Talk to your family 👨‍👩‍👧
* Prepare your school bag 🎒
* Draw or journal ✏️

Your brain sleeps better without screens before bed.

---

### 3️⃣ Move Your Body Every Day

If you spend time on screens, make sure you also:

* Run
* Cycle
* Play sports
* Walk outside
* Help with simple chores

A simple rule:

```
For every long screen session → move your body after.
```

Even 10–15 minutes helps.

---

### 4️⃣ Keep Devices Out of the Bedroom at Night

If possible:

* Charge phones outside the bedroom
* Do not sleep with tablets
* Use alarm clocks instead of phones

Why?

Because notifications and games make it harder to sleep well.

Good sleep helps you:

* Grow taller
* Think better
* Feel happier
* Stay healthy

---

### 5️⃣ Ask: Is This Helping Me or Just Passing Time?

Before using a device, ask yourself:

**"Am I using this to learn or just because I'm bored?"**

Good uses:

* Homework
* Learning videos
* Talking to family
* Creative apps

Okay in moderation:

* Games
* Entertainment videos

Try not to scroll endlessly without purpose.

You are the boss of your technology — not the other way around.

---

### Red Flags 🚩 — Time to Take a Break

You should pause screen time if you notice:

* Your eyes hurt
* You feel angry after gaming
* You skip meals
* You stop playing outside
* You hide your screen from parents
* You feel upset when told to stop

Taking breaks is a sign of strength — not weakness.

---

### A Simple Daily Balance Example

Here is what a healthy day could look like:

```
🏫 School and homework
🏃 Play outside
🍽️ Family meals
📱 Some screen fun
🛁 Night routine
😴 Sleep
```

Screens are just **one part** of your day — not the whole day.

---

### Quick Activity — My Tech Balance Check ✅

Try this:

Write down yesterday:

1. How many hours did I use screens?
2. Did I play outside?
3. Did I sleep well?
4. Did I take breaks?

Ask yourself:

**What is one small improvement I can make tomorrow?**

Small improvements matter more than perfect rules.

## For Parents — A Note 👨‍👩‍👧

This page introduces **digital self-regulation**, not just restriction. The goal is to help children develop internal judgement about technology use rather than relying only on enforcement.

Research consistently shows that outcomes depend less on raw screen hours and more on:

* Content quality
* Whether usage is passive or active
* Sleep disruption
* Physical activity levels
* Parent modelling behaviour

**Practical family rules that work well:**

* No devices during meals
* No screens 1 hour before bed
* Devices charge outside bedrooms
* Family tech agreements rather than punishments
* Parents modelling the same habits

**Useful conversation starters:**

* "What do you enjoy most online?"
* "How do you feel after gaming for a long time?"
* "What makes a video worth your time?"
* "What is a good stopping point?"

**Practical digital resilience skills to teach:**

* Recognising addictive design (autoplay, endless scroll)
* Taking voluntary breaks
* Choosing educational over passive content
* Understanding online persuasion and advertising (connects to Section 8)

## Family Tech Agreement Idea 📜

Consider creating simple household rules like:

```
📱 No phones at meals
😴 No devices after 9pm
🏫 Homework before games

💬 Talk if something online feels uncomfortable
```

Children follow rules more easily when they help create them.
