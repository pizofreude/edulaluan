## For Kids 👦👧

### The Most Important Numbers You Should Know

If something very serious happens — like a fire, someone gets badly hurt, or you are in danger — you need to call for help.

In Malaysia, these are the numbers that get help to you fast:

---

### 🚨 999 — The Emergency Number

**999** is the most important number.

Call **999** when:

* There is a **fire** 🔥
* Someone is **badly hurt** or not breathing 🏥
* Someone is in **danger** right now
* You need the **police** urgently 🚔

When you call 999, a person will answer and ask you what happened.

Tell them:
1. **What happened** — "There is a fire" / "Someone is hurt"
2. **Where you are** — your address, building name, or nearby landmark
3. **Your name** — so they know who called

**Stay on the phone** until they tell you it is okay to hang up.

---

### 🚒 994 — BOMBA (Fire and Rescue)

**994** is the number for BOMBA — the fire and rescue team.

Call **994** when:

* There is a **fire**
* Someone is **trapped** (in a car, building, or flooded area)
* There has been an accident and someone needs to be rescued

BOMBA also helps during floods and landslides.

---

### 🚑 999 or 112 — Ambulance

If someone is sick or injured and needs to go to hospital fast, call **999** and ask for an ambulance.

You can also call **112** — this is a special number that works even when your phone has **no credit or signal**. 

Use **112** when:
* Your phone credit has run out
* You have very weak signal
* You need help urgently

---

### 🚔 999 or 03-2266 2222 — Police (PDRM)

Call **999** for urgent police help — like if someone is being hurt or is in danger right now.

For non-urgent police matters (not an emergency), you can call the nearest police station or visit in person.

---

### ☎️ Numbers to Remember

| Who | Number | When to Call |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Emergency (all) | **999** | Fire, injury, danger, crime happening now |
| Emergency (no credit/signal) | **112** | Works without credit |
| BOMBA (fire and rescue) | **994** | Fire, trapped, floods |
| Poison hotline | **03-2260 9025** | If someone swallowed something dangerous |
| Befrienders (for adults who are very sad) | **03-7627 2929** | For a grown-up who needs someone to talk to |

---

### How to Call for Help — Practice This!

Imagine you are in your house and you smell smoke. Here is what to do:

```
Step 1: GET OUT of the building first — do not go looking for the fire

Step 2: When you are safe, call 999

Step 3: Say: "There is a fire at [your address]"

Step 4: Give your name and stay on the line

Step 5: Wait outside for BOMBA to arrive
```

**Never go back into a burning building — even for pets or belongings.**

---

### What If Your Phone Has No Credit?

**Dial 112.** 

112 works on any phone, even:
* With no credit
* With very weak signal
* On someone else's phone
* On a phone without a SIM card

---

### When NOT to Call 999

999 is for real emergencies only. Calling 999 for non-emergencies means real emergencies might get slower help.

Do **not** call 999 for:
* Asking for directions
* A stray animal (unless it is dangerous)
* Minor cuts or bruises that are not serious
* To test if it works

If you accidentally call 999, **do not hang up** — stay on the line and tell the operator it was an accident.

### A Story to Help You Remember

**Aiman's Apartment**

One afternoon, Aiman (age 9) smelled smoke coming from the apartment next door. He told his mum, and together they knocked on the neighbour's door — no answer. His mum could see smoke under the door.

Aiman remembered what he had practised: **Get out first, then call.**

They left the building and went to the open air. Aiman called **999** on his mum's phone.

"999, what is your emergency?"

"There is a fire in Block B, Level 7, Apartment 7C, in Taman Damai."

"Thank you. Are you safe?" "Yes, we are outside."

BOMBA arrived in four minutes. The fire was contained to one unit.

Aiman remembered the number, stayed calm, and gave a clear address. That made all the difference.

### What Do These Numbers Mean? (For Parents)

A note for parents reading this with your child:

| Service | Why It Is Important to Teach |
| --- | --- |
| 999 (main emergency) | Single number covers all — simplest to remember |
| 112 (no credit) | Children often use phones with limited credit; this is critical knowledge |
| 994 (BOMBA) | Useful if local fire station is being called directly |
| 03-2260 9025 (Poison) | For accidental ingestion of household chemicals, medications — ask to speak to a doctor |

**Suggested parent activity:** Practice the emergency call script with your child at home — including giving your home address clearly and calmly. Children who have practised respond better under stress.
