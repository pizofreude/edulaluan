## For Kids 👦👧

### The Internet Is Amazing — And You Need to Know How to Stay Safe In It

You can learn almost anything on the internet. You can watch videos, play games, chat with friends, and discover things from all over the world.

But just like in real life — not everyone online is who they say they are, and some places online are not safe.

Here is what you need to know. 👇

---

### Rule 1: Your Personal Information Is Private 🔒

**Personal information** is anything that could help a stranger find you in real life.

This includes:

* Your full name
* Your home address
* Your school name
* Your phone number
* Your photo (your face)
* Your parents' names or where they work

**Never share this information with anyone online** — even if they seem friendly.

A real friend already knows these things about you. A stranger who asks does not need to.

---

### Rule 2: Not Everyone Online Is Who They Say They Are 🎭

Someone online might say they are:

* A kid your age
* A famous person
* A teacher or trusted adult
* Someone from your school

But they might not be telling the truth.

**If someone online:**
* Asks for your photo
* Wants to meet you in real life
* Asks you to keep your conversations secret from your parents
* Makes you feel uncomfortable or scared

→ **Stop talking to them. Tell a trusted adult immediately.**

You will not get in trouble for telling. You are doing the right thing.

---

### Rule 3: If Something Feels Wrong, Tell Someone 🗣️

Your feelings are your superpower.

If something online makes you feel:
* Scared 😨
* Confused 😕
* Uncomfortable 😣
* Sad 😢
* Like you should hide it from your parents

**→ That is a signal. Tell a trusted adult.**

A trusted adult is someone you feel safe with — a parent, grandparent, teacher, or school counsellor.

**You will never be in trouble for telling.** The problem is not your fault.

---

### Rule 4: Think Before You Post 💭

Once something is posted online — a photo, a message, a video — it can be very hard to take it back.

Before you share anything online, ask yourself:

```
Would I be okay if my teacher saw this?
Would I be okay if my parents saw this?
Would I be okay if this was shown to everyone at school?
```

If the answer to any of these is **no** → do not post it.

---

### Rule 5: Be Kind Online — Always 💛

How you treat people online matters just as much as how you treat them in person.

**Cyberbullying** means bullying someone using a phone, tablet, or computer — like sending mean messages, leaving someone out of a group chat on purpose, or posting unkind things about someone.

Cyberbullying hurts real people.

**If someone is being unkind to you online:**
* Do not reply to mean messages
* Screenshot and save the evidence
* Block the person
* Tell a trusted adult

**If you see someone being unkind to someone else online:**
* Do not join in — even if others are doing it
* You can privately check on the person being bullied
* Tell a trusted adult

---

### Rule 6: Ask Before You Download 📲

Apps and games can sometimes carry hidden risks — like viruses, scams, or content that is not for kids.

**Always ask a parent or guardian before downloading anything** — even if it looks fun and safe.

A parent can help you check:
* Is this app safe?
* Is this app appropriate for your age?
* Does this app need your personal information?

---

### The 5 Online Safety Rules — Quick Reminder

```
1. Keep personal information PRIVATE
2. Not everyone online is who they say they are
3. Tell a trusted adult if something feels wrong
4. Think before you post
5. Be kind — always
```

### A Story to Help You Remember

**Priya's New Online Friend**

Priya (age 11) was playing an online game when another player started chatting with her. They called themselves "SuperFan12" and said they were 11 years old too and loved the same music as her.

Over a few days, SuperFan12 sent her lots of nice messages. Then one day SuperFan12 asked: "What school do you go to? I might know you!"

Priya felt something was a little strange. She did not know this person in real life. She remembered Rule 1 — **personal information is private**.

She did not answer the question. She showed the messages to her mum.

Her mum looked at the account — the profile had very little information and had only been created recently. She helped Priya block the account and report it on the platform.

Priya felt good about what she did. She trusted her feeling that something was off — and she told a trusted adult.

### What If You Are Being Bullied Online?

You are not alone. Many kids experience this. It is not your fault.

**What to do:**

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Do not reply to the mean messages |
| 2 | Take a screenshot (press the side button + volume button on most phones) |
| 3 | Block the person on the platform |
| 4 | Tell a trusted adult — show them the screenshots |
| 5 | If it happens at school, tell a teacher too |

If you are very upset and need someone to talk to:
**Childline Malaysia: 1-800-88-2040** (free, 24 hours)

### The Internet — What It Is and Is Not

| The internet IS... | The internet is NOT... |
| --- | --- |
| A place to learn and explore | Always safe |
| Fun and creative | Full of people you can trust |
| A place to connect with friends | Anonymous — people can find you |
| Useful for schoolwork | A place to share private information |

### For Parents — A Note 👨‍👩‍👧

Your child just read this page. A few things that help at home:

**Have the conversation:** Ask your child what they do online, who they talk to, and how it makes them feel. Curiosity works better than rules alone.

**Create a safe reporting culture:** The most important thing is that your child feels they can tell you anything that happens online without fear of losing their device. If children fear punishment, they hide problems.

**Know the platforms:** Ask your child to show you the apps and games they use. Use them together occasionally — it builds shared language.

**Practical tools:**
* Parental controls on devices (Screen Time on iOS, Digital Wellbeing and Family Link on Android)
* Family-friendly DNS (Cloudflare 1.1.1.3 for families) — blocks inappropriate content at the network level
* Place devices in common areas during homework time

**If your child has encountered something harmful online:**
* Stay calm — your reaction determines whether they tell you next time
* Preserve evidence (screenshots)
* Report to the platform and, if serious, to MCMC (aduan.skmm.gov.my) or PDRM
