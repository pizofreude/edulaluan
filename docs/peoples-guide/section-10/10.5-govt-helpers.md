## For Kids 👦👧

### Malaysia Has Many People Whose Job Is to Help You

Have you ever seen a police officer, a firefighter, or a doctor at work and wondered what their job is really like?

These people work for the government — which means they are paid using our taxes — and their job is to keep us safe, healthy, and protected.

Let's meet some of Malaysia's helpers! 🇲🇾

---

### 👮 The Police — PDRM

**PDRM** stands for **Polis Diraja Malaysia** — the Royal Malaysian Police.

**Their job:**
* Keep people safe from crime
* Investigate crimes when they happen
* Direct traffic and keep roads safe
* Help during disasters and emergencies
* Protect important places and events

**When would you call the police?**
* If you see a crime happening
* If you or someone is in danger
* If something is stolen from you or your family
* If there has been a car accident

📞 **Emergency: 999**

**Fun fact:** PDRM has special units for different jobs — including a marine police unit that patrols Malaysia's seas, a dog unit that helps find missing people, and a cyber crime unit that investigates crimes that happen online.

**How to talk to a police officer:**
* Be calm and speak clearly
* Tell them exactly what happened
* Give your name and address
* Listen to their instructions

Police officers are there to help you. You do not need to be afraid to talk to them.

---

### 🚒 BOMBA — Fire and Rescue Department

**BOMBA** is short for **Jabatan Bomba dan Penyelamat Malaysia** — the Malaysian Fire and Rescue Department.

**Their job:**
* Put out fires 🔥
* Rescue people who are trapped (in buildings, cars, or floods)
* Help during floods and landslides
* Respond to chemical and industrial accidents
* Rescue people from heights and water

**When would you call BOMBA?**
* If there is a fire
* If someone is trapped
* If there has been a serious accident and someone needs to be rescued

📞 **BOMBA: 994**

**Fun fact:** BOMBA officers train for years to stay physically fit, operate heavy equipment, and make decisions under pressure. They also teach fire safety in schools — you may have already met one!

**Fire safety at home — remember these:**

```
🚪 Know two ways out of every room
🔥 Never leave cooking unattended
💡 Do not overload electrical sockets
🚨 Test your smoke alarm with a parent monthly
🚫 Never play with fire or fireworks indoors
```

### 🚑 Ambulance and Hospital Workers — KKM

**KKM** stands for **Kementerian Kesihatan Malaysia** — the Ministry of Health Malaysia.

The people who work in hospitals and clinics include:

| Who | What They Do |
| --- | --- |
| 👨‍⚕️ Doctors | Examine patients, diagnose illnesses, prescribe medicine |
| 👩‍⚕️ Nurses | Care for patients, give medicine, take blood samples, assist doctors |
| 🚑 Paramedics | Provide emergency medical care in ambulances and at accident scenes |
| 💊 Pharmacists | Prepare and dispense medicine safely |
| 🦷 Dentists | Take care of your teeth and gums |

**When would you need them?**
* If you are very sick or injured
* For regular check-ups and vaccinations
* For emergency medical help

📞 **Ambulance: 999**

**Fun fact:** Malaysia has over 145 government hospitals and more than 2,800 government clinics (Klinik Kesihatan) across the country. A visit to a Klinik Kesihatan costs just **RM 1** for Malaysian citizens — made possible by our taxes.

**How to stay healthy:**
```
🥦 Eat fruits and vegetables
💧 Drink enough water every day
🏃 Exercise and play outside regularly
😴 Get enough sleep (9–11 hours for ages 7–12)
🦷 Brush your teeth twice a day
💉 Keep your vaccinations up to date
```

### 🏫 Teachers — Ministry of Education (KPM)

Teachers work for **Kementerian Pendidikan Malaysia (KPM)** — the Ministry of Education.

**Their job:**
* Teach you knowledge and skills
* Help you grow into a capable adult
* Guide your learning and support your development

Government school teachers are paid by the government — with tax money. Every lesson you attend is a public service.

---

### 🌊 Flood Rescue — APM and JKM

Malaysia experiences floods, especially during the monsoon season.

| Agency | Who They Are | What They Do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| APM (Angkatan Pertahanan Awam Malaysia) | Civil Defence Force | Disaster response, evacuation, rescue |
| JKM (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat) | Department of Social Welfare | Welfare aid, relief supplies, support for affected families |
| NADMA | National Disaster Management Agency | Coordinates all disaster response |

During big floods in Malaysia, thousands of volunteers and government workers work together to rescue families, provide food, and find temporary shelter.

### 🌳 Other Helpers You Might Not Think About

Many government workers help us every day without us noticing:

| Who | What They Do |
| --- | --- |
| Road engineers (JKR) | Build and maintain the roads you drive on |
| Water treatment workers (SYABAS, Air Selangor, etc.) | Make sure clean water comes out of your tap |
| Rubbish collectors (local councils) | Keep our neighbourhoods clean and disease-free |
| Forest rangers (Jabatan Perhutanan) | Protect Malaysia's forests and wildlife |
| Coast guard (APMM) | Protect Malaysia's waters and rescue people at sea |

---

### These Helpers Need Our Help Too

Here is something important to understand:

**These helpers can only do their job well when we cooperate.**

This means:
* Do not call 999 for non-emergencies — it takes resources away from real emergencies
* Follow instructions from police, BOMBA, or medical workers during emergencies
* Do not obstruct rescue operations or ambulances
* Be kind and polite to government workers you meet
* Follow the rules (traffic rules, health guidelines, fire safety) — they exist because of real experiences

### A Story to Help You Remember

**The Night the Floodwater Came**

It was 2am when 9-year-old Zara heard her father shouting. Water was coming in under the front door.

Outside, the rain had not stopped for two days.

Her father called **999** and told them their address. Within 20 minutes, two BOMBA officers arrived in a small rescue boat. They helped Zara, her parents, and her little brother out of the house and into the boat.

At the nearby school that was being used as a temporary shelter, JKM workers had set up mattresses and were handing out food and water. APM officers were helping direct families to their spaces.

Zara looked around at all the people in yellow and orange uniforms. Police, BOMBA, JKM, doctors — all working through the night.

"Who are all these people?" she asked her father.

"People whose job is to help," he said. "And they are good at it."

### For Parents — A Note 👨‍👩‍👧

This page introduces children to public services and the concept of civic responsibility — the idea that public institutions exist to serve us and that we have responsibilities toward them in return.

**Conversations to have:**

* "What do you think a police officer's day is like?"
* "How do hospitals get money to pay doctors and nurses?" (answer: taxes — connecting back to 10.4)
* "What would you do if there was a flood warning?" — practical disaster preparedness for families

**Practical preparedness:**

* Know your family's flood evacuation plan if you live in a flood-prone area
* Keep emergency numbers saved in your phone — not just memorised
* Visit a Klinik Kesihatan with your child at least once so they know what it looks like and that it is not scary
