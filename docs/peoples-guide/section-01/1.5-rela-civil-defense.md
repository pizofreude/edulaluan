## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**Who are RELA and APM?**
RELA and APM are groups of trained Malaysians who help during emergencies and disasters. They are not the police or the army, but they work alongside them to keep communities safe.

**What do they do?**
- Help during floods, fires, and other disasters.
- Assist police during big events or emergencies.
- Run community safety programmes.
- Provide first aid training to the public.

**Can I join when I grow up?**
Yes. When you are 18 years old, you can apply to join RELA or APM. You do not need to be a soldier or police officer. You just need to be a Malaysian citizen who wants to help your community.

**How do you contact them?**
- RELA: rela.gov.my or 03-8883 3888
- APM: apm.gov.my or 991

---

### 🧑 Detailed

**Overview**

Malaysia has two key civilian support forces that operate alongside its uniformed emergency services. RELA (Ikatan Relawan Rakyat) functions as a grassroots civilian volunteer corps under KDN, focused on community safety, public order support, and disaster relief assistance. APM (Angkatan Pertahanan Awam Malaysia), or the Civil Defence Force, operates under the Ministry of Home Affairs with a specific mandate for civil protection, disaster preparedness, and emergency response coordination.

While both organisations support government agencies during crises, they have distinct roles, legal powers, and scopes of operation.

**RELA vs APM at a Glance:**

| | RELA | APM |
|---|---|---|
| Full name | Ikatan Relawan Rakyat | Angkatan Pertahanan Awam Malaysia |
| Under | Ministry of Home Affairs (KDN) | Ministry of Home Affairs (KDN) |
| Nature | Civilian volunteer corps | Civil Defence Force |
| Primary role | Community safety, public order support | Disaster preparedness, civil protection |
| Legal powers | Limited enforcement powers during active deployment | Civil Defence Act 1951 powers during emergencies |
| Training | Basic volunteer training | Structured disaster response training |
| Minimum age | 18 years | 18 years |
| Gender | Open to all | Open to all |
| Website | rela.gov.my | apm.gov.my |

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Flood relief centre managed by RELA**
During the 2022 Klang Valley floods, RELA volunteers were deployed to relief centres across Selangor to assist JKM with registration, food distribution, and basic crowd management. Over 3,000 RELA volunteers were activated within 48 hours. They do not conduct water rescues but play a critical role in keeping relief operations organised and humane.

**Scenario 2: APM disaster response training in Sabah**
Following the 2015 Ranau earthquake, APM conducted community disaster preparedness workshops across Sabah, teaching residents how to respond to earthquakes, build emergency kits, and perform basic search and rescue techniques. These sessions are open to the public and free of charge.

**Scenario 3: Azri wants to serve his community**
Azri, 22, wanted to contribute to his community but was not interested in joining the police or military. He enrolled in RELA through his local KDN district office. After completing basic training, he was deployed during a local flood to assist at the Pusat Pemindahan. He later took APM's first aid course to build additional skills.

**Scenario 4: Big public event in KL**
During a major public event at Dataran Merdeka, RELA volunteers supported PDRM with crowd management, directing attendees and maintaining orderly access to exits. RELA members are frequently activated for national celebrations, political events, and large public gatherings under PDRM coordination.

---

**Path A: I need help during an active emergency or disaster**

Step 1 of 2: Call 999 or 991.
- RELA and APM are activated by government authorities, not directly by the public during emergencies.
- Calling 999 or 991 triggers the coordinated response that includes RELA and APM deployment where needed.

Step 2 of 2: Cooperate with deployed RELA and APM personnel.
- Follow instructions from uniformed personnel at relief centres.
- RELA members wear green uniforms with official ID. APM members wear blue-grey uniforms.
- If you are unsure of a person's identity, ask to see their official identification card.

---

**Path B: I want to join RELA**

Step 1 of 3: Confirm your eligibility.
- Malaysian citizen aged 18 and above.
- No serious criminal record.
- Physically fit for volunteer duties.

Step 2 of 3: Register at your nearest KDN district office or online.
- Website: rela.gov.my
- Bring your MyKad and a recent passport-size photo.
- Registration is free.

Step 3 of 3: Complete basic RELA training.
- Training covers public order support, basic emergency response, and community safety.
- Duration: typically 3 to 5 days depending on the state programme.
- Upon completion you will receive your RELA volunteer identification card and uniform.

---

**Path C: I want to join APM**

Step 1 of 3: Confirm your eligibility.
- Malaysian citizen aged 18 and above.
- No serious criminal record.
- Medically fit as assessed during intake.

Step 2 of 3: Apply at your nearest APM state or district office.
- Website: apm.gov.my
- Contact: 991 (non-emergency enquiries) or 03-2691 6703 (HQ).
- Bring your MyKad, academic certificates, and passport-size photo.

Step 3 of 3: Complete APM structured training.
- APM training is more intensive than RELA, covering disaster response, search and rescue basics, first aid, and civil protection.
- Volunteers may progress to specialised units including HAZMAT support, water rescue assistance, and urban search and rescue (USAR) support teams.

---

**Path D: I want to attend APM public training**

Step 1 of 1: Contact your nearest APM state office or check apm.gov.my for scheduled programmes.
- Available programmes include: Community First Aid, Disaster Preparedness Workshop, Fire Safety Awareness, and Youth Civil Defence (for students aged 13 and above).
- Most public programmes are free or heavily subsidised.
- Corporate and institutional training is available at a scheduled rate.

---

**Path E: I want to request RELA or APM support for a community event**

Step 1 of 2: Submit a formal request at least 2 weeks before your event.
- For RELA: contact your nearest KDN district office.
- For APM: contact your nearest APM state office.
- Include event details, expected attendance, location, and type of support needed.

Step 2 of 2: Confirm deployment and briefing with the assigned officer.
- RELA and APM personnel deployed to events operate under their own chain of command.
- Your role as an organiser is to coordinate logistics and access, not to direct their personnel.

## FAQ

**Q: Does RELA have the power to arrest people?**
RELA members have limited enforcement powers only when officially activated and deployed under specific legal provisions such as the Essential (Ikatan Relawan Rakyat) Regulations. In normal circumstances, they do not have arrest powers. They act in a supporting role to PDRM and other enforcement agencies.

**Q: Is APM the same as the Malaysian Army (ATM)?**
No. APM is a civil defence organisation, not a military force. It operates under the Ministry of Home Affairs, not the Ministry of Defence. APM's role is civilian protection and disaster response. The Malaysian Armed Forces (ATM) may be deployed during major disasters but operate under a separate command structure.

**Q: Can women join RELA or APM?**
Yes. Both organisations are open to all Malaysian citizens regardless of gender. Women serve in various roles including community safety, first aid, registration, and disaster coordination.

**Q: Is there a salary for RELA or APM volunteers?**
RELA members are volunteers and do not receive a fixed salary. Allowances may be provided during active deployment. APM has a mix of full-time staff and volunteers. Full-time APM officers are paid civil servants. Volunteer members receive allowances during active service.

**Q: What is the difference between RELA and Skim Rondaan Sukarela (SRS)?**
SRS is a community neighbourhood watch programme under PDRM, focused specifically on crime prevention through voluntary patrols in residential areas. RELA has a broader mandate covering public order, immigration support, and disaster relief. Both are civilian volunteer programmes but operate under different agencies and scopes.
