## About This Page

This page lists Malaysian content creators, official agency channels, and media resources that educate the public on safety and emergency topics. It is a living page. If you know a creator or channel that belongs here, use the "Suggest a resource" button at the bottom.

**Note:** EduLaluan does not endorse any individual creator's views beyond the specific educational content listed. Always verify emergency information against official agency sources.

---

## Official Government Channels

These are the primary authoritative sources for safety and emergency information in Malaysia.

| Agency | Platform | Handle or Link | What They Post |
|---|---|---|---|
| BOMBA (JBPM) | Facebook | facebook.com/Mediabombatv | Weekly activity roundups, fire safety campaigns |
| PDRM | TikTok | @pdrmsia_rasmi | Crime alerts, scam warnings, public safety tips |
| PDRM | Instagram | @pdrmsia_official | Community policing, crime awareness |
| PDRM | X (Twitter) | @PDRMsia | Official statements, emergency announcements |

## Scam Awareness Creators

This is currently the strongest category of safety education content among Malaysian creators.

| Creator | Platform | Handle | Language | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rahmat Fitri | TikTok | @rahmatfitrilawanscammer | BM | Anti-scams trainer, entrepreneur safety |
| Wan Fatin Sufina | TikTok | @wfsufina | BM | AI-powered scams, entrepreneur safety, cyberbullying |

**Context:** These creators were part of TikTok Malaysia's official #ThinkTwice scam prevention campaign in collaboration with PDRM, MCMC, and the Securities Commission. Their content is grounded in real victim experiences and actionable advice.

**Hashtags to follow on TikTok:**
- #ThinkTwice
- #FikirDulu
- #SemakMule
- #AntiScamMalaysia

## Media Organisations Covering Safety and Emergencies

These newsrooms produce consistent, factual coverage on emergencies, disasters, and public safety in Malaysia.

| Organisation | Platform | Why It Is Useful |
|---|---|---|
| Bernama | YouTube / Web | Official national newswire. First to publish NADMA and PDRM statements |
| The Rakyat Post | Web / Instagram | Accessible explainers on scams and digital safety |
| Sinar Harian | Facebook / TikTok | BM-language safety and emergency news, wide reach |

## Content Gap Notice

As of March 2026, there are no prominent independent Malaysian creators producing consistent long-form educational content specifically on:

- Fire safety and home preparedness
- Flood preparedness and go-bag guides
- First aid and CPR tutorials in BM or Manglish
- Ambulance and emergency medical response

**If you create or know of a channel covering these topics, please suggest it below.** EduLaluan will review and add verified resources.

This gap is also an opportunity. If you are an educator, NGO, or content creator interested in filling it, the resources on this page are a starting point for your own content.
