## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**Got scammed? Do these 3 things fast.**

**Step 1: Call 997 right now.**
Tell them what happened. Have your bank account number and the scammer's account number ready. They will help freeze the scammer's account.

**Step 2: Call your bank.**
Ask them to freeze your account and block any more transactions. Every bank has a 24-hour fraud hotline.

**Step 3: Make a police report.**
Go to the nearest police station or report online at sso.rmp.gov.my. Bring your IC and screenshots of the scam.

**Remember:**
- The government will NEVER ask for your bank password or OTP.
- No real investment guarantees you will never lose money.
- If something feels wrong, stop and check first.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

**Overview**

Scams in Malaysia cost victims billions of ringgit each year. The National Scam Response Centre (NSRC) was set up to coordinate fast response across banks, police, and regulators. Knowing exactly who to call and when can make the difference between recovering your money and losing it permanently.

**Types of scams covered in this guide:**
- Online investment fraud (Macau scam, fake forex, crypto)
- Phone and SMS phishing (fake Bank Negara, fake LHDN calls)
- Fake online sellers (goods paid but not delivered)
- Love scams and social engineering
- Mule account recruitment

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Uncle Ravi and the "investment opportunity"**
Uncle Ravi received a WhatsApp message about a guaranteed 30% monthly return investment. He transferred RM8,000 over three days. When he tried to withdraw his profit, the platform disappeared. He had less than 24 hours to call 997 and attempt a freeze on the scammer's account.

**Scenario 2: Puan Aishah and the fake Shopee seller**
Puan Aishah paid RM450 for a KitchenAid mixer from a Shopee seller with 5-star reviews. The goods never arrived, and the seller deleted their account. Her case falls under KPDNHEP for consumer protection.

**Scenario 3: Syafiq and the "Bank Negara" call**
Syafiq received a call from someone claiming to be Bank Negara, saying his account was used for money laundering. They asked him to transfer funds to a "safe account." He hung up and called BNM directly at 1-300-88-5465 to verify. It was a scam.

**Scenario 4: Mei Ling and the mule account**
Mei Ling received RM500 and was asked to transfer it to another account, not knowing she was acting as a money mule. She is now legally liable under the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA). Mule accounts are a crime, even if you did not know you were part of a scam.

**Path A: I transferred money to a scammer**

Step 1 of 4: Call 997 immediately.
- Operating hours: 8AM to 8PM daily.
- Tell them: your full name, your bank and account number, scammer's bank and account number, amount transferred, and date and time of transfer.
- They will coordinate with your bank to attempt an account freeze.

Step 2 of 4: Call your bank's fraud hotline.
- Maybank: 1-300-88-6688
- CIMB: +603-6204-7788
- Public Bank: 1-800-22-5555
- RHB: 1-800-88-9878
- Hong Leong: 1-300-88-1234
- Other banks: check the back of your debit or credit card.

Step 3 of 4: File a police report.
- Online: sso.rmp.gov.my (selected cases only)
- Walk-in: any police station, within 24 hours.
- Bring: your IC, screenshots, transaction receipts, chat logs.
- CCID Hotline: 03-2610 1559

Step 4 of 4: Report to the relevant agency.
- Investment scam: Securities Commission at aduan@sc.com.my or 03-6204-8999
- Fake online seller: KPDNHEP eAduan at eaduan.kpdn.gov.my
- Phishing or fake website: Cyber999 at 1-300-88-2999

---

**Path B: I gave my OTP or bank password to someone**

Step 1 of 3: Call your bank immediately, 24 hours.
- Ask them to freeze your account and disable online banking access.
- Change your PIN at the nearest ATM or branch.

Step 2 of 3: Call 997 if money has already left your account.
- Report as a scam case. Provide your account details and transaction history.

Step 3 of 3: File a police report.
- Walk-in or online at sso.rmp.gov.my.

---

**Path C: I bought something online and it never arrived**

Step 1 of 2: Report to KPDNHEP.
- Website: eaduan.kpdn.gov.my
- WhatsApp: 019-279 4317
- App: ezAduan

Step 2 of 2: Report to the platform directly.
- Shopee: shopee.com.my/help
- Lazada: lazada.com.my/helpcenter
- Document everything before reporting.

---

**Path D: I think I am being scammed right now**

Step 1: Stop all communication with the person.
Step 2: Do NOT transfer any money.
Step 3: Check the account at semakmule.bnm.gov.my and penipu.my before doing anything.
Step 4: Call a trusted family member or friend to verify.
Step 5: If you already transferred money, start Path A above.

---

**Path E: I received a suspicious call or message**

Step 1: Do not engage. Hang up or ignore the message.
Step 2: Verify independently. Call the agency directly using numbers from their official website only.
Step 3: Report the number to MCMC at aduan.skmm.gov.my or 1-800-18-0300.

---

**Path F: Someone asked me to receive and transfer money for them**

This is called a mule account arrangement. It is a criminal offence under AMLA 2001, even if you did not know it was a scam.

Step 1: Stop all transactions immediately.
Step 2: Do not transfer any remaining funds.
Step 3: Go to the nearest police station and make a voluntary report. This protects you legally.
Step 4: Consult a lawyer if you have already transferred funds on someone else's behalf.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get my money back after a scam?**
It depends on how fast you report. If the scammer's account is frozen before funds are withdrawn, partial or full recovery is possible. The faster you call 997, the better the chance. There is no guarantee of recovery.

**Q: Do I need to call both 997 and my bank?**
Not necessarily. Calling 997 triggers coordination with your bank automatically. However, calling your bank directly as well speeds up the account freeze. Both calls take less than 10 minutes total.

**Q: What if I accidentally became a mule account?**
Report yourself to the police voluntarily as soon as possible. A voluntary report shows you did not knowingly participate. Continuing to transfer funds after discovering the scam increases your legal liability significantly.

**Q: Is 997 available 24 hours?**
No. NSRC operates from 8AM to 8PM daily. Outside these hours, call your bank's 24-hour fraud hotline directly and file a police report online.

**Q: What if the scam happened months ago?**
You can still file a police report. Recovery becomes less likely over time, but a report creates an official record and contributes to investigations that may help other victims.
