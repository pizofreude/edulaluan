## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is BOMBA?**
BOMBA is the Fire and Rescue Department of Malaysia. They help people during fires, floods, accidents, and other emergencies. Their job is to save lives and keep people safe.

**When should you call BOMBA?**
- There is a fire at home, school, or anywhere nearby.
- Someone is trapped in a car, building, or flood.
- There is a dangerous chemical spill.
- A flood is rising and people need rescue.

**What is the number to call?**
Call **999**. It is free. It works 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

**What do you say when you call?**
Tell them:
1. Your name.
2. Where the emergency is (full address).
3. What is happening (fire, flood, accident).
4. How many people are affected.

**Stay safe while waiting:**
- Get out of the building if there is a fire.
- Do not use the lift during a fire.
- Go to your nearest neighbour or open space.
- Do not go back inside for any reason.

### 🧑 Detailed

**Overview**

The Fire and Rescue Department of Malaysia (Jabatan Bomba dan Penyelamat Malaysia) operates under the Ministry of Housing and Local Government. BOMBA is responsible for fire suppression, emergency rescue, flood and disaster response, HAZMAT operations, and public fire safety education. They operate over 400 fire stations nationwide across Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, and Sarawak.

**Full Scope of BOMBA Services:**
- Fire suppression: residential, commercial, industrial, and forest fires.
- Emergency rescue: road accidents, trapped persons, structural collapse.
- Flood and disaster response: water rescue, evacuation support.
- HAZMAT: chemical, biological, and gas leak response.
- Ambulance services: in areas where BOMBA operates emergency medical response.
- Fire safety inspections: buildings, factories, schools, and homes.
- Community training: fire drills, first aid, and safety awareness programmes.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Auntie Rokiah's kitchen fire**
Auntie Rokiah was frying keropok when the oil caught fire. She panicked and threw water on it, which made the flames worse. Her neighbour called 999 immediately. BOMBA arrived within 8 minutes. The fire was contained to the kitchen. Had she known not to use water on an oil fire, the damage could have been avoided entirely. BOMBA later offered her household a free fire safety briefing.

**Scenario 2: Flood rescue in Kuala Krai**
During the monsoon season, rising waters trapped a family of five on the upper floor of their home in Kuala Krai, Kelantan. A neighbour with a working phone called 999. BOMBA coordinated with the district disaster management committee to deploy water rescue units. The family was evacuated safely within two hours.

**Scenario 3: School fire drill request**
A primary school in Subang Jaya had never conducted a proper fire drill. The headmaster contacted the nearest BOMBA station to request an official fire drill and safety briefing for teachers and students. BOMBA conducted the session at no cost. This is a free service available to all schools and organisations in Malaysia.

**Scenario 4: Chemical spill at a factory**
A small factory in Shah Alam experienced a gas leak from a storage tank. The factory manager called 999 and specified it was a chemical emergency. BOMBA dispatched a HAZMAT team equipped with specialised gear. Regular employees were evacuated and the area was cordoned off. Never attempt to handle chemical or gas emergencies without professional support.

---

**Path A: There is a fire in my home or building**

Step 1 of 4: Get everyone out first.
- Do not stop to collect belongings.
- Do not use the lift. Use the stairs.
- Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire.
- Go to your building assembly point or nearest open space.

Step 2 of 4: Call 999 from a safe location.
- State your full address clearly.
- Tell them if anyone is still inside.
- Stay on the line if possible.

Step 3 of 4: Do not re-enter the building.
- Even to retrieve valuables or pets.
- Wait for BOMBA to declare it safe.

Step 4 of 4: After the fire.
- Contact your insurance provider for property damage claims.
- If you have no insurance, contact your nearest JKM (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat) office for emergency aid.
- Request a fire cause report from BOMBA for insurance purposes.

---

**Path B: Someone is trapped**

Step 1 of 3: Call 999 immediately.
- Specify: road accident, flood rescue, structural collapse, or lift breakdown.
- Give the exact location including nearby landmarks.

Step 2 of 3: Do not move an injured person unless there is immediate danger (fire, rising water).
- Moving an injured person incorrectly can worsen spinal or internal injuries.

Step 3 of 3: Keep the person calm and conscious until BOMBA arrives.
- Talk to them.
- Keep them warm if possible.
- Do not give food or water to an unconscious person.

---

**Path C: Gas or chemical leak**

Step 1 of 3: Evacuate the area immediately.
- Do not switch any electrical switches on or off.
- Do not use your phone inside the affected area.
- Move upwind from the source.

Step 2 of 3: Call 999 from a safe distance.
- Specify it is a gas or chemical emergency.
- Do not re-enter until BOMBA HAZMAT team clears the area.

Step 3 of 3: Inform neighbours nearby.
- Especially elderly or mobility-impaired residents who may need help evacuating.

---

**Path D: Request a fire safety inspection**

Step 1 of 2: Contact your nearest BOMBA station directly.
- Find your nearest station at bomba.gov.my.
- Inspections are free for residential properties.
- Commercial and industrial properties may require a scheduled appointment.

Step 2 of 2: Prepare for the inspection.
- BOMBA will check fire extinguishers, smoke detectors, escape routes, and electrical panels.
- They will issue a report with recommendations.
- No penalties are issued for residential inspections. The goal is prevention.

---

**Path E: Request a fire drill or community training**

Step 1 of 1: Contact your nearest BOMBA station or email through bomba.gov.my.
- Available for: schools, offices, condominiums, community halls, factories.
- Services include: fire drill facilitation, fire extinguisher training, first aid basics.
- Free of charge for most community and educational institutions.

## FAQ

**Q: Does BOMBA charge for their services?**
No. All emergency response services including fire suppression, rescue, and flood response are free of charge. Fire safety inspections for residential properties are also free.

**Q: Can I call BOMBA for a cat stuck in a tree or a bee hive removal?**
Yes. BOMBA does assist with animal rescue and bee or hornet nest removal, particularly when it poses a safety risk. Call 999 or your nearest BOMBA station for non-life-threatening cases.

**Q: What is the difference between BOMBA and APM (Civil Defence)?**
BOMBA focuses on fire suppression, rescue, and HAZMAT. APM (Angkatan Pertahanan Awam Malaysia) is the Civil Defence Force, which supports during large-scale disasters and civil emergencies. They often work together during major floods or disasters. APM is covered in Page 1.4.

**Q: How fast will BOMBA arrive?**
The standard response target for urban areas is 7 to 10 minutes. Rural and remote areas may take longer depending on station proximity. Calling 999 clearly and immediately gives BOMBA the best chance to respond fast.

**Q: What should I keep at home for fire safety?**
At minimum: one working fire extinguisher (ABC type), a smoke detector on every floor, and a clear exit plan that every household member knows. BOMBA can advise on this during a home inspection.
