## About This Page

This page lists Malaysian content creators, official agency channels, and media resources that help the public understand business registration, SME financing, startup culture, and digital commerce. This is a living page. If you know a creator or channel that belongs here, use the **"Suggest a resource"** button at the bottom.

**Note:** EduLaluan does not endorse any individual creator's business or investment views. Business and legal information from creators should always be verified against SSM, LHDN, SME Corp, or a licensed professional. Be especially cautious of creators promoting specific investment products, MLM schemes, or "business opportunity" content — verify SSM registration and BNM / SC licensing before engaging.

## Content Gap Notice

As of March 2026, Malaysian business creator content has grown substantially — particularly around e-commerce and side income — but meaningful gaps remain:

* **Business legal literacy in plain language** — SSM compliance, company secretary obligations, director duties, and shareholder agreements are almost entirely absent from creator content; most business owners learn these the hard way or not at all
* **Grant application walkthroughs** — while grant listings exist, step-by-step video guides on actually completing a Cradle CIP, DISF, or state grant application are rare; the gap between knowing a grant exists and knowing how to apply is enormous
* **Deep tech and R&D commercialisation** — MRANTI, MaGIC's deep tech programmes, and IP commercialisation are underserved by independent creators; content defaults to e-commerce and dropshipping
* **BM-language startup ecosystem content** — the Malaysian startup ecosystem conversation happens almost entirely in English, excluding a significant portion of the founder community who are more comfortable in BM
* **Failure and recovery stories** — Malaysian entrepreneur content skews heavily toward success narratives; content on business failure, restructuring, debt management for businesses, and pivoting is scarce despite being among the most useful content for real founders
* **East Malaysia business context** — Sabah and Sarawak have distinct business registration requirements, licensing frameworks, and grant ecosystems that are almost entirely absent from national creator content

**If you create or know of a channel covering any of these gaps, please suggest it below.** EduLaluan will review and add verified resources.
