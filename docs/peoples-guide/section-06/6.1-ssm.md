## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**Why do you need to register a business?**

In Malaysia, running a business without registering it is illegal.

Registering with SSM means:

* Your business name is officially yours
* You can open a business bank account
* You can issue proper invoices and receipts
* Customers and suppliers can verify you are a real, registered business
* You are entitled to apply for government grants and loans

SSM is the government agency that registers all businesses in Malaysia.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

**Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM)** — the Companies Commission of Malaysia — is the statutory body responsible for registering and regulating all businesses and companies in Malaysia. It operates under the Companies Act 2016 and the Registration of Businesses Act 1956.

Every business entity operating in Malaysia, regardless of size, must be registered with SSM before commencing operations.

This page covers the registration process for the two most common structures — **sole proprietorships / partnerships** and **private limited companies (Sdn Bhd)** — and the full checklist of what follows registration before you are truly ready to operate.

For a full comparison of business structures, see **Section 6.2 — Types of Business Structures in Malaysia**.

## Part 1: Business Name Search and Approval

Before registering, your proposed business name must be:

* Available (not already registered)
* Compliant with SSM naming guidelines

### Name Search

| Method | How To |
| --- | --- |
| Online (SSM e-Info) | ssm.com.my → e-Info → Business Name Search |
| MyCoID portal | mycoid.ssm.com.my |
| SSM counter | Walk in to any SSM branch |

Name searches are free and instantaneous online.

### Naming Rules

SSM will reject names that:

* Are identical or confusingly similar to existing registered names
* Contain restricted words without approval (e.g. "Bank", "Insurance", "Royal", "National", "Malaysia")
* Are offensive or contrary to public interest
* Imply a connection with a government body without authorisation

### Name Reservation (Sdn Bhd)

For Sdn Bhd registrations, your proposed company name must be **approved by SSM before incorporation**:

* Submit via MyCoID
* Approval typically within **1 working day**
* Approved name is reserved for **30 days** while you complete incorporation

Sole proprietorships and partnerships do not require advance name approval — the name is registered at the point of application.

## Part 2: Registering a Sole Proprietorship or Partnership

### Who It Is For

* Individual running a business under their own name or a trade name
* Two or more individuals running a business together (partnership)
* Freelancers and self-employed individuals who want a formal business identity

### Registration Methods

| Method | Where | Processing Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Online (ezBiz) | ezbiz.ssm.com.my | Immediate upon payment |
| MyGOV portal | mygov.my → Business | Immediate upon payment |
| SSM counter (walk-in) | Any SSM branch nationwide | Same day |

**ezBiz is the fastest route** — registration is completed online in under 30 minutes if you have all information ready.

### Required Information

| Item | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Applicant's MyKad | IC number and personal details |
| Business name | Your proposed trade name |
| Business address | Principal place of business |
| Business nature | Main activity code (MSIC code) |
| Partners' details | If registering a partnership |

### Fees

| Registration Type | Annual Fee |
| --- | --- |
| Sole proprietor — own name (nama sendiri) | RM 30 |
| Sole proprietor — trade name (nama perniagaan) | RM 60 |
| Partnership — own names | RM 60 |
| Partnership — trade name | RM 60 |
| Branch registration (each branch) | RM 5 |

### Validity and Renewal

Sole proprietor and partnership registrations are valid for **1 year** and must be renewed annually.

Renewal can be done via ezBiz online or at any SSM counter.

**Late renewal attracts a penalty.** If renewal lapses by more than 12 months, re-registration as a new business is required — your original registration number is lost.

## Part 3: Registering a Private Limited Company (Sdn Bhd)

### Who It Is For

* Businesses wanting a separate legal entity from the owner
* Businesses seeking investment, government tenders, or formal contracts
* Two or more founders building a scalable business

### Key Differences from Sole Proprietor

| Feature | Sole Proprietor | Sdn Bhd |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Legal entity | Not separate from owner | Separate legal entity |
| Liability | Unlimited personal liability | Limited to share capital |
| Ownership | Owner only | Shareholders |
| Registration fee | RM 30–RM 60 | RM 1,010 (standard) |
| Annual obligation | Renew registration | Annual Return, Audited Accounts |
| Tax filing | Form B (personal) | Form C (corporate) |

### Registration Methods

| Method | Where | Processing Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Online (MyCoID) | mycoid.ssm.com.my | 1 working day |
| SSM counter | SSM branches | 1–3 working days |
| Via company secretary | Appointed secretary submits | 1–3 working days |

**A licensed company secretary is required by law for all Sdn Bhd companies** and must be appointed within 30 days of incorporation. Many businesses use a company secretary to handle both incorporation and ongoing compliance.

### Required Information and Documents

| Item | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Company name | Pre-approved via MyCoID |
| Registered address | Must be a physical address in Malaysia |
| Directors | Minimum 1, must be Malaysian resident aged 18+ |
| Shareholders | Minimum 1, maximum 50 for Sdn Bhd |
| Share capital | Minimum RM 1 (no minimum paid-up capital required since 2016) |
| Memorandum and Articles | Constitution (auto-generated or custom) |

### Fees

| Item | Fee |
| --- | --- |
| Name approval | RM 50 |
| Incorporation | RM 1,010 (for up to RM 400,000 authorised capital) |
| Company secretary (annual) | RM 600–RM 2,400 (varies by provider) |

### Ongoing Obligations (Sdn Bhd)

Unlike sole proprietors, Sdn Bhd companies have significant annual compliance obligations:

| Obligation | Deadline |
| --- | --- |
| Annual Return (AR) | Within 30 days of anniversary of incorporation |
| Audited financial statements | Within 6 months of financial year end |
| Tax filing (Form C) | Within 7 months of financial year end |
| Director updates | Notify SSM within 14 days of any changes |
| Share changes | Notify SSM within 14 days |

Failure to file Annual Returns results in compounds (fines) and can lead to SSM striking off the company.

## Part 4: Post-Registration Checklist

SSM registration is step one. A business is not fully ready to operate legally until several more registrations and obligations are in place.

### The Full Registration-to-Operation Checklist

```
✅ 1. SSM registration (this page)
✅ 2. Business bank account
✅ 3. Tax registration with LHDN
✅ 4. SST registration (if applicable — see Section 3.6 and 6.5)
✅ 5. EPF registration (if hiring staff)
✅ 6. SOCSO registration (if hiring staff)
✅ 7. EIS registration (if hiring staff)
✅ 8. Business operating licence (local authority — MBPJ, DBKL, etc.)
✅ 9. Industry-specific licences and permits (see Section 6.4)
✅ 10. Business insurance (optional but recommended)
```

### Step-by-Step Post-Registration

#### 2. Business Bank Account

* Most banks require SSM registration certificate and MyKad
* Sdn Bhd requires additional documents: M&A, director resolutions, company seal
* Open a dedicated business account — never mix personal and business funds

#### 3. LHDN Tax Registration

* Register as a taxpayer with LHDN at **mytax.hasil.gov.my**
* Sole proprietors use their existing individual tax number (Form B)
* Sdn Bhd must register separately for corporate tax (Form C) and obtain a tax reference number
* Cross-reference: **Section 3.6 — Business Tax Basics**

#### 4. SST Registration (if applicable)

* Register for Service Tax if taxable services exceed RM 500,000 per 12 months
* Register for Sales Tax if manufacturing taxable goods above the threshold
* Via: **mysst.customs.gov.my**
* Cross-reference: **Section 3.6 — Business Tax Basics**

#### 5–7. EPF, SOCSO, and EIS (if hiring employees)

| Contribution | Register At | Deadline |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EPF (KWSP) | kwsp.gov.my | Before first employee payroll |
| SOCSO (PERKESO) | perkeso.gov.my | Before first employee payroll |
| EIS | perkeso.gov.my | Before first employee payroll |

Employers who hire even one employee are legally required to register and contribute.

Cross-reference: **Section 2.4 (EPF)**, **Section 2.5 (SOCSO)**, **Section 2.6 (EIS)**

#### 8. Business Operating Licence (Lesen Perniagaan)

* Required from the local authority (PBT) where your business operates
* Apply at your local MBPJ, DBKL, MPKj, MBJB, MBPP, or equivalent
* Required for any physical business premises
* Annual renewal required
* Some councils offer online application via their portals

#### 9. Industry-Specific Licences

Certain industries require additional licences beyond the operating licence:

| Industry | Licence / Permit | Issuing Body |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Food and beverage | Food premise licence, halal cert (optional) | Local authority, JAKIM |
| Healthcare clinic | Private healthcare facility licence | KKM |
| Money services | Money services licence | BNM |
| Import / export | Import / export licence | MITI, Customs |
| Education | Private school / tuition licence | MOE |
| Security services | Security agency licence | PDRM |

Cross-reference: **Section 6.4 — Business Licences and Permits**

## Part 5: SSM Searches — How the Public Looks Up a Business

SSM's public search tool allows anyone to verify whether a business or company is legitimately registered.

### Why This Matters

Before engaging any supplier, contractor, or business partner:

* Verify their SSM registration is active
* Confirm the registered business name matches what they present
* Check director names for Sdn Bhd (indicates who is legally responsible)

### How to Search

| Tool | Access | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SSM e-Info (basic) | ssm.com.my → e-Info | Free (name existence check) |
| SSM e-Info (detailed) | ssm.com.my → e-Info | RM 10–RM 30 (full business info) |
| MyCoID business search | mycoid.ssm.com.my | Free (company status) |

### What You Can Find

| Information | Available? |
| --- | --- |
| Business registration status (active / struck off) | Yes — free |
| Registration number | Yes — free |
| Registered address | Yes — paid |
| Directors / owners | Yes — paid (Sdn Bhd) |
| Date of incorporation | Yes — paid |
| Share capital | Yes — paid (Sdn Bhd) |
| Annual return filing history | Yes — paid (Sdn Bhd) |

**Practical use:** Before paying a deposit to any contractor or supplier, do a basic SSM search. An unregistered or struck-off business has no formal legal standing — disputes are harder to resolve.

## Part 6: Deregistration and Striking Off

### Voluntary Deregistration (Sole Proprietor / Partnership)

If you cease operations:

* File for deregistration at any SSM counter or via ezBiz
* SSM cancels the registration
* Avoid leaving a registration lapsed without formal deregistration — it may affect future registrations under the same name

### Striking Off (Sdn Bhd)

A company can be voluntarily struck off by applying to SSM under Section 549 of the Companies Act 2016 if:

* It has not commenced operations, or
* It has ceased operations and has no outstanding liabilities

Conditions:

* No outstanding tax (clearance from LHDN required)
* No outstanding debts or legal proceedings
* Directors' declaration that the company is dormant and solvent

SSM may also strike off a company **involuntarily** if it fails to file Annual Returns for two or more consecutive years.

**A struck-off company ceases to exist as a legal entity.** Any assets remaining in the company vest in the government (bona vacantia).

## Practical Habits for New Business Owners

Business owners who start on solid ground tend to:

* Complete the full post-registration checklist before taking on clients or employees
* Open a dedicated business bank account immediately — mixing personal and business funds creates accounting and tax complications
* Keep their SSM registration certificate, business licence, and tax registration documents in a single folder
* Set calendar reminders for SSM renewal, Annual Return, and tax filing deadlines
* Engage a company secretary and an accountant early — their fees are far less than the cost of compliance failures

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Operating without SSM**

A home baker sold cakes via Instagram for two years without SSM registration. When she applied for a business loan, the bank could not process it without a valid SSM certificate. Retroactive registration was straightforward — but the two years of unregistered operation created gaps in her business history that affected the loan assessment.

**Scenario 2: SSM registered but no operating licence**

A hair salon registered with SSM and opened without applying for a business operating licence from the local council. An enforcement raid resulted in a compound fine and a temporary closure notice. The SSM certificate is necessary but not sufficient to operate premises legally.

**Scenario 3: Supplier verified via SSM before payment**

A small business owner was approached by a contractor with a professional-looking proposal. Before paying the RM 5,000 deposit, she ran an SSM search. The company had been struck off six months earlier. She declined and found a registered alternative — avoiding a probable loss.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I register a business before I have a business address?**
You need a registered address for SSM. For home-based businesses, your home address is acceptable. Virtual office addresses are also accepted by SSM for Sdn Bhd registration.

**Q: Is a sole proprietorship right for me or should I go straight to Sdn Bhd?**
See Section 6.2 for a full comparison. In brief: sole proprietorship is simpler and cheaper to start but carries unlimited personal liability. Sdn Bhd limits liability but has higher compliance costs. Most one-person businesses start as sole proprietors and convert to Sdn Bhd when revenue justifies it.

**Q: Do I need a company secretary from day one?**
For Sdn Bhd: yes — a licensed company secretary must be appointed within 30 days of incorporation. For sole proprietors: no company secretary is required.

**Q: Can a foreigner register a business in Malaysia?**
Foreigners can incorporate a Sdn Bhd in Malaysia, subject to foreign equity restrictions in certain sectors. Sole proprietorships and partnerships are generally only available to Malaysian citizens and permanent residents. Specific restrictions depend on the industry.

**Q: What is the difference between a business registration number and a company registration number?**
Sole proprietor and partnership registrations have a **business registration number** issued under the Registration of Businesses Act 1956 (format: XXXXXXXX-X). Sdn Bhd companies have a **company registration number** issued under the Companies Act 2016 (format: XXXXXXXX-X or the newer 12-digit format). Both are issued by SSM but under different legislation.
