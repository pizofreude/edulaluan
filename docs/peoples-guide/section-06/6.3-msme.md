## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is an MSME?**

MSME stands for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprise.

Most businesses in Malaysia are MSMEs — from a hawker stall to a small factory.

The government has agencies that specifically help MSMEs with:

* Business loans at low interest rates
* Free or subsidised training
* Grants that do not need to be repaid
* Help setting up, growing, or going digital

Different agencies help different groups of people.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysia defines MSMEs by annual sales turnover and number of full-time employees, varying by sector:

| Category | Manufacturing (Sales) | Services and Other Sectors (Sales) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Micro | Less than RM 300,000 | Less than RM 300,000 |
| Small | RM 300,000–RM 15 million | RM 300,000–RM 3 million |
| Medium | RM 15 million–RM 50 million | RM 3 million–RM 20 million |

Source: SME Corp Malaysia / BNM MSME definition (verify current thresholds as these are periodically revised).

Most programmes in this page are available to businesses that meet these thresholds. Beyond this size, businesses are considered large enterprises and access different support channels.

## Agency 1: SME Corp Malaysia

**Open to: All Malaysians**

### What It Is

SME Corp Malaysia is the central coordinating agency for MSME development under the Ministry of Entrepreneur and Cooperatives Development (MECD). It does not primarily offer financing directly — instead it coordinates, aggregates, and facilitates access to the broader MSME support ecosystem.

### Key Roles

| Function | What It Does |
| --- | --- |
| Policy coordination | Coordinates MSME programmes across 15+ government agencies |
| SME Portal | Central directory of all government MSME programmes and grants |
| Business advisory | Free advisory services via SME Corp offices nationwide |
| Capacity building | Training programmes, market linkages, industry development |
| Grant facilitation | Manages selected grants directly and refers to other agencies |

### Key Programmes

| Programme | What It Offers | Who Qualifies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Domestic Investment Strategic Fund (DISF) | Grant for domestic investment in growth sectors | SMEs in manufacturing and services |
| Productivity Nexus Programme | Productivity improvement and lean management | Manufacturing SMEs |
| SME Digitalisation Initiative | Grant matching for digital tools adoption (RM 5,000–RM 10,000) | All SMEs with SSM registration |
| Business Accelerator Programme (BAP) | Mentoring, advisory, and market access | Early-stage and growth-stage SMEs |
| SME Financing Guarantee Scheme (via CGC) | Loan guarantee for SMEs without collateral | All SMEs |

### How to Access

* **SME Portal:** smecorp.gov.my — browse all programmes by category, sector, or eligibility
* **SME Corp offices:** Nationwide, including state offices in all 13 states
* Walk in or book appointment for free business advisory

### What to Prepare

* SSM registration certificate
* Business profile or brief business plan
* Latest financial statements (if available)
* MyKad

## Agency 2: MARA (Majlis Amanah Rakyat)

**Open to: Bumiputera Malaysians only**

### What It Is

MARA is a statutory body under the Ministry of Rural and Regional Development focused on the economic and educational advancement of Bumiputera communities. It operates across education, business financing, and retail development.

### Key Financing Programmes

| Programme | Amount | Interest / Profit Rate | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Skim Pinjaman Perniagaan Am | RM 20,000–RM 5 million | 4% p.a. (flat) | General business financing |
| Skim Usahawan Muda MARA | Up to RM 200,000 | 4% p.a. | Young entrepreneur financing |
| Skim Francais MARA | RM 50,000–RM 500,000 | Concessional rate | Franchise business acquisition |
| Skim Perumahan Usahawan | Up to RM 500,000 | Concessional rate | Business premises / shophouse |

### Training and Capacity Building

| Programme | What It Covers |
| --- | --- |
| MARA Digital | Digital skills, e-commerce, online business setup |
| Kelas Kemahiran | Vocational and trade skills (cooking, tailoring, craft) |
| Entrepreneurship development courses | Business planning, financial management, marketing |
| MARA Bazaar and marketplace | Physical and digital market access for MARA entrepreneurs |

### How to Apply

* **Portal:** mara.gov.my
* Walk in to nearest MARA office (nationwide)
* Bring: SSM certificate, business plan, financial statements, MyKad, land / tenancy documents (if applicable)

### What to Prepare

A business plan is important for MARA financing applications — not necessarily elaborate, but it should cover:

* Business description and products / services
* Target market
* Financial projections (3 years)
* How the financing will be used

## Agency 3: TEKUN Nasional

**Open to: Bumiputera Malaysians (main schemes) and Indian entrepreneurs (Skim TUBE)**

### What It Is

TEKUN (Tabung Ekonomi Kumpulan Usaha Niaga) is a micro-financing agency under the Ministry of Entrepreneur and Cooperatives Development. It focuses on the smallest businesses — micro enterprises and small traders who may not qualify for bank financing.

### Key Financing Schemes

| Scheme | Open To | Loan Amount | Rate | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Skim Pembiayaan TEKUN | Bumiputera | RM 1,000–RM 100,000 | 4% p.a. (flat) | Micro and small business |
| Skim TUBE (Tabung Usahawan India Bumiputera) | Indian entrepreneurs | RM 1,000–RM 50,000 | 4% p.a. (flat) | Micro and small business |
| Skim Pembiayaan Wanita | Bumiputera women | RM 5,000–RM 100,000 | 4% p.a. | Women-owned businesses |
| Skim e-TEKUN | Bumiputera | Up to RM 10,000 | 4% p.a. | Online and e-commerce businesses |

### Key Features

* No collateral required for most schemes
* Repayment period typically 3–5 years
* Group guarantee scheme available for community-based applicants
* Targeted at those with limited credit history or no existing bank relationship

### Training Programmes

| Programme | What It Covers |
| --- | --- |
| Modul Keusahawanan TEKUN | Basic entrepreneurship and business skills |
| Digital commerce training | Social media selling, Shopee and Lazada setup |
| Financial literacy for micro enterprises | Cash flow, bookkeeping basics |

### How to Apply

* **Portal:** tekun.gov.my
* TEKUN offices in all states — walk-in or appointment
* Bring: MyKad, SSM certificate (if registered), business photos, bank statements (3–6 months)

**Note:** TEKUN accepts applications from businesses with or without SSM registration for the smallest loan amounts — this makes it accessible to informal traders looking to formalise.

## Agency 4: PUNB (Perbadanan Usahawan Nasional Berhad)

**Open to: Bumiputera Malaysians only**

### What It Is

PUNB focuses on developing Bumiputera entrepreneurs in the retail, franchise, and manufacturing sectors. Unlike TEKUN (micro focus) or MARA (broad mandate), PUNB specifically develops structured business ventures — particularly in franchise and retail.

### Key Programmes

| Programme | What It Offers | Amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Skim Pembiayaan Perniagaan PUNB | Business financing for established ventures | RM 50,000–RM 5 million |
| Skim Usahawan Francais PUNB | Franchise acquisition and development | RM 100,000–RM 1 million |
| Program Vendor PUNB | Vendor development for supply chain integration | Varies |
| Skim Pembangunan Usahawan Runcit (SPUR) | Retail business development (mini markets, convenience) | RM 50,000–RM 300,000 |

### Capacity Building

| Programme | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| Business coaching and mentoring | Structured entrepreneur development |
| Retail management training | Operations, inventory, customer service |
| Franchise readiness programme | Preparing entrepreneurs for franchise ownership |
| Supply chain and vendor linkages | Connecting PUNB entrepreneurs with large buyers |

### How to Apply

* **Portal:** punb.com.my
* PUNB offices in Kuala Lumpur and state liaison offices
* Bring: SSM certificate, business plan, financial statements, franchise agreement (if applicable)

**PUNB financing tends to be for businesses with some operating history** — unlike TEKUN which accommodates new or micro businesses. Most PUNB programmes expect a business with at least 1–2 years of operations.

## Agency 5: MDEC (Malaysia Digital Economy Corporation)

**Open to: All Malaysians**

### What It Is

MDEC is the government agency driving Malaysia's digital economy agenda. For MSMEs, MDEC's most relevant role is facilitating digital adoption, supporting tech-enabled businesses, and developing the digital export capability of Malaysian companies.

### Key Programmes for MSMEs

| Programme | What It Offers | Who Qualifies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SME Digitalisation Grant | RM 5,000 matching grant for digital tools (accounting, HR, e-commerce) | SMEs with SSM registration |
| Global Acceleration and Innovation Network (GAIN) | Acceleration programme for tech startups | Early-stage tech companies |
| eRezeki | Digital income platform for individuals | Low-income individuals |
| Digital Marketing Grant | Support for digital export marketing | Exporting SMEs |
| Malaysia Tech Entrepreneur Programme (MTEP) | Visa and support for foreign tech founders | Non-Malaysian tech founders |
| ScaleUp Malaysia | Growth programme for scaling tech companies | Revenue-generating tech startups |

### How to Access

* **Portal:** mdec.my — programme listings and applications
* **SME Digitalisation:** Apply via the SME Portal (smecorp.gov.my) — MDEC and SME Corp jointly administer this
* Tech startup programmes: Apply directly at mdec.my or via programme-specific portals

### What to Prepare (varies by programme)

* SSM registration certificate
* Business profile and website / social media presence
* Financial statements
* Evidence of digital readiness or gap (for digitalisation grants)
* Pitch deck (for acceleration programmes)

## How to Approach MSME Support — A Practical Framework

With so many agencies and programmes, the risk is paralysis by information overload. A practical approach:

```
Step 1: Determine your eligibility baseline
        → Bumiputera: MARA, TEKUN, PUNB, SME Corp, MDEC all potentially apply
        → Non-Bumiputera: SME Corp and MDEC are primary government channels
        → Indian entrepreneur: TEKUN Skim TUBE is specifically available

Step 2: Identify your most urgent need
        → Need financing now: TEKUN (micro), MARA (established), SME Corp (via CGC)
        → Need to go digital: MDEC / SME Digitalisation Grant
        → Need training / advisory: SME Corp advisory, MARA courses
        → Need market access: PUNB vendor programme, MDEC digital export

Step 3: Visit the SME Portal (smecorp.gov.my)
        → Use the programme directory to filter by eligibility and need
        → This saves visiting multiple agency portals individually

Step 4: Contact the relevant agency directly
        → Free advisory consultations are available at most agency offices
        → Bring your SSM certificate and a one-page business summary

Step 5: Prepare documentation properly
        → Most applications fail due to incomplete documentation, not ineligibility
```

## What to Prepare — Universal Checklist

Most MSME programme applications require some version of this:

| Document | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| SSM registration certificate | Current and not expired |
| MyKad (applicant) | Owner / director's IC |
| Bank statements | 3–6 months of business account |
| Financial statements | Latest audited or management accounts |
| Business plan | Can be simple — 5–10 pages covering product, market, financials |
| Quotations or invoices | For equipment or specific purpose financing |
| Photos of business premises | Interior and exterior |
| Tenancy agreement | If applicable |

**The business plan does not need to be a formal consultant-prepared document.** A clear, honest description of what the business does, who it serves, how it makes money, and how the support will be used is sufficient for most micro and small business applications.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: SME Digitalisation Grant underused**

A small accounting firm in Melaka was manually managing client files and billing with spreadsheets. A friend mentioned the SME Digitalisation Grant. The firm applied for RM 5,000 in matching funds to adopt cloud accounting software — reducing administrative time by 40%. The grant had been available for two years before they heard about it.

**Scenario 2: TEKUN for informal trader going formal**

A Bumiputera kuih seller had been operating informally for years. With a RM 5,000 TEKUN micro loan — which did not require SSM registration at that loan amount — she upgraded her equipment, registered with SSM using the income, and reapplied for a larger loan the following year. TEKUN's accessibility to informal businesses was the entry point she needed.

**Scenario 3: Wrong agency, wasted time**

A Chinese-Malaysian food business owner spent three weeks preparing documents for a MARA financing application after seeing an advertisement. He was not eligible — MARA is Bumiputera-only. Checking eligibility first (a two-minute review of the agency's criteria) would have redirected him to SME Corp or bank SME financing immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a non-Bumiputera apply for TEKUN?**
TEKUN's main schemes are for Bumiputera entrepreneurs. However, the **Skim TUBE (Tabung Usahawan India Bumiputera)** is specifically for Indian Malaysian entrepreneurs. Non-Bumiputera Chinese and other Malaysians should look to SME Corp, MDEC, and commercial bank SME financing.

**Q: Do I need a formal business plan for a TEKUN micro loan?**
For the smallest TEKUN amounts (RM 1,000–RM 10,000), a detailed business plan is not required. A simple description of the business and how the loan will be used is typically sufficient. Larger amounts require more documentation.

**Q: Are grants repayable?**
No — grants are non-repayable funding. Loans must be repaid on the agreed schedule. Read your programme offer carefully to confirm whether it is a grant or a loan. See **Section 6.6** for a detailed breakdown of grants and funding types.

**Q: What if my business has been rejected for MSME financing before?**
Common reasons for rejection include incomplete documentation, insufficient business history, poor credit history, or mismatched programme eligibility. SME Corp's free advisory service can help identify the root cause and suggest alternatives. Credit Guarantee Corporation (CGC) schemes are specifically designed for businesses that cannot meet standard bank collateral requirements.

**Q: Is there a single portal that shows all MSME programmes?**
Yes — **smecorp.gov.my** is the most comprehensive. BNM also maintains an SME financing directory at bnm.gov.my/sme covering bank and government financing options.
