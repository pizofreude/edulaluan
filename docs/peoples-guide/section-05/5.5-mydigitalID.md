### 🧒 Simple

**What is MyDigital ID?**

MyDigital ID is a digital version of your identity — stored on your phone.

Instead of showing your physical MyKad to prove who you are, you can use the app to verify your identity online or at a counter.

It uses your face and fingerprint to confirm you are the real owner of the identity.

Think of it as your MyKad — but on your phone, for online use.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

MyDigital ID is Malaysia's national digital identity platform, developed under the Malaysia Digital Economy Corporation (MDEC) and managed by Pos Digicert — a subsidiary of Pos Malaysia and a licensed Certification Authority.

It is part of Malaysia's broader push toward a unified digital identity infrastructure — reducing reliance on photocopies of MyKad, manual verification counters, and paper-based KYC (Know Your Customer) processes.

As of 2025, MyDigital ID is functional and growing in adoption — but rollout across government agencies and private sector entities remains uneven. This page reflects the current state honestly.

## Part 1: What MyDigital ID Is and How It Works

### The Digital Identity Stack

Malaysia's digital identity infrastructure has three layers that work together:

```
MyKad (physical chip)
    ↓ biometric anchor
MyDigital ID (digital credential)
    ↓ authentication layer
eKYC (remote verification process)
```

| Layer | What It Is | Who Manages It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MyKad chip | Physical biometric anchor — stores fingerprints and PKI certificate | JPN |
| MyDigital ID | Digital credential derived from MyKad biometrics | Pos Digicert / MDEC |
| eKYC | Process of verifying identity remotely using facial recognition + liveness check | Implemented by individual agencies and banks |

### How MyDigital ID Works

1. You register using your MyKad and biometrics
2. A digital credential is issued and stored in the MyDigital ID app
3. When a service requires identity verification, you authenticate via the app using your face or fingerprint
4. The app cryptographically confirms your identity to the requesting service — without transmitting raw biometric data

The underlying technology uses **Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)** — the same standard used in banking and secure communications globally.

### What It Is Not

* Not a replacement for physical MyKad in all contexts — some transactions still require the original
* Not a social login (like "Sign in with Google") — it is a verified legal identity credential
* Not fully deployed across all government services as of 2025

## Part 2: How to Register and Set Up

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Malaysian citizen | MyKad required |
| Age | 18 and above (minors via MyKid have limited access) |
| Smartphone | iOS 12+ or Android 8+ |
| Internet connection | Required for registration and authentication |
| Physical MyKad | Required during registration for chip scan |

### Registration Process

1. Download the **MyDigital ID app** from App Store or Google Play
2. Open the app and select **Register**
3. Scan your MyKad using your phone's NFC sensor (the card chip is read contactlessly)
4. Complete liveness check — facial scan confirming you are physically present
5. Set a PIN or biometric unlock for the app
6. Digital credential is issued and stored in the app

**NFC is required** — phones without NFC cannot complete MyKad chip scanning. Check your phone's specifications before attempting registration. Most mid-range and flagship smartphones from 2018 onwards support NFC.

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Issue | Common Cause | Resolution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NFC scan fails | Phone case blocking NFC, or NFC not enabled | Remove case; enable NFC in phone settings |
| Liveness check fails | Poor lighting, glasses, or camera quality | Ensure good lighting, remove glasses if needed |
| MyKad chip not detected | Damaged chip or older MyKad | Visit JPN for MyKad replacement |
| App not available in region | Store region settings | Ensure App Store / Play Store is set to Malaysia |

## Part 3: eKYC — What It Is and Where It Is Used

### What eKYC Means

eKYC (electronic Know Your Customer) is the process of verifying a person's identity remotely — without physical document submission or counter visits.

It typically involves:

* Submitting a photo or scan of an identity document (MyKad)
* A liveness check (facial recognition confirming a live person, not a photo)
* Matching the face to the identity document photo

eKYC is the verification **process**. MyDigital ID is one of the **credentials** used in that process.

### Where eKYC Is Used in Malaysia

| Sector | Common Use Cases |
| --- | --- |
| Banking and finance | New account opening, loan applications, credit card applications |
| Insurance and takaful | Policy applications and claims |
| Telecommunications | SIM card registration and postpaid sign-up |
| E-wallets and fintech | Account registration (TNG, GrabPay, Boost, etc.) |
| Government services | Selected JPN, Imigresen, and LHDN processes |
| Property | Tenancy agreement signing and identity verification |
| Securities | Brokerage account opening (Bursa-linked brokers) |

### eKYC with MyDigital ID vs Standard eKYC

Not all eKYC processes use MyDigital ID specifically. Many institutions implement their own eKYC using MyKad photo upload and selfie matching.

| Method | Uses MyDigital ID? | Security Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MyDigital ID authentication | Yes | Highest — chip-backed biometric |
| MyKad photo upload + selfie | No — proprietary eKYC | Medium — image-based |
| Manual IC submission + counter | No | Standard physical KYC |

When a service specifically requests **MyDigital ID**, it is requesting the chip-backed cryptographic credential — a higher assurance level than a photo upload.

## Part 5: Privacy and Security Considerations

### Where Your Data Lives

| Data Type | Where Stored |
| --- | --- |
| Biometric fingerprints | On your MyKad chip — not on any server |
| Facial recognition template | Generated locally during liveness check; not permanently stored by the app |
| Digital credential (PKI certificate) | In the MyDigital ID app on your device |
| Authentication logs | On Pos Digicert's servers (as the Certification Authority) |

The design intentionally keeps raw biometrics off central servers — a meaningful privacy safeguard. However, authentication event logs (who you authenticated with and when) are retained by the CA.

### PDPA and Your Digital Identity

The Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) applies to how organisations use data collected via eKYC and MyDigital ID:

* Organisations must state why they are collecting your identity data
* They may not use it for purposes beyond what was declared
* You have the right to request access to and correction of your data

For government bodies, PDPA coverage has historically had carve-outs — a gap that has been debated in parliament. Be aware that government agencies handling your MyDigital ID data may operate under different data governance rules than private entities.

Cross-reference: **Section 8.4.2 — Data Privacy and PDPA**

### Security Best Practices

| Practice | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Set a strong PIN for the MyDigital ID app | Prevents access if phone is stolen |
| Enable phone biometric lock (fingerprint/face) | Second layer of protection |
| Do not share your MyDigital ID QR code or screenshots | QR codes are single-use but sharing them is risky |
| Report lost or stolen phones to Pos Digicert | Allows credential suspension |
| Keep the app updated | Security patches are released periodically |

### Honest Assessment of Current Limitations

MyDigital ID is a well-designed system that has not yet reached its full potential due to uneven adoption:

| Limitation | Current Status |
| --- | --- |
| Not all agencies support it | Many government portals still use older MyKad number + password authentication |
| NFC dependency excludes some users | Older or budget phones without NFC cannot register |
| Private sector integration is patchy | Banks and telcos have varying levels of support |
| Awareness is low | Many Malaysians have not heard of it or do not know how to use it |
| Non-citizens excluded | Permanent residents and foreigners cannot use MyDigital ID as it is MyKad-tied |

This does not make MyDigital ID not worth setting up — it does mean managing expectations about where it will and will not work today.

## Practical Habits for Digital Identity

Malaysians who manage their digital identity well tend to:

* Set up MyDigital ID proactively — before they need it urgently
* Check whether their phone supports NFC before attempting registration
* Use MyDigital ID wherever it is accepted for the highest security assurance
* Keep a digital scan of their MyKad in secure cloud storage as a backup for eKYC processes that accept document uploads
* Never share IC numbers, MyDigital ID credentials, or eKYC selfies via unverified channels or in response to unsolicited requests

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Bank account opened in 10 minutes**

A graduate opened a new bank account entirely via the bank's app — eKYC with a MyKad photo and selfie, no branch visit required. The process that once required a half-day at a branch took under 15 minutes from her bedroom.

**Scenario 2: MyDigital ID blocked a fraud attempt**

A man received a call from someone claiming to be his bank asking him to verify his identity via a link. The link requested a selfie and IC upload — standard-looking but unsolicited. Knowing that legitimate banks use their own verified eKYC flows (not ad-hoc links), he declined and reported to the bank. His identity was not compromised.

**Scenario 3: Uneven rollout caused confusion**

A civil servant tried to use her MyDigital ID to authenticate a government portal transaction. The portal did not support MyDigital ID yet — it still required the older username/IC number login. Understanding the current state of rollout would have saved frustration.

## FAQ

**Q: Is MyDigital ID the same as MyKad?**
No. MyKad is the physical identity card issued by JPN. MyDigital ID is a digital credential derived from your MyKad biometrics, managed by Pos Digicert. They are linked but separate.

**Q: My phone does not have NFC — can I still register?**
No. NFC is required to scan the MyKad chip during registration. If your phone does not support NFC, you cannot currently register for MyDigital ID. Upgrading to an NFC-capable device is the only option.

**Q: Is MyDigital ID mandatory?**
No. It is voluntary. Physical MyKad and traditional KYC processes remain valid for all services.

**Q: Can permanent residents or foreigners use MyDigital ID?**
No. MyDigital ID is tied to the MyKad chip, which is only issued to Malaysian citizens and selected permanent residents (MyPR). eKYC processes using passport-based verification are available at some institutions for foreigners.

**Q: How is MyDigital ID different from just uploading a photo of my IC?**
A MyDigital ID authentication is chip-backed and cryptographically signed — it proves the credential comes from the actual MyKad chip, not a forged or photographed document. A photo upload is easier to fake and provides a lower assurance level. Where both are accepted, MyDigital ID is the more secure option.
