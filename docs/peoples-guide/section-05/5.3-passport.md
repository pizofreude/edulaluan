## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is a Malaysian passport?**

A passport is your identity document for international travel.

It proves you are a Malaysian citizen when you are outside Malaysia.

You need a passport for all international travel except crossing to Singapore via the Causeway or Second Link (where MyKad suffices for Malaysians).

Your passport must be valid — most countries require at least 6 months of validity remaining when you enter.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

Malaysian passports are issued by **Jabatan Imigresen Malaysia (Imigresen)** under the Home Ministry. JPN's role is limited to verifying citizenship status — the actual passport document, biometric capture, and issuance are handled entirely by Imigresen.

This page is not on MyGOV. Passport services must be accessed via:

* **imi.gov.my** — Imigresen's main portal
* **MyImigresen app** — iOS and Android
* **Imigresen counters** nationwide

The Malaysian passport is an **ePassport** — it contains a biometric chip compliant with ICAO standards, enabling e-gate and ASEAN lane access at participating airports.

### What It Contains

| Feature | Detail |

| Biometric chip | Stores digitised photo and two fingerprints |

| Validity | 5 years (standard); 2 years (children under 12) |
| Pages | 36 pages (standard); 64 pages (frequent traveller — same price) |
| Colour | Maroon / dark red cover |

### ePassport Features at Airports

| Feature | Where Available | How It Works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Automated e-gate | KLIA, klia2, Penang, Langkawi, JB Senai, Kuching, KK | Chip scan + fingerprint verification — no officer needed |
| ASEAN lane | Participating ASEAN airports | Dedicated fast lane for ASEAN passport holders |
| Visa-free travel | 180+ countries | Malaysian passport consistently ranked among strongest in Asia |

**ASEAN lane access** is available at airports in Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, Brunei, Philippines, and others — look for the ASEAN logo at immigration counters. Requires a valid ePassport.

## Part 2: New Passport Application (First Passport)

### Who Needs to Apply

* Any Malaysian citizen who has never held a passport
* Children of any age (no minimum age — infant passports are available)
* Individuals whose previous passport has expired and cannot be renewed (rare cases)

### Required Documents

| Applicant | Documents Required |
| --- | --- |
| Adult (18 and above) | MyKad (original), completed application form |
| Minor (12–17) | MyKad, parent/guardian MyKad, birth certificate, parent/guardian present |
| Child under 12 | MyKid, parent/guardian MyKad, birth certificate, parent/guardian present |
| Infant (no MyKid yet) | Birth certificate, parent/guardian MyKad, statutory declaration if only one parent present |

### Application Process

1. Book an appointment via **imi.gov.my** or **MyImigresen app**
2. Attend the Imigresen office on your appointment date
3. Submit documents at the counter
4. Biometrics captured (photo and fingerprints) at the counter
5. Pay the fee
6. Collect passport — standard: **1 working day** at most major offices; up to **3 working days** at smaller offices

### Fees

| Passport Type | Fee |
| --- | --- |
| 36-page passport (5 years, adult) | RM 200 |
| 64-page passport (5 years, adult) | RM 200 (same price) |
| Child passport (2 years, under 12) | RM 100 |
| 12–17 years (5 years) | RM 200 |

## Part 3: Renewal

### When to Renew

| Situation | Recommended Action |
| --- | --- |
| Less than 6 months validity remaining | Renew before travelling internationally |
| Less than 12 months validity remaining | Renew soon — some countries require more than 6 months |
| All pages used (visas/stamps) | Apply for new passport even if validity remains |
| Personal details changed (name) | Renew and update simultaneously |

**Do not wait until your passport expires.** Many airlines and immigration authorities will deny boarding if your passport has less than 6 months validity — regardless of visa status.

### Renewal Process

Identical to a new application — biometrics are recaptured at every renewal.

1. Book appointment via **imi.gov.my** or **MyImigresen app**
2. Bring current (expiring) passport and MyKad
3. Biometrics recaptured at counter
4. Pay RM 200
5. Collect next working day (most major offices)

Old passport is returned to you, cancelled — keep it if it contains valid visas.

### Online Pre-Registration

Imigresen offers online pre-registration to reduce counter time:

* Complete personal details online via MyImigresen app before your appointment
* Biometrics still captured in person — fully online issuance is not available
* Pre-registration reduces counter processing time by approximately 10–15 minutes

## Part 4: Child Passport

### Key Points

* Children of any age may hold a passport — there is no minimum age
* **Children cannot be added to a parent's passport** — each traveller requires their own document
* Child passports are valid for **2 years** (under 12) then transition to a 5-year adult passport at age 12
* Both parents' consent is generally required for a child's passport — if only one parent is present, a statutory declaration or supporting documentation explaining the other parent's absence is required

### Solo Travel by Minors

A minor travelling internationally without both parents may be required to present:

* A letter of consent from the absent parent(s)
* Parent/guardian contact details
* Supporting documentation (in some destinations)

Requirements vary by destination country and airline. Check with the destination country's embassy and your airline before travel.

### Passport for Newborns

Newborns can receive a passport before their MyKid is issued:

* Bring birth certificate and both parents' MyKad
* If only one parent is present, bring a statutory declaration
* Passport valid for 2 years

## Part 5: Lost or Damaged Passport

### If Lost in Malaysia

1. **File a police report (Laporan Polis)** at the nearest police station
2. Visit an Imigresen office with the police report and MyKad
3. Complete a lost passport declaration form at the counter
4. Pay the replacement fee
5. New passport issued — typically next working day

**Fee for lost passport replacement:** RM 300 (higher than standard renewal as a deterrent against carelessness)

### If Lost Abroad

1. Report to the nearest local police station (get a written report)
2. Contact the **nearest Malaysian Embassy or Consulate** immediately
3. The embassy will issue an **Emergency Travel Document (ETD)** or an emergency passport for your return to Malaysia
4. On return, apply for a full replacement at Imigresen

**Malaysian Embassy emergency contacts:** Available at **kln.gov.my** (Ministry of Foreign Affairs directory)

### If Damaged

Bring the damaged passport to any Imigresen office. A damaged passport is treated similarly to a lost passport:

* Fee: RM 300 if damage is determined to be due to negligence
* RM 200 if damage is production-related (chip failure, printing defect)

Keep passports away from water, heat, and bending — chip damage is not always visible but renders the ePassport non-functional at e-gates.

## Part 6: Emergency / Urgent Passport

### What It Is

An emergency passport is issued for urgent travel needs when standard processing time is insufficient.

| Feature | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Processing time | Same day or next day |
| Valid for | As per standard passport (5 years adult, 2 years child) |
| Available at | Selected major Imigresen offices only |
| Surcharge | Additional fee applies on top of standard passport fee |

### When It Applies

* Medical emergency requiring overseas treatment
* Death of immediate family member abroad
* Urgent business travel with documented need
* Imminent departure with passport not yet ready

### How to Apply

1. Visit a major Imigresen office in person — **no appointment available for emergency applications**
2. Bring supporting documentation (e.g. hospital letter, flight itinerary, death certificate)
3. Explain the urgency at the counter — a supervisor will assess
4. Pay standard fee plus emergency surcharge
5. Collect same day or as confirmed by the officer

**Note:** Emergency processing is at the discretion of the Imigresen officer. Documentation supporting the urgency significantly improves the outcome.

## JPN vs Imigresen — Who Does What

A common source of confusion:

| Task | Agency | Where to Go |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Citizenship verification | JPN | jpn.gov.my |
| Birth registration | JPN | jpn.gov.my |
| MyKad / MyKid issuance | JPN | jpn.gov.my |
| Passport issuance and renewal | Imigresen | imi.gov.my |
| Visa and entry permits | Imigresen | imi.gov.my |
| Foreign worker passes | Imigresen | imi.gov.my |
| Permanent Resident status | Imigresen | imi.gov.my |

JPN establishes your citizenship. Imigresen manages your travel and entry documents. They are separate agencies with separate counters — do not go to JPN for a passport.

## Practical Habits for Passport Management

Malaysians who manage travel documents well tend to:

* Check passport validity 12 months before any planned international trip
* Request the 64-page passport — it costs the same as 36-page and lasts longer for frequent travellers
* Keep a photocopy and digital scan of their passport stored separately and in cloud storage
* Register with the Malaysian Embassy or High Commission when living or travelling extensively abroad
* Carry their old cancelled passport when travelling if it contains valid visas for destinations they plan to visit

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Denied boarding due to passport validity**

A family travelling to Japan for a holiday was denied boarding at KLIA. The father's passport had 4 months remaining — Japan requires 6 months validity. An earlier renewal check would have caught this. The trip was delayed by 3 days while an urgent renewal was processed.

**Scenario 2: Child added to parent passport**

A parent assumed his newborn could travel on her passport without a separate document, as he had heard this was possible decades ago. Malaysian passports no longer allow child endorsement — the child needed her own passport. Knowing the current rule before the trip saved a last-minute scramble.

**Scenario 3: Lost passport handled smoothly abroad**

A student in the UK lost her passport. She filed a local police report, contacted the Malaysian High Commission in London, and received an Emergency Travel Document within 48 hours to return home. Knowing the High Commission contact and process in advance made a stressful situation manageable.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use my MyKad instead of a passport to travel internationally?**
Only for crossing to Singapore via the Johor Causeway and Second Link on land. For all other international destinations — including Singapore by air or sea — a valid passport is required.

**Q: How long does passport renewal take?**
Standard processing at major Imigresen offices is 1 working day. Smaller offices may take up to 3 working days. Emergency processing is same-day at selected offices with supporting documentation.

**Q: Can I travel if my passport expires in 3 months?**
Most countries require a minimum of 6 months validity. Some require more. Check the destination country's entry requirements before travelling. Airline check-in staff will also flag insufficient validity.

**Q: Do I need to be present in person to renew my passport?**
Yes — biometric capture (photo and fingerprints) is required in person at every application and renewal. There is no fully postal or online option.

**Q: What is the difference between a 36-page and 64-page passport?**
The only difference is the number of pages for visa stamps and entry/exit endorsements. Both cost RM 200 and are valid for 5 years. Frequent travellers or those applying for many visas should request the 64-page version.
