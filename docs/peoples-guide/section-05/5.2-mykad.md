## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is MyKad?**

MyKad is Malaysia's national identity card.

Every Malaysian citizen aged 12 and above must have one and carry it at all times.

It contains:

* Your name, photo, and IC number
* A chip with personal and biometric data
* Information used for healthcare, voting, driving, and more

Losing it or not renewing it on time can cause problems with many services.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

MyKad was introduced in 2001, replacing the older laminated identity card (Kad Pengenalan Lama). It is issued and managed by the **Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara (JPN)** — the National Registration Department.

MyKad is more than an identity document. Its embedded chip serves as:

* A healthcare identifier (linked to KKM records)
* A driving licence (optional feature)
* A Touch 'n Go e-purse (optional feature)
* An ATM card (optional, via selected banks)
* A PKI digital certificate carrier (for digital signatures)

It is the most important identity document a Malaysian citizen carries.

## Part 1: What MyKad Contains

### Visible Information (Card Surface)

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Full name | As registered with JPN |
| IC number | 12-digit unique identifier (YYMMDD-SS-NNNN) |
| Date of birth | Embedded in IC number |
| Gender | Encoded in last digit of IC number (odd = male, even = female) |
| Nationality | Malaysian Citizen |
| State of birth | Encoded in digits 7–8 of IC number |
| Photo | Updated at each renewal |
| Religion | Shown for Muslims; blank for non-Muslims |

### IC Number Structure

```
YYMMDD - SS - NNNN

YY = birth year (last 2 digits)
MM = birth month
DD = birth date
SS = state of birth / country code (for those born abroad)
NNNN = unique sequence number (last digit odd = male, even = female)
```

### Chip Data

The MyKad chip stores:

* Biometric fingerprint data (two fingerprints)
* Digitised photo
* Personal health information (blood type, medical alerts)
* PKI digital certificate
* Optional: driving licence, Touch 'n Go, bank card data

## Part 2: MyKad Variants

### MyKid (Children under 12)

| Item | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Who | Malaysian citizens from birth to 11 years old |
| Purpose | Identity document for children; required for school enrolment, medical, travel |
| Application | At JPN, within 1 year of birth recommended |
| Transition | Automatically replaced by MyKad at age 12 |
| Cost | Free for first application; replacement fees apply |

### MyTentera (Armed Forces)

| Item | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Who | Active members of the Malaysian Armed Forces (ATM) |
| Purpose | Combines MyKad with military service identification |
| Features | Same as MyKad plus armed forces service data |
| Issued by | ATM in coordination with JPN |

### MyPR (Permanent Residents)

| Item | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Who | Non-citizens holding Malaysian Permanent Resident status |
| Purpose | Identity and residency document for PRs |
| Appearance | Similar to MyKad but marked "Pemastautin Tetap" |
| Rights | Grants access to some (not all) citizen services |
| Limitations | Cannot vote, cannot hold certain government positions |
| Renewal | Required when PR status is renewed or personal details change |

### MyKAS (Temporary Residents — Legacy)

Older identity document for long-term temporary residents. Being phased out in favour of updated immigration documentation. Holders should check their status with JPN and Imigresen.

## Part 3: Renewal and Replacement

### When to Renew or Apply

| Situation | Action Required |
| --- | --- |
| Child turns 12 | Apply for MyKad (replaces MyKid) |
| Name change (marriage, legal) | Update MyKad |
| Address change | Update MyKad (optional but recommended) |
| Damaged card | Replace MyKad |
| Lost card | Report to police + replace at JPN |
| Chip malfunction | Replace at JPN |
| Expiry (MyKad does not expire for citizens) | No renewal unless details change |

**MyKad for Malaysian citizens does not expire.** There is no periodic renewal requirement unless your personal details change or the card is damaged or lost.

### Application Process

All MyKad applications require a **physical visit to a JPN counter**. There is no fully online application option.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Locate your nearest JPN office: **jpn.gov.my** or via MyGOV
2. Book an appointment via JPN's online appointment system (recommended to avoid long queues)
3. Bring required documents (see below)
4. Biometrics (fingerprints and photo) are captured at the counter
5. MyKad is typically ready for collection within **5–7 working days**
6. Collect in person at the JPN counter where you applied

### Required Documents

| Situation | Documents Needed |
| --- | --- |
| First application (age 12) | Birth certificate, school letter, parent/guardian present |
| Replacement (lost) | Police report (Laporan Polis), birth certificate or old IC |
| Replacement (damaged) | Damaged MyKad, birth certificate or supporting document |
| Name change | Court order or marriage certificate, current MyKad |
| Address change | Current MyKad, proof of new address (utility bill, tenancy agreement) |

### Fees

| Application Type | Fee |
| --- | --- |
| First MyKad (age 12) | Free |
| Replacement — lost | RM 10 (first replacement per year) |
| Replacement — lost (subsequent) | RM 20 |
| Replacement — damaged | RM 10 |
| MyKid (first application) | Free |
| MyKid replacement | RM 10 |

Fee waivers may apply for low-income applicants. Ask at the JPN counter.

### Express and Mobile Services

| Service | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| JPN Express (Hospital) | Available at major government hospitals for newborn registration and bedridden patients |
| JPN Mobile Unit | Deployed periodically to rural areas and Orang Asli communities — check JPN announcements |
| Sabah and Sarawak | Additional JPN offices in major towns; appointment booking via jpn.gov.my |

## Part 4: Uses of MyKad

MyKad touches almost every formal interaction in Malaysian life.

### Government and Civil Services

| Use | How MyKad Is Used |
| --- | --- |
| Voting | Electoral roll verification at polling station |
| Healthcare (Klinik Kesihatan) | Patient registration and RM 1 fee eligibility |
| Tax filing | Taxpayer identification (LHDN) |
| EPF / SOCSO | Contributor identification |
| Driving licence | Optional integration on MyKad chip |
| Civil service applications | SPA / JPA identity verification |
| Court proceedings | Legal identity verification |
| Welfare assistance | JKM and BSH eligibility verification |

### Financial and Commercial

| Use | How MyKad Is Used |
| --- | --- |
| Bank account opening | KYC (Know Your Customer) identity verification |
| Loan and credit applications | Credit bureau identity check (CCRIS, CTOS) |
| Insurance and takaful | Policyholder identity |
| Property transactions | Buyer / seller identity at land office |
| SIM card registration | MCMC-required identity verification |
| e-Wallet registration | Identity verification (TNG, GrabPay, etc.) |

### Travel

MyKad is accepted for travel between Malaysia and Singapore via the Johor Causeway and Second Link — no passport required for Malaysian citizens using these land crossings. For all other international travel, a passport is required.

## Part 5: Lost or Damaged MyKad — What to Do

### If Lost

1. **File a police report (Laporan Polis)** at the nearest police station — this is required for the JPN replacement application
2. Keep a copy of the police report
3. Visit JPN with the police report and your birth certificate
4. Pay RM 10 replacement fee
5. Collect new MyKad within 5–7 working days

**Carry the police report receipt** as temporary identity proof until your new MyKad is ready.

### If Damaged

1. Bring the damaged MyKad to JPN — do not discard it
2. JPN staff will assess whether it is a damage replacement or chip replacement
3. Pay RM 10 fee
4. Collect new MyKad within 5–7 working days

### Preventing Identity Fraud After Loss

After reporting a lost MyKad:

* Notify your bank and monitor your accounts for unusual activity
* Alert your telco — a lost IC can be used to port or register SIM cards fraudulently
* Check your CCRIS report (via BNM) if you suspect misuse for loan applications
* Report suspected identity fraud to PDRM's Commercial Crime Investigation Department (CCID)

## Practical Habits Around MyKad

Malaysians who manage their MyKad well tend to:

* Keep a photocopy of their MyKad stored separately from the card itself
* Book JPN appointments online rather than walking in — saves 1–2 hours on average
* Update their MyKad address after moving — some services (postal delivery, electoral roll) are tied to the registered address
* Ensure their child's MyKid is applied for within the first year of life — delays affect school enrolment and medical records
* Never share their IC number via WhatsApp or unverified websites — it is the primary key for identity fraud

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Lost MyKad before a critical appointment**

A job applicant lost her MyKad two days before a civil service interview requiring original IC. She filed a police report the same day, visited JPN with an appointment the next morning, and collected her replacement card on the fourth day — just in time. Knowing the process in advance made all the difference.

**Scenario 2: Child's MyKid delayed, school enrolment affected**

A family did not apply for their child's MyKid until age 6, assuming it was optional. The missing document created complications at primary school enrolment where MyKid is a required document. The application was processed in time but caused unnecessary stress.

**Scenario 3: Lost IC used for SIM fraud**

A man lost his MyKad and did not report it promptly. Two weeks later, he received a bill for a SIM card registered in his name at a postpaid telco. The fraud was reported to PDRM and the telco, and resolved — but a prompt police report and telco alert would have prevented it entirely.

### Path A: My child is turning 12 — how do I get their MyKad?

**Step 1:** Book an appointment at your nearest JPN office via **jpn.gov.my**.

**Step 2:** Bring the child's birth certificate, current MyKid, and parent/guardian MyKad.

**Step 3:** Attend appointment together — child's biometrics will be captured.

---

### Path B: I lost my MyKad

**Step 1:** File a police report at the nearest police station.

**Step 2:** Book a JPN appointment via **jpn.gov.my**.

**Step 3:** Bring police report and birth certificate to JPN.

**Step 4:** Pay RM 10 replacement fee.

**Step 5:** Collect within 5–7 working days. Carry the police report as temporary ID.

---

### Path C: I want to update my address on MyKad

Visit JPN with your current MyKad and proof of new address (utility bill or tenancy agreement).

No police report needed for address update.

Fee: Free for address update.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it illegal not to carry my MyKad?**
Yes. Under the National Registration Act 1959, Malaysians aged 12 and above are legally required to carry their MyKad at all times. Failure to produce it when required by an authorised officer is an offence.

**Q: Can I use a photocopy of my MyKad as identification?**
Generally no for formal transactions — banks, government counters, and most official processes require the original. A photocopy may be accepted as supporting documentation in limited situations.

**Q: Does MyKad expire?**
For Malaysian citizens, MyKad does not have an expiry date and does not require periodic renewal. MyPR cards may have expiry tied to PR status.

**Q: Can I add or remove the driving licence or Touch 'n Go feature?**
The driving licence integration is managed by JPJ. Touch 'n Go integration can be activated or deactivated at Touch 'n Go service centres. Neither affects the core MyKad identity function.

**Q: What if I was born abroad — is my IC number different?**
Yes. Digits 7–8 of your IC number reflect the country code for those born overseas rather than a Malaysian state code. The rest of the card functions identically.
