## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is MyGOV?**

MyGOV is a website and app run by the Malaysian government.

Think of it as a directory and front door to government services online.

Instead of searching for each agency separately, you can find many of them in one place.

Examples of what you can do via MyGOV:

* Renew your road tax or driving licence (via JPJ)
* Check summons
* Apply for permits and licences
* Pay government bills
* Find government job vacancies

You still interact with the actual agency — MyGOV connects you to them.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

MyGOV (mygov.my) is Malaysia's federal e-government portal, administered by the Malaysian Administrative Modernisation and Management Planning Unit (MAMPU) under the Prime Minister's Department.

Its purpose is to serve as a **single point of access** to government services — reducing the need for citizens to know which agency handles which service and navigate multiple separate portals.

It is a directory and gateway, not a service provider itself. Transactions happen within the linked agency's own system — MyGOV routes you there.

## Part 1: What MyGOV Aggregates

MyGOV connects to services from across the federal government. The breadth is significant but uneven — some agencies are deeply integrated, others are linked superficially via a redirect.

### Major Service Categories on MyGOV

| Category | What Is Accessible | Key Agencies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identity and citizenship | MyKad renewal, birth certificate, citizenship applications | JPN (Jabatan Pendaftaran Negara) |
| Transport | Road tax renewal, driving licence, summons check and payment | JPJ, PDRM |
| Business | Company registration status, SSM searches, business licences | SSM, local authorities |
| Employment | Government job vacancies, civil service applications | SPA, JPA |
| Education | Scholarship applications, university applications | JPA, UPU, PTPTN |
| Health | Links to MySejahtera, KKM services, FOMEMA | KKM |

| Complaints | eAduan portal for government service complaints | MAMPU |

## Part 2: Key Services in Detail

### JPJ Services (Road Transport)

Among the most frequently used via MyGOV:

| Service | How to Access |
| --- | --- |
| Road tax (cukai jalan) renewal | MyGOV → Transport → JPJ → Road Tax Renewal |
| Driving licence renewal | MyGOV → Transport → JPJ → Driving Licence |
| Summons check | MyGOV → Transport → Semak Saman |
| Vehicle ownership transfer | MyGOV → Transport → JPJ (appointment required) |

Road tax renewal requires a valid vehicle inspection (Puspakom) certificate for vehicles over 5 years. MyGOV links to Puspakom booking as well.

### JPN Services (National Registration)

| Service | How to Access |
| --- | --- |
| MyKad renewal or replacement | MyGOV → Identity → JPN |
| Birth certificate application | MyGOV → Identity → JPN |
| Death registration | MyGOV → Identity → JPN |
| Name change application | MyGOV → Identity → JPN |

Most JPN transactions still require a physical visit to a JPN counter. MyGOV facilitates appointment booking and form downloads.

Cross-reference: **Section 5.2 — MyKad: Renewal, Replacement, and Uses**

### LHDN (Tax Filing)

MyGOV links directly to the MyTax portal:

* e-Filing for income tax
* CP58 and EA form retrieval
* Tax payment and instalment setup
* Amendment submissions

Cross-reference: **Section 3.1 — LHDN and e-Filing**

### SPA / JPA (Government Employment)

| Service | How to Access |
| --- | --- |
| Civil service job vacancies | MyGOV → Employment → SPA Online |
| Scholarship applications (JPA) | MyGOV → Education → JPA |
| Civil service exam registration | MyGOV → Employment → SPA |

Cross-reference: **Section 5.4 — JPA and Scholarships**, **Section 5.6 — Civil Service**

### eAduan (Government Complaints)

MyGOV hosts the federal **eAduan** portal for complaints about government services:

* Submit complaints about any federal agency
* Track complaint status
* Escalate unresolved issues

Access: **mygov.my → eAduan** or directly at **eaduan.mampu.gov.my**

This is distinct from sector-specific complaint channels (MCMC for telcos, BNM for financial services, KKM for healthcare). eAduan covers general public service delivery issues.

## Part 3: How to Navigate and Use the Portal

### Web Portal (mygov.my)

The web portal is organised by:

* **Life events** — e.g. "Having a Baby", "Starting a Business", "Retiring" — grouping relevant services by situation rather than agency
* **Agency directory** — direct links to agency portals if you know which agency you need
* **Search** — keyword search across services

**Practical tip:** The life events approach is more useful than searching by agency if you are unsure which government body handles your need.

### Authentication

Most transactional services require authentication via:

| Method | Used For |
| --- | --- |
| MyKad number + personal details | Basic identity verification on agency portals |
| MyDigital ID | Stronger authentication for sensitive transactions |
| Singpass-style login (in development) | Future unified login across agencies |

Authentication experience varies by agency — some have their own login systems that MyGOV simply redirects you to.

Cross-reference: **Section 5.5 — MyDigital ID and eKYC**

### Step-by-Step: Finding a Service

1. Go to **mygov.my**
2. Use the search bar or browse by life event category
3. Select the relevant service
4. You will be redirected to the agency's own portal or form
5. Complete the transaction on the agency's system
6. Return confirmation or receipt from the agency directly — not from MyGOV

## Part 4: MyGOV App vs Web Portal

| Feature | Web Portal (mygov.my) | MyGOV App (iOS / Android) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Service directory | Comprehensive | Subset of web portal |
| Notifications | No | Push notifications for applications |
| MyDigital ID integration | Partial | Yes |
| Offline access | No | Limited (cached content) |
| Best for | Full service browsing and transactions | Quick access and notifications |

The app is most useful for tracking application statuses and receiving government notifications. For full service navigation, the web portal is more complete.

Download: Search "MyGOV Malaysia" on App Store or Google Play.

## Part 5: Limitations and What MyGOV Does Not Cover

Understanding what MyGOV cannot do prevents frustration.

### Services Not on MyGOV

| Service | Where to Go Instead |
| --- | --- |
| Passport application and renewal | Imigresen Malaysia (imi.gov.my) — separate portal |
| SOCSO/PERKESO claims | perkeso.gov.my — separate portal |
| EPF transactions | kwsp.gov.my (i-Akaun) — separate portal |
| Zakat payment | State zakat authority portals (see Section 3.8) |
| BNM financial complaints | BNM TELELINK — separate channel |
| MCMC complaints (telco) | aduan.skmm.gov.my — separate portal |
| Court-related services | eCourt, e-Filing (court system) — separate |
| State government services | State portals (e.g. SELangkah for Selangor) — not on MyGOV |

### Coverage Gaps

* **State-level services** are not on MyGOV — each state government operates its own portal. Local council services (cukai pintu, assessment rates, licensing) must be accessed via the respective local authority.
* **Integration depth varies** — some agencies are fully transactional via MyGOV; others are listed but redirect to a PDF form or phone number.
* **Mobile experience is inconsistent** — some agency portals linked from MyGOV are not mobile-optimised.
* **Non-citizen services are limited** — most MyGOV services are MyKad-dependent. Foreigners with only a passport may find fewer services accessible.

## Practical Habits for Using MyGOV Well

Malaysians who get the most out of MyGOV tend to:

* Start with the life events approach rather than searching by agency name
* Bookmark frequently used agency portals directly after finding them via MyGOV — for future visits
* Use the MyGOV app for status notifications on pending applications
* Keep their MyDigital ID set up for faster authentication at agencies that support it
* Check both MyGOV and the specific agency's portal when a service is not easily found

**Scenario 1: Road tax renewed without leaving home**

A working parent renewed her car's road tax via MyGOV → JPJ in under 10 minutes during lunch. The physical Pos Malaysia queue she used to endure is now a last resort rather than a default.

**Scenario 2: Complaint tracked and resolved**

## Full Wizard Content (Modal / Sidebar)

### Path A: I want to renew my road tax

MyGOV → Transport → JPJ → Road Tax Renewal

Ensure your vehicle has a valid Puspakom inspection certificate if over 5 years old.

Have your vehicle registration number and MyKad ready.

Payment via FPX or online banking.

---

### Path B: I want to make a complaint about a government service

MyGOV → eAduan

Or go directly to: **eaduan.mampu.gov.my**

Select the agency involved, describe your issue, and submit.

You will receive a reference number — use it to track the complaint status.

---

### Path C: I cannot find the service I need on MyGOV

Check if the service is state-level — it will not be on MyGOV.

Check the agency's own website directly (e.g. imi.gov.my for immigration).

Call KKM Careline (1-800-88-1000) for health services, or the relevant agency helpline.

Use the eAduan portal if a government service is unclear or unresponsive.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to create an account on MyGOV?**
For browsing and finding services, no account is needed. For transactional services, authentication is handled by the individual agency portal you are redirected to — not by MyGOV itself.

**Q: Is MyGOV the same as MyDigital ID?**
No. MyGOV is the service portal. MyDigital ID is a digital identity credential used to authenticate yourself on government portals. They work together but are separate systems. See Section 5.5 for MyDigital ID.

**Q: Are all government services available online via MyGOV?**
No. Many services — especially those requiring physical documents, biometrics, or in-person verification (e.g. MyKad replacement, passport issuance) — still require a physical counter visit. MyGOV facilitates appointment booking and form preparation for these.

**Q: Can foreigners use MyGOV?**
Partially. Some services are accessible with a passport number. Most services require a MyKad. Foreign residents should check specific agency portals for services relevant to them.

**Q: What if I submit a complaint via eAduan and get no response?**
Follow up using your reference number. If still unresolved, escalate to the agency's head of department or write to MAMPU directly. For sector-specific issues, use the relevant regulator (MCMC, BNM, KKM) as a parallel channel.
