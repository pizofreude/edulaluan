## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is JKKP?**
JKKP stands for Jabatan Keselamatan dan Kesihatan Pekerjaan. In English it is called DOSH, the Department of Occupational Safety and Health. They make sure workplaces are safe for everyone. Think of them as the safety inspectors for all offices, factories, and worksites in Malaysia.

**What can JKKP help with?**
- Your workplace is dangerous and your employer is not fixing it.
- You were injured at work.
- Your workplace has chemicals or machines that are not being handled safely.
- You were exposed to something harmful at work and became sick.

**How do you contact JKKP?**
- Website: dosh.gov.my
- File a complaint online via the SISPAA system at dosh.gov.my
- Walk in to your nearest JKKP state office.

**Remember:**
- You have the right to a safe workplace. This is the law.
- Your employer cannot fire you for raising a genuine safety concern.

### 🧑 Detailed

**Overview**

JKKP/DOSH is a government agency under the Ministry of Human Resources. It is responsible for promoting and enforcing occupational safety and health matters as per approved legislation in Malaysia. The main objective of DOSH is to ensure a safe and healthy working environment for employees across various industries. They develop and enforce regulations, conduct inspections and audits, provide training and education, and collaborate with employers, employees, and other stakeholders to improve workplace safety.

JKKP is the Malay name for DOSH. JKKP stands for Jabatan Keselamatan dan Kesihatan Pekerjaan. They are the same department, just referred to in different languages.

**Key legislation enforced by JKKP:**

| Act or Regulation | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| Occupational Safety and Health Act 1994 (OSHA / Act 514) | Core workplace safety duties for employers and employees |
| Factories and Machinery Act 1967 (FMA) | Machinery, equipment, and factory premises |
| Petroleum (Safety Measures) Act 1984 | Petroleum industry safety |
| OSH (Amendment) Act 2022 | Extended coverage and new duties from June 2024 |

**Major 2024 update:**
The OSH Amendment Act 2022 came into effect on June 1, 2024, expanding the applicability of OSHA to all places of work throughout Malaysia with limited exceptions. Previously, OSHA applied only to specific industries such as manufacturing, mining, and construction. This means offices, retail shops, restaurants, and service businesses are now all covered.

**New duties introduced from June 2024:**
The OSH Amendment Act introduced new safety obligations including that a principal must ensure the safety and health of contractors, subcontractors, and their employees at the workplace.

**Key duties under OSHA 1994:**

| Who | Duties |
|---|---|
| Employer | Provide safe equipment, safe work systems, safe premises, adequate training, and a written OSH policy (for 5 or more employees) |
| Employee | Follow safety rules, use protective equipment, report hazards, cooperate with employer on OSH matters |
| Self-employed | Ensure own safety and the safety of others affected by their work |
| Principal / contractor | Ensure safety of all workers including subcontractors on site |

**Industries covered under JKKP:**
All sectors including manufacturing, mining and quarrying, construction, agriculture, forestry and fishing, utilities, storage and communication, wholesale and retail trades, hotel and restaurant, and finance, insurance, real estate and business services are governed by OSHA 1994. From June 2024, coverage extends further to all remaining workplaces.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Rajan's factory floor accident**
Rajan, a machine operator in Shah Alam, had two fingers amputated after a machine guard was removed for "faster production." The accident was not reported to JKKP by the employer. Under OSHA 1994, employers must notify JKKP of any accident involving serious injury within 7 days using Form JKKP 8. Rajan's family filed a complaint via SISPAA. JKKP investigated and the employer was fined for both the safety violation and failure to report.

**Scenario 2: Office worker with repetitive strain injury**
Lina, a data entry clerk in a KL shared office space, developed severe wrist pain after years of working without proper ergonomic support. Post-June 2024, her employer (a services company previously exempt from OSHA) is now legally required to assess and address ergonomic risks. Lina reported the hazard to her safety officer. When no action was taken, she filed a complaint with JKKP.

**Scenario 3: Construction site without proper safety equipment**
A foreign worker at a construction site in Johor was working at height without a safety harness. A site visitor reported the unsafe condition to JKKP via the SISPAA online complaint system. JKKP officers conducted an unannounced inspection within days. The contractor received a stop-work order until compliance was achieved.

**Scenario 4: Chemical exposure at a small factory**
Workers at a rubber glove factory were regularly exposed to chemical fumes with no ventilation or respiratory protection. Several developed respiratory conditions. A union representative filed a complaint with JKKP citing violations of the Occupational Safety and Health (Use and Standards of Exposure of Chemicals Hazardous to Health) Regulations 2000 (USECHH). JKKP conducted a Chemical Health Risk Assessment (CHRA) audit on the premises.

---

**Path A: My workplace has a safety hazard my employer is ignoring**

Step 1 of 4: Report the hazard internally first.
- Tell your supervisor or Safety and Health Officer (SHO) in writing.
- If your company has a Safety and Health Committee, raise it there.
- Keep a copy of all written communications including WhatsApp messages.

Step 2 of 4: If no action is taken, file a complaint with JKKP.
- Online: SISPAA complaint system at dosh.gov.my.
- Walk-in: nearest JKKP state office with your IC and documentation.
- You may file anonymously if you fear retaliation, though named complaints are processed faster.

Step 3 of 4: JKKP inspection process.
- JKKP officers will conduct a workplace inspection, announced or unannounced.
- You may be asked to provide a statement. This is confidential.
- Employers found in violation may receive an improvement notice, prohibition notice, or face prosecution.

Step 4 of 4: Follow up on your complaint.
- You can check your complaint status via the SISPAA portal.
- If JKKP determines no violation, you will be notified with reasons.

---

**Path B: I was injured at work**

Step 1 of 3: Seek medical treatment immediately.
- Government hospital or Klinik Kesihatan for emergency treatment.
- Inform the doctor that it is a workplace injury. This is important for SOCSO claims.
- Keep all medical reports, MC, and receipts.

Step 2 of 3: Ensure the accident is reported to JKKP.
- Your employer is legally required to report serious workplace accidents to JKKP.
- If they do not, you or your representative can report via SISPAA.
- Reportable accidents: death, serious injury, dangerous occurrence, or occupational disease.

Step 3 of 3: File for SOCSO benefits.
- Work-related injuries and diseases are covered under SOCSO's Employment Injury Scheme.
- See Page 2.5 for full SOCSO claims guidance.
- You do not need to choose between filing with JKKP and claiming SOCSO. Do both.

---

**Path C: I was exposed to chemicals or harmful substances**

Step 1 of 3: Seek medical attention and inform the doctor of the specific substance or exposure.
- Occupational diseases can take time to develop. Early documentation is critical.
- Report an occupational disease via e-JKKP7 at dosh.gov.my.

Step 2 of 3: Check whether your employer conducted a Chemical Health Risk Assessment (CHRA).
- Employers handling hazardous chemicals must conduct a CHRA under USECHH regulations.
- If no CHRA was done and you were exposed to hazardous chemicals, this is a violation.

Step 3 of 3: File a complaint with JKKP via SISPAA.
- Include the chemical name or product name if known.
- Include dates and duration of exposure.
- Include any medical reports linking your condition to the workplace.

---

**Path D: I am an employer who needs to understand my OSH obligations**

Step 1 of 3: Assess whether you need a written OSH Policy.
- Required if you employ 5 or more workers.
- The policy must be displayed prominently at the workplace.

Step 2 of 3: Determine if you need a Safety and Health Officer (SHO).
- Compulsory for certain industries and workplaces above a set number of employees.
- Check the OSHA (Safety and Health Officer) Regulations 1997 for thresholds.

Step 3 of 3: Register with JKKP where required.
- Certain industries must notify JKKP of their industrial activities using Form JKKP 5.
- Machinery above a set threshold must be registered and inspected by JKKP.
- Check MyKKP at dosh.gov.my for your specific registration requirements.

## Penalties for OSHA Violations

| Violation | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|
| General OSH duty violation | RM50,000 fine or 2 years imprisonment or both |
| Failure to comply with improvement or prohibition notice | RM50,000 fine or 2 years imprisonment or both |
| Repeat offence | RM500,000 fine or 5 years imprisonment or both |
| Death resulting from negligence | Significantly higher penalties under amended Act |

## FAQ

**Q: What is the difference between JKKP and JTK?**
JTK handles employment terms, wages, leave entitlements, and wrongful dismissal under the Employment Act 1955. JKKP handles physical workplace safety and health conditions under OSHA 1994. If you were injured at work AND your employer has not paid your medical bills, you file with both JKKP (safety violation) and JTK (wage/benefit violation) separately.

**Q: Does OSHA cover my office job?**
Yes. From June 1, 2024, the OSH Amendment Act expanded coverage to all places of work throughout Malaysia with limited exceptions. Offices, restaurants, retail shops, and service businesses are now all covered under OSHA 1994.

**Q: Can my employer punish me for reporting a safety issue to JKKP?**
Retaliatory action against an employee for making a genuine OSH complaint is prohibited under OSHA 1994. If this happens, document everything and file a separate complaint with both JKKP and JTK simultaneously.

**Q: Who is required to have a Safety and Health Committee?**
Workplaces with 40 or more employees are required to establish a Safety and Health Committee under the OSH (Safety and Health Committee) Regulations 1996. The committee must meet at least once every 3 months and maintain minutes of all meetings.

**Q: What happens after I file a complaint with JKKP?**
JKKP will assess your complaint and determine whether an inspection is warranted. If a violation is found, the employer may receive an improvement notice (with a deadline to fix the issue) or a prohibition notice (immediate stop-work order). Serious violations proceed to prosecution. You will be informed of the outcome through the SISPAA portal.
