## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is PERKESO?**

PERKESO (SOCSO in English) is insurance that protects you if something bad happens while you are working. For example:

* If you get injured at work
* If you get sick because of your job
* If you become unable to work permanently
* If you die, your family may receive support

Every month, your employer pays a small amount for this protection. Sometimes you also contribute a small amount.

**Why is it important?**

If you are injured and cannot work, PERKESO may:

* Pay your medical bills
* Replace part of your salary
* Help you return to work
* Support your family if you pass away

**How do you know if you are covered?**

You can:

* Check your payslip
* Ask your HR department
* Check with PERKESO
* Register on the ASSIST Portal

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

The Social Security Organisation (PERKESO/SOCSO) is Malaysia’s statutory social protection agency under the Ministry of Human Resources. It provides protection to employees against contingencies such as workplace injuries, occupational diseases, invalidity, and death.

PERKESO operates mainly through two major protection schemes:

| Scheme                   | Malay Name             | Purpose                                                        |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Employment Injury Scheme | Skim Bencana Pekerjaan | Protection for work-related accidents and diseases             |
| Invalidity Scheme        | Skim Keilatan          | Protection for permanent disability or death unrelated to work |

A third scheme, the **Employment Insurance System (EIS)**, provides unemployment protection (covered separately in Page 2.6).

---

## Contribution Structure

PERKESO contributions depend on salary and age.

**For employees below age 60:**

| Contribution Type        | Paid By               |
| ------------------------ | --------------------- |
| Employment Injury Scheme | Employer only         |
| Invalidity Scheme        | Employer and employee |

**General structure:**

| Contributor   | Contribution responsibility                           |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Employer      | Mandatory contribution                                |
| Employee      | Contribution only for Invalidity Scheme               |
| Self-employed | Optional under Self-Employment Social Security Scheme |

Employees earning above RM6,000 can still be covered if both employer and employee agree to continue contributions.

---

## What Does PERKESO Cover?

### Employment Injury Scheme covers:

* Workplace accidents
* Accidents while commuting to or from work
* Accidents during work-related travel
* Occupational diseases (for example chemical exposure)

Benefits may include:

* Free medical treatment at panel clinics or government hospitals
* Temporary disability payments (salary replacement)
* Permanent disability compensation
* Dependent’s benefits if death occurs
* Rehabilitation services

### Invalidity Scheme covers:

* Permanent disability preventing work
* Death not related to work
* Survivor pensions for dependents

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Workplace injury**

Faizal injured his hand while operating machinery. His employer reported the accident to PERKESO. His hospital treatment was covered and he received temporary disability payments while recovering.

**Scenario 2: Commuting accident**

Siti was involved in a motorcycle accident while travelling to work. Because commuting accidents are covered, she received medical treatment and wage replacement during her recovery.

**Scenario 3: Employer did not register employee**

Jason worked for 8 months before discovering he was never registered with PERKESO. After reporting this, PERKESO investigated and required the employer to register him and pay outstanding contributions.

**Scenario 4: Permanent disability**

Rahman suffered a stroke at age 45 and could no longer work. Under the Invalidity Scheme, he qualified for monthly invalidity pension payments.

### Path A: I want to check if I am registered

**Step 1 of 2: Check your payslip**

* Look for SOCSO or PERKESO deductions.
* Confirm employer contributions are listed.

**Step 2 of 2: Check directly with PERKESO**

* Register on the ASSIST Portal.
* Contact PERKESO.
* Visit the nearest PERKESO office.

---

### Path B: I was injured at work

**Step 1 of 3: Inform your employer immediately**

* Report the accident as soon as possible.
* Employer must submit an accident report.

**Step 2 of 3: Seek treatment**

* Go to a PERKESO panel clinic or government hospital.
* Inform them it is a workplace injury.

**Step 3 of 3: Ensure claim submission**

* Employer submits claim forms.
* Follow up with PERKESO if needed.

---

### Path C: I want to make a claim

**Step 1 of 2: Prepare documents**

Typical documents include:

* IC
* Medical reports
* Employer accident report
* Payslips
* Bank details

**Step 2 of 2: Submit claim**

Claims can be submitted via:

* Employer HR department
* PERKESO office
* Online portal (where applicable)

Processing time depends on claim complexity.

---

### Path D: Employer not contributing

**Step 1 of 2: Gather evidence**

Prepare:

* Payslips
* Employment contract
* Employer details
* Employment dates

**Step 2 of 2: Report to PERKESO**

You can:

* File a complaint directly
* Visit PERKESO office
* Contact hotline

Failure to register employees is an offence under the Employees’ Social Security Act 1969.

---

### Path E: Self-employed coverage

Self-employed individuals (for example Grab drivers, delivery riders, freelancers) can register under the Self-Employment Social Security Scheme.

**Step 1 of 2: Register**

* Apply via PERKESO website.
* Select coverage plan.

**Step 2 of 2: Pay contribution**

* Annual contribution based on selected income level.
* Enables accident protection during work activities.

## FAQ

**Q: Is PERKESO the same as EPF?**
No. EPF is retirement savings. PERKESO is insurance protection against accidents, disability, and death.

**Q: Can I claim PERKESO if I resign?**
Yes, if the incident happened while you were covered and eligible under the scheme.

**Q: What if my accident happened outside the workplace?**
If it happened while commuting or during work duties, it may still qualify.

**Q: Can I check my PERKESO contributions?**
Yes. You can check through the ASSIST Portal or by contacting PERKESO.

**Q: What happens if my employer never registered me?**
The employer can be investigated and required to pay outstanding contributions and penalties.
