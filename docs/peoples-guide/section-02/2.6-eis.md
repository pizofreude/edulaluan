## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is EIS?**

EIS (Employment Insurance System) helps you if you lose your job. If you are retrenched or your company closes, EIS may:

* Give you temporary monthly money
* Help you find a new job
* Provide training to learn new skills

You and your employer both pay a small monthly contribution while you are working. If you lose your job later, you may receive help.

**Why is it important?**

Losing a job can happen suddenly. EIS helps you survive financially while you look for your next job.

**When can you claim?**

Usually when:

* You are retrenched
* Your company closes
* Your contract ends early
* You are forced to resign due to serious workplace issues

You normally cannot claim if you resign voluntarily.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

The Employment Insurance System (EIS) is Malaysia’s unemployment protection scheme administered by PERKESO. It provides temporary financial assistance and re-employment support to workers who lose their jobs.

The objectives of EIS are:

* Provide temporary income replacement
* Accelerate re-employment
* Improve workforce employability
* Reduce financial hardship after job loss

EIS combines **financial assistance** with **active labour market programmes** such as training and job matching.

---

## Contribution Structure

EIS contributions are shared equally between employer and employee.

**Contribution summary:**

| Contributor | Rate                   |
| ----------- | ---------------------- |
| Employer    | 0.2% of monthly salary |
| Employee    | 0.2% of monthly salary |

Total contribution: **0.4% of monthly wages**

Applies to:

* Malaysian citizens
* Permanent residents
* Employees aged 18 to 60

Does not normally apply to:

* Government employees
* Domestic workers
* Self-employed individuals (unless voluntarily registered under other schemes)

---

## What Benefits Does EIS Provide?

EIS provides several types of benefits:

| Benefit                       | Purpose                                    |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Job Search Allowance (JSA)    | Monthly financial support                  |
| Early Re-employment Allowance | Incentive if you find work quickly         |
| Reduced Income Allowance      | Support if forced to accept lower salary   |
| Training Allowance            | Financial support during approved training |
| Training Fees                 | Covers cost of approved programmes         |
| Career counselling            | Job placement assistance                   |

---

## Job Search Allowance (JSA)

This is the main EIS benefit.

Eligible claimants may receive monthly payments for several months while seeking employment.

General structure:

| Month   | % of assumed monthly salary |
| ------- | --------------------------- |
| Month 1 | 80%                         |
| Month 2 | 50%                         |
| Month 3 | 40%                         |
| Month 4 | 30%                         |
| Month 5 | 30%                         |
| Month 6 | 30%                         |

Actual payments depend on contribution history and eligibility conditions.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Retrenchment**

Nadia was retrenched when her company downsized. She applied for EIS within weeks and received Job Search Allowance payments while attending job interviews arranged through PERKESO.

**Scenario 2: Company closure**

A small logistics company shut down operations. Employees who were registered with EIS received temporary income support while transitioning to new jobs.

**Scenario 3: Faster re-employment**

Daniel found a new job after receiving two months of EIS support. Because he found work early, he qualified for the Early Re-employment Allowance incentive.

**Scenario 4: Skills upgrading**

Mei Ling lost her marketing job and used EIS training benefits to complete a digital marketing certification programme before securing a new role.

### Path A: I just lost my job

**Step 1 of 3: Confirm eligibility**

You may qualify if:

* You were retrenched
* Your contract ended unexpectedly
* Company closed
* Voluntary separation scheme (VSS/MSS)

Usually not eligible if:

* You resigned voluntarily
* You were dismissed for misconduct
* Your contract ended normally

**Step 2 of 3: Apply quickly**

Applications should normally be made within **60 days** of job loss.

**Step 3 of 3: Register as job seeker**

You may need to:

* Attend PERKESO interviews
* Accept suitable job offers
* Participate in programmes

---

### Path B: I want to apply for EIS

**Step 1 of 2: Prepare documents**

Prepare:

* IC
* Termination letter
* Bank details
* Payslips
* Employment contract

**Step 2 of 2: Submit application**

Apply via:

* PERKESO office
* EIS portal
* PERKESO officers

Approval depends on eligibility review.

---

### Path C: I want to know if I qualify

Eligibility generally depends on:

* Contribution history
* Reason for job loss
* Age requirements
* Active job search participation

PERKESO makes the final determination.

---

### Path D: I want job search help

PERKESO provides:

* Career counselling sessions
* Resume improvement advice
* Interview preparation
* Job matching through MYFutureJobs portal

Participation may be required to continue receiving benefits.

---

### Path E: I want training support

Eligible claimants may receive:

* Training fee coverage
* Monthly training allowance
* Certification programmes
* Skills upgrading opportunities

Training must usually be approved by PERKESO.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I claim EIS if I resign?**
Usually no. EIS is meant for involuntary job loss. Exceptions may apply in constructive dismissal cases.

**Q: How fast must I apply?**
Normally within 60 days of losing your job. Apply as soon as possible.

**Q: Do I still receive EIS if I find a job quickly?**
You may qualify for Early Re-employment Allowance instead.

**Q: Can contract workers claim EIS?**
Yes, if they contributed and the contract ended unexpectedly or early.

**Q: Do I need to attend PERKESO programmes?**
Yes. Participation may be required to continue receiving benefits.
