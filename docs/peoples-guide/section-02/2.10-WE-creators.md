## About This Page

This page lists Malaysian content creators, official agency channels, and media resources that help the public understand employment rights, career development, workplace protection, and job market trends. This is a living page. If you know a creator or channel that belongs here, use the **"Suggest a resource"** button at the bottom.

**Note:** EduLaluan does not endorse any individual creator's views beyond the specific educational content listed. Always verify employment law information against official government agencies.

## Official Government Channels

These are the primary authoritative sources for employment law and worker protection information in Malaysia.

| Agency                               | Platform | Handle or Link               | What They Post                                   |
| ------------------------------------ | -------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Ministry of Human Resources (KESUMA) | Website | www.mohr.gov.my | Labour policies, worker initiatives              |
| Labour Department (JTKSM)            | Website | jtksm.mohr.gov.my       | Employment rights education, enforcement updates |
| PERKESO                              | Website | www.perkeso.gov.my | Worker protection programmes, EIS updates        |
| PERKESO                              | TikTok   | @perkesoofficial             | Worker benefits education, employment programmes |
| MYFutureJobs                         | Website  | myfuturejobs.gov.my          | Job matching, career resources                   |

## Content Gap Notice

As of March 2026, there are relatively few Malaysian independent creators producing consistent educational content specifically on:

* Employment law education in simple language
* How to file labour complaints step-by-step
* Understanding employment contracts
* Workplace dispute case studies
* HR best practices explained for employees (not employers)

**If you create or know of a channel covering these topics, please suggest it below.** EduLaluan will review and add verified resources.

This gap represents an opportunity for educators, HR professionals, lawyers, and worker advocates to create public education content.
