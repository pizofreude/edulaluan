## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**Why should you ask HR questions?**

Because once you accept a job, it becomes harder to change the terms.

Before saying yes, you should understand:

* How much you will be paid
* How many hours you will work
* What benefits you get
* What happens if you leave

**Example good questions:**

* What is my exact salary?
* Is overtime paid?
* How many leave days do I get?
* When is salary paid?

Good employers will answer clearly.

---

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

A job offer is a legal agreement. Many employment disputes happen because employees did not clarify details before accepting.

Key areas you should always clarify:

* Compensation structure
* Working hours and expectations
* Benefits and protection
* Performance expectations
* Exit terms

Asking questions is professional and expected. It shows diligence, not distrust.

---

## Salary and Compensation Questions

Always confirm the **real total compensation**, not just the headline salary.

Important questions:

| Question                                | Why it matters                    |
| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| What is the basic salary?               | Determines EPF and benefits       |
| Are allowances fixed or conditional?    | Some allowances can be removed    |
| Is there a probation salary difference? | Some companies pay less initially |
| How often are salary reviews done?      | Indicates growth prospects        |
| Is bonus guaranteed or discretionary?   | Many bonuses are not guaranteed   |

Example clarification:

Instead of asking:
"What is the salary?"

Ask:
"Can you confirm the breakdown between basic salary, allowances, and variable pay?"

---

## Working Hours and Expectations

Many job dissatisfaction issues come from unclear expectations.

Important questions:

| Question                                    | Why it matters                   |
| ------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| What are official working hours?            | Prevents hidden overtime culture |
| Is overtime expected?                       | Identifies workload culture      |
| Is overtime paid or replaced with time off? | Financial impact                 |
| Is weekend work required?                   | Work-life balance                |
| Is remote or hybrid work allowed?           | Flexibility expectations         |

---

## Benefits Questions

Beyond salary, benefits significantly affect total compensation.

Ask about:

| Question                                 | Why it matters              |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Is medical insurance provided?           | Healthcare protection       |
| What is the annual leave policy?         | Work-life balance           |
| Are there training opportunities?        | Career growth               |
| Is there EPF above minimum rate?         | Long-term financial benefit |
| Are there PERKESO and EIS contributions? | Legal compliance            |

---

## Contract Terms

Never accept a job without understanding the contract terms.

Important questions:

| Question                                   | Why it matters             |
| ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| What is the probation period?              | Job security               |
| What confirms probation?                   | Avoid silent extensions    |
| What is the notice period?                 | Exit planning              |
| Are there non-compete clauses?             | Future career restrictions |
| Are there penalties for early resignation? | Financial risk             |

---

## Performance Expectations

Clarify how success is measured.

Ask:

| Question                        | Why it matters     |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| How is performance evaluated?   | Promotion clarity  |
| What are KPIs?                  | Clear expectations |
| How often are reviews done?     | Feedback culture   |
| What defines probation success? | Job security       |

---

## Exit and Risk Questions

Many employees only think about entry, not exit.

Important questions:

| Question                                  | Why it matters       |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| What is the termination process?          | Legal protection     |
| What happens if the company restructures? | Retrenchment risk    |
| Is there severance policy?                | Financial protection |
| What happens to unused leave?             | Compensation clarity |

---

## Red Flags To Watch For

Be cautious if:

* Salary details are vague
* Contract not provided before start
* Employer avoids written terms
* Overtime expectations unclear
* Benefits verbally promised but undocumented
* Pressure to accept immediately

Transparency usually indicates a healthier workplace.

---

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Hidden overtime culture**

A graduate accepted a job without asking about working hours. She later discovered daily unpaid overtime expectations.

**Scenario 2: Allowance misunderstanding**

An employee thought RM800 allowance was permanent. It was performance-based and removed later.

**Scenario 3: Notice period surprise**

A worker discovered a 3-month notice period only after wanting to resign.

**Scenario 4: No written offer**

An employee joined based on verbal promises that were never formalised.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it okay to ask many questions before accepting?**
Yes. Professional employers expect this.

**Q: Can I negotiate salary after receiving an offer?**
Yes, before acceptance.

**Q: Should I accept immediately if pressured?**
No. You may request reasonable time to review.

**Q: Should everything be in writing?**
Yes. Verbal promises are difficult to enforce.

**Q: Can I reject after accepting verbally?**
Yes, but professionally and quickly.
