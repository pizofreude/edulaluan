## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is EPF?**
EPF stands for Employees Provident Fund. In Malay it is called KWSP, Kumpulan Wang Simpanan Pekerja. Every month, a small part of your salary is saved automatically into your EPF account. Your employer also adds their own contribution on top of yours. This money is kept safely until you retire.

**Why is it important?**
When you stop working at retirement age, EPF is the money you live on. The earlier you start working and saving, the more you will have when you retire.

**What are the three accounts?**
- **Akaun Persaraan:** Your main retirement savings. Touch this last.
- **Akaun Sejahtera:** Can be used for housing, education, and healthcare before retirement.
- **Akaun Fleksibel:** Can be withdrawn at any time for any reason. This is your buffer account.

**How do you check your EPF savings?**
- Download the **myEPF app** on your phone.
- Log in to i-Akaun at kwsp.gov.my.
- Visit any EPF office with your IC.

### 🧑 Detailed

**Overview**

EPF (Employees Provident Fund) or KWSP (Kumpulan Wang Simpanan Pekerja) is Malaysia's mandatory retirement savings scheme. It is a social security institution that helps employees save for retirement through monthly contributions from both employee and employer. Savings earn annual tax-free dividends and are withdrawable at age 55.

EPF is one of the world's largest provident funds, managing over RM1 trillion in savings for more than 15 million members.

**The Three-Account Structure (effective May 11, 2024):**

The EPF restructured member accounts so that each member account now comprises three accounts. Akaun Persaraan (previously Account 1) is for retirement savings. Akaun Sejahtera (previously Account 2) is for various lifecycle needs. Akaun Fleksibel is the new third account for short-term financial needs. The main goals of the restructuring are to help members better balance their short and long-term financial needs and respond better to labour market changes.

| Account | Malay Name | Share of Contributions | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retirement Account | Akaun Persaraan | 75% | Retirement. Full withdrawal at 55. |
| Wellbeing Account | Akaun Sejahtera | 15% | Housing, education, healthcare, investments |
| Flexible Account | Akaun Fleksibel | 10% | Flexible withdrawal anytime, minimum RM50 |

**Post-55 accounts:**
Akaun 55 is a combination of Akaun Persaraan, Akaun Sejahtera, and Akaun Fleksibel that can be withdrawn once a member reaches 55 years old. Akaun Emas is an account that exists after a member reaches 55 years old. Any contribution after age 55 is credited into this account and can only be withdrawn once the member reaches 60 years old.

**Contribution Rates (Malaysian citizens below 55):**

| Contributor | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employee | 11% of monthly salary |
| Employer (salary above RM5,000) | 12% of monthly salary |
| Employer (salary RM5,000 and below) | 13% of monthly salary |

**Contribution Rates (non-citizen employees, from October 2025):**
Starting October 2025, both employers and non-citizen employees under age 75 must each contribute a mandatory 2% of monthly wages.

**Voluntary contributions:**
Self-employed individuals, freelancers, and those who want to top up their savings can contribute voluntarily via i-Saraan (for the self-employed) or i-Saraan Plus. Voluntary contributions also qualify for personal income tax relief.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Amirul's first job and EPF registration**
Amirul started his first job at 23. He noticed his payslip showed an 11% EPF deduction but had never registered for i-Akaun. He did not know how much was in his account or whether his employer was contributing correctly. He registered for i-Akaun online in 10 minutes. He discovered his employer had not been contributing for the first two months and immediately filed a complaint with EPF.

**Scenario 2: Puan Nora withdrawing from Akaun Sejahtera for housing**
Puan Nora was buying her first home and wanted to reduce her home loan principal using her EPF savings. She applied for a housing withdrawal from Akaun Sejahtera via i-Akaun online. The process took under two weeks. She also used a portion for monthly housing loan repayment reduction, which is an ongoing approved withdrawal purpose.

**Scenario 3: Self-employed photographer using i-Saraan**
Rizwan is a freelance photographer with no fixed employer. He has no EPF contributions because nobody deducts it for him. He enrolled in i-Saraan voluntarily, contributing RM200 per month. Beyond building retirement savings, he receives an annual government matching incentive of up to RM300 and can claim his contributions as tax relief.

**Scenario 4: Employer paying EPF late**
A small business owner in Penang paid EPF contributions on the 20th of each month due to cash flow issues. Employer and employee contributions must be paid to EPF no later than the 15th of the following month. Late payments attract penalty charges and can be reported by employees directly to any EPF office.

**Q: What do you need help with?**

---

**Path A: I am a new employee and want to register with EPF**

Step 1 of 3: Your employer should register you automatically within 7 days of employment.
- Confirm your EPF number appears on your payslip.
- If your employer has not registered you after one month, contact EPF directly.

Step 2 of 3: Register for i-Akaun (Member) to manage your account online.
- Go to kwsp.gov.my or download the myEPF app.
- You will need your IC number and a registered mobile number.
- First-time registration requires a one-time visit to an EPF office or via MyDigital ID verification.

Step 3 of 3: Set up a beneficiary (nomination).
- Log in to i-Akaun and go to the nomination section.
- Nominate who receives your EPF savings if you pass away before withdrawal.
- Without a nomination, funds go to your estate and may be delayed by probate proceedings.

---

**Path B: I want to check if my employer is contributing correctly**

Step 1 of 3: Log in to i-Akaun and check your contribution history monthly.
- Your employer's contribution and your own deduction should both appear.
- Contributions for the current month's salary should appear by the 15th of the following month.

Step 2 of 3: Cross-check with your payslip.
- Your payslip must show the EPF deduction amount.
- Compare the amount deducted from your salary with the amount credited in i-Akaun.

Step 3 of 3: If contributions are missing or incorrect, file a complaint with EPF.
- Bring your personal information including IC number and EPF membership number, your employer's name, contact number and address, and the contract of service between you and your employer if available.
- Walk in to any EPF office or submit via the EPF enquiry portal at kwsp.gov.my.

---

**Path C: I want to make a withdrawal before retirement**

Step 1 of 2: Determine which account and purpose applies to you.

Withdrawals from **Akaun Fleksibel:**
- Anytime, any purpose, minimum RM50 per withdrawal.
- Via i-Akaun online or myEPF app.

Withdrawals from **Akaun Sejahtera:**

| Purpose | Condition |
|---|---|
| Housing purchase or loan repayment | First or second home |
| Education | For self or children at approved institutions |
| Healthcare | Serious illness, approved medical expenses |
| Unit trust investment | Via EPF Members Investment Scheme (MIS) |
| Housing withdrawal at age 50 | Up to 30% of Akaun Persaraan savings |

Withdrawals from **Akaun Persaraan:**
- Full withdrawal before 55 is only permitted for permanent disability, critical illness, death, or permanent departure from Malaysia.
- Partial housing withdrawal allowed from age 50.

Step 2 of 2: Apply via i-Akaun or visit the nearest EPF office.
- Most withdrawal applications are processed within 3 to 7 working days.
- Ensure your bank account is registered with EPF for direct crediting.

---

**Path D: I am self-employed and want to contribute voluntarily**

Step 1 of 2: Register for i-Saraan via kwsp.gov.my or any EPF office.
- Open to all Malaysians without mandatory EPF contributions including freelancers, hawkers, Grab drivers, and small traders.
- No minimum monthly contribution amount.
- Government matching incentive: up to RM300 per year for contributions of RM1,200 and above (subject to annual budget).

Step 2 of 2: Claim your voluntary contribution as tax relief.
- EPF contributions (mandatory and voluntary) qualify for individual income tax relief of up to RM4,000 per year under LHDN.
- See Page 3.3 for full tax relief details.

---

**Path E: I want to understand my EPF at retirement**

Step 1 of 2: Know your retirement benchmarks.
- The EPF recommends three savings levels at retirement: Basic Savings Level at RM390,000 which supports monthly withdrawals of RM1,625 in year one. Adequate Savings Level at RM650,000 which enables monthly withdrawals starting at RM2,708 in year one. Enhanced Savings Level at RM1.3 million.
- As of 2024, only 36% of active formal EPF members meet the Basic Savings Level. Plan early.

Step 2 of 2: Understand your withdrawal options at 55 and beyond.
- At 55, your three accounts merge into Akaun 55 for full or partial withdrawal.
- You can choose to withdraw all, withdraw partially, or leave savings to continue earning dividends.
- At 60, Akaun Emas contributions (post-55 contributions) become accessible.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens to my EPF if I change jobs?**
Your EPF account stays with you regardless of your employer. Your EPF number and all savings remain in the same account. Your new employer will contribute to the same account using your EPF membership number. You do not need to open a new account.

**Q: Can my employer deduct EPF from my salary without contributing their share?**
No. The employer is legally required to contribute their share in addition to deducting yours. Deducting your portion but not contributing it to EPF is a criminal offence under the EPF Act 1991. Report this to EPF immediately with your payslips as evidence.

**Q: What is the annual EPF dividend and how is it calculated?**
EPF declares an annual dividend based on its investment returns. Legally, EPF is only obligated to provide 2.5% dividends as per Section 27 of the Employees Provident Fund Act 1991. In practice, EPF has consistently declared dividends well above this minimum. Dividends are credited directly to your account and are tax-free.

**Q: Can I contribute more than 11% to EPF?**
Yes. You can elect to contribute at a higher rate by submitting Form KWSP 17A (AHL) to your employer. The additional amount above 11% does not attract the employer's matching contribution but qualifies for additional tax relief.

**Q: What if I am retrenched before retirement age?**
You are not required to withdraw your EPF upon retrenchment. Your savings continue to earn dividends. You may withdraw from Akaun Fleksibel for immediate needs. Akaun Persaraan and Akaun Sejahtera are best left untouched until the approved withdrawal purposes are met. Also refer to Page 2.6 for EIS (Employment Insurance System) benefits upon retrenchment.
