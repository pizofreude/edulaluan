## Audience Tabs

### 🧒 Simple

**What is a payslip?**

A payslip is a document that shows:

* How much money you earned
* What was deducted
* How much money you actually receive

**Example:**

If your salary is RM3,000:

* EPF deduction: RM330
* PERKESO deduction: small amount
* EIS deduction: small amount

Your final take-home salary may be around RM2,600+.

**Why should you check your payslip?**

To make sure:

* Your employer paid you correctly
* Contributions were deducted correctly
* You are registered with EPF and PERKESO
* There are no mistakes

Always keep your payslips.

### 🧑 Detailed

## Overview

A payslip (salary slip) is a mandatory employment document that shows salary components, statutory deductions, and net pay. Under the Employment Act 1955 (amendments effective 2023), employers must provide payslips to employees.

A proper Malaysian payslip typically contains:

* Employer details
* Employee details
* Salary breakdown
* Allowances
* Overtime (if applicable)
* Statutory deductions
* Net salary

Payslips are critical financial records and may be required for:

* Loan applications
* Rental applications
* Visa applications
* Tax filing
* Labour disputes

---

## Basic Payslip Structure

Typical structure:

| Section          | What it shows            |
| ---------------- | ------------------------ |
| Employee details | Name, IC, employee ID    |
| Employer details | Company name             |
| Salary period    | Month paid               |
| Gross pay        | Salary before deductions |
| Allowances       | Extra payments           |
| Deductions       | Required contributions   |
| Net pay          | Final take-home salary   |

## Common Deductions

Typical deductions include:

| Deduction        | Purpose                           |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------- |
| EPF              | Retirement savings                |
| PERKESO          | Injury and disability insurance   |
| EIS              | Unemployment protection           |
| PCB (MTD)        | Monthly income tax deduction      |
| Zakat            | Optional for Muslim employees     |
| Other deductions | Loans, advances, company benefits |

## Red Flags To Watch For

You should investigate if:

* EPF deducted but not contributed
* PERKESO missing
* EIS missing
* Salary different from contract
* Overtime unpaid
* Unknown deductions appear
* Payslip not provided

If deductions appear but contributions are not made, this may be an offence.

## Why This Matters

**Scenario 1: Missing EPF contributions**

Hafiz noticed EPF deductions on his payslip but they were missing from his EPF account. He reported this and the employer was investigated.

**Scenario 2: Wrong salary calculation**

An employee discovered her allowance was excluded from overtime calculations. After raising it with HR, it was corrected.

**Scenario 3: No PERKESO registration**

A worker discovered no PERKESO deduction after 6 months. This revealed the employer never registered him.

**Scenario 4: PCB confusion**

A fresh graduate thought tax deductions were errors. HR explained PCB is a monthly income tax prepayment.

## FAQ

**Q: Is a payslip required by law?**
Yes. Employers must provide payslips under Malaysian labour regulations.

**Q: Can salary be paid without a payslip?**
Payment may occur, but payslips must still be provided.

**Q: Why is my net pay lower than expected?**
Because of statutory deductions like EPF, PERKESO, EIS, and PCB.

**Q: Should allowances be EPF deductible?**
Some allowances are EPF-contributable depending on classification.

**Q: How long should I keep payslips?**
At least 7 years for tax and legal purposes is good practice.
